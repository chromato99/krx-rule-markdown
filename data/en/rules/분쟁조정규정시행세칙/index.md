CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS 

§1. Purpose   

The  purpose  of  this  Enforcement  Rules  is  to  stipulate  the  matters  necessary  for  the 
enforcement  of  the  Dispute  Mediation  Regulation  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the 
Regulation”). 

§2. Definitions 

The terms used in this Enforcement Rules shall have the same meanings given to them 
in the Regulation. 

CHAPTER II. REQUEST FOR DISPUTE MEDIATION, ETC. 

§3. Preparation of the Application for Mediation, etc. 

(1)  The  application  for  dispute  mediation  and  the  record  of  dispute  mediation  request 
pursuant to [§4(6)] of the Regulation shall be in accordance with the Form 1 and Form 2 
of Annex, respectively, and in case where there is an agent, the letter of attorney shall be 
in accordance with Form 3 of Annex. (Amended on November 30, 2006) 

(2)  The  request  for  dispute  mediation  and  the  confirmation  of  record  of  dispute 
mediation  request  pursuant  to  [§4(6)]  of  the  Regulation  shall  be  made  through  such 
method  as  the  postal  service,  personal  delivery,  facsimile,  E-mail  and  other  similar 
electronic communication methods. 

§4. Notification of the Receipt of Request for Mediation   

Upon  receiving  the  request  for  mediation  pursuant  to  [§4(6)]  of  the  Regulation,  the 
Chairman  of  the  Market  Oversight  Commission  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the 
Chairman”) shall immediately notify the petitioner of the fact about the receipt and the 
position title, name, telephone numbers, etc. of the employee of the Exchange who is in 
charge  of  the  case  through  such  means  as  the  postal  service,  telephone,  facsimile,  E-
mail and other similar electronic communication methods.

§5. Supplementation of Request for Mediation 

(1) “The period stipulated in the Enforcement Rules” in [§6] of the Regulation shall be 
seven (7) days from the day when the petitioner has received the notification asking to 
supplement the request submitted. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(2)  In  case  where  the  petitioner  does  not  supplement  the  request  within  the  period 
specified in Para.(1), the Chairman may, once again, request to supplement the request 
by setting a period shorter than seven (7) days. (Amended on November 30, 2006) 

§6. Submission Period of Opinion Statements, etc. 

(1) “The period stipulated in the Enforcement Rules” in [§7(2)] of the Regulation shall 
be fourteen (14) days (excluding the public holiday pursuant to the Rules on Holidays 
of  Public  Agencies  or  Saturday).  However,  in  case  where  the  respondent  requests  an 
extension for the justifiable reasons, the Chairman may extend the period. (Amended on 
November 30, 2006; February 3, 2009) 

(2)  The  details  of  request  for  mediation  to  be  notified  pursuant  to  [§7(4)]  of  the 
Regulation shall be such facts as the receipt of mediation request, the intention of and 
reasons for request and the submission period of opinion statement. 

(3) The details of opinion statement to be notified pursuant to [§7(4)] of the Regulation 
shall be the objectives and details of the opinion statement submitted by the respondent. 

(4)  The  provisions  of  [§4]  shall  be  applied  mutatis  mutandis  to  the  method  of 
notification pursuant to Paras. (2) and (3). 

§7. Investigation of Facts 

(1)  In  cases  where  pursuant  to  [§8(4)]  of  the  Regulation,  the  Chairman  conducts  an 
investigation by requesting the concerned parties and other implicated persons to appear 
and  inquiring  about  the  facts  and  background  or  asks  the  concerned  parties  and  other 
implicated persons to confirm the facts or submit background information, the request 
shall be made in writing, stating the reasons for such requests, date to appear, types of 
information required, the due date for submission of information, etc. However, in case 
where  it  is  deemed  necessary  for  the  speedy  confirmation  of  facts  and  compilation  of 
information,  the  employee  of  the  Exchange  who  is  conducting  the  investigation  may 
visit the concerned parties and other implicated persons and make the request personally. 
(Amended on November 30, 2006) 

(2)  In  case  where  the  investigation  is  conducted  by  such  methods  as  the  visit  to  the 
concerned parties and other implicated persons and site visit pursuant to [§8(4)] of the 
Regulation,  the  employee  of  the  Exchange  who  is  conducting  the  investigation  shall

persons. (Amended on November 30, 2006) 

(3) In case where the confirmation of facts is necessary, the employee of the Exchange 
who  is  conducting  the  investigation  pursuant  to  [§8(4)]  of  the  Regulation  may  either 
collect  the  confirmation  statement  or  prepare  the  record  of  interview  after  personally 
hearing  the  statements.  In  this  case,  in  order  to  ensure  that  the  statement  was  made 
voluntarily,  no  one  shall  be  forced  to  make  statements.  (Amended  on  November  30, 
2006)

Regulation, any methods such as meeting, writing, calling, etc. may be taken.

CHAPTER III. CLOSING OF DISPUTE CASE PRIOR TO REFERRAL TO THE 

COMMISSION 

§8. Notification of Legal Proceedings Launched by the Petitioner, etc. 

“The matter stipulated in the Enforcement Rules” in [§9(2)] of the Regulation shall be 
each of the following items: (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

1.  The  court  with  which  the  legal  proceedings  or  the  organization  with  which  the 
request for mediation has been filed; 

2. The date when the legal proceedings or the request for mediation was filed; 

3. Serial number assigned to the legal proceedings or the case; and 

4. A copy of legal proceedings or request for mediation.

§9. Withdrawal Application, etc. 

(1)  The  application  for  withdrawal  of  mediation  request  pursuant  to  [§10(3)]  of  the 
Regulation shall be in accordance with Form 4 of Annex. 

(2)  In  case  where  the  petitioner  makes  a  verbal  withdrawal  request,  employee  of  the 
Exchange  who  is  responsible  for  dispute  mediation  may  confirm  the  petitioner’s 
willingness of withdrawal through such methods as recording.     

(Total amendment on November 30, 2006) 

CHAPTER IV. DISPUTE MEDIATION BY THE COMMISSION 

§10. Referral to the Commission and Notification Thereof 

(1) In the event that the Chairman refers a case to the Commission pursuant to [§12(3)] 
of  the  Regulation,  the  Commission  members  (in  case  of  requesting  the  Deliberation 
Committee to deliberate the case, the members of the Deliberation Committee shall be 
included; the same hereinafter in this Article) shall be notified of the case being referred, 
with the attached relevant materials. (Amended on November 30, 2006) 

(2) Pursuant to [§12(3)] of the Regulation, the Chairman shall immediately inform the 
concerned parties of the  facts  about  the referral  to the Commission  (in case of having 
requested the Deliberation Committee to deliberate the case, such fact shall be included), 
the  fact  that  additional  materials  may  be  submitted,  the  list  of  Commission  members, 
the fact that the request  for exclusion of and challenge against a Commission member 
may be filed. (Amended on November 30, 2006) 

§11. Tenure of Members of Deliberation Committee, etc. 

(1)  Pursuant  to  [§13(6)]  of  the  Regulation,  the  term  of  the  persons  who  have  been 
appointed  as  a  member  of  deliberation  committee  shall  be  two  (2)  years  from  the 
appointment date, and he/she may be  reappointed up to  two (2) times for an one-year 
term each time. (Amended on February 3, 2009; July 7, 2011) 

(2) The persons who correspond to any of the following items shall be disqualified as a 
member of deliberation committee:      (Amended on February 3, 2009; July 7, 2011) 

1. The person who comes under the disqualification criteria stipulated in [§382(1)] 
of the Financial Investment Services and Capital Market Act; and       

4 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
2.  The  person  against  whom  the  Financial  Services  Commission  has  imposed  the 
disciplinary  measures  that  is  severer  than  the  reprehension  warning  or  salary 
reduction. 

(3) The Chairman shall request the members appointed to the Deliberation Committee 
to submit the ethics pledge noted in Form 5-2 of Annex.      [July 7, 2011] 

(Wholly amended on November 30, 2006) 

§11-2. Reasons for Dismissal of Committee Member 

In  case  where  the  Committee  member  corresponds  to  any  of  the  following  cases,  the 
Committee shall dismiss the concerned member:       

1.    After having been appointed to the Committee, when it becomes aware the fact 

that  the  concerned  member  comes  under  the  exclusion  causes  or  the  concerned 

member came under the exclusion causes;   

2.    After  having  been  appointed  to  the  Committee,  when  the  concerned  member 

lost the qualifications noted in each Item of [§13(2)] of the Regulation; 

3.    After  having  been  appointed  to  the  Committee,  if  the  concerned  member  has 

violated  the  obligation  to  keep  the  confidentiality  noted  in  [§11]  and  obligation  to 

maintain  the  integrity  noted  in  [§13]  of  the  Regulation  for  the  Establishment  and 

Operation of the Deliberation & Advisory Committees; and 

4.    In case where the details of qualifications submitted at the time of appointment 

were found to be false.     

[July 7, 2011] 

§12. Operation of Deliberation Committee 

(1) The decisions  of deliberation committee shall be made by a majority  voting and a 
majority of members deliberating the concerned case presented. (Amended on February 
3, 2009) 

(2)  The  members  of  deliberation  committee  composed  to  deliberate  a  concerned  case 
shall ensure that he/she does not come under the causes for exclusion noted in [§14] of 
the Regulation and prepare and submit the confirmation of not coming under the causes 

5 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
for exclusion in accordance with Form 5-3 of Annex.    [July 7, 2011] 

(3)    When the deliberation of case is completed, the deliberation committee shall report 
the result of deliberation to the Commission.   

(Wholly amended on November 30, 2006) 

§13. Secretary of Deliberation Committee 

The secretary of Deliberation Committee shall be the head of the Department-in-charge 
of dispute mediation. (Amended on November 30, 2006) 

§14. Exclusion of Commission Members and Members of Deliberation Committee, 

etc. 

(1) Pursuant to [§14(5)] of the Regulation, when the concerned parties intend to request 
the  exclusion  of  or  challenge  against  a  Commission  member  (including  a  member  of 
Deliberation Committee; the same hereinafter in this Paragraph), such request shall be 
made within five (5) days from the receipt date of the list of Commission members. In 
this case, the Chairman shall make the decision whether or not to exclude or accept the 
challenge within three (3) days from the receipt of the request. 

(2) In case where a member of deliberation committee is excluded due to the reasons of 
exclusion  request,  challenge  to  or  voluntary  exclusion  pursuant  to  [§13(3)]  of  the 
Regulation,  the  Chairman,  when  it  is  deemed  necessary,  may  replace  the  excluded 
member with another member from deliberation committee. In this case, the concerned 
parties  shall  be  notified  of  the  list  of  replaced  member  of  deliberation  committee. 
(Amended on November 30, 2006) 

(3) The exclusion and challenge or evasion shall be requested in accordance with Form 
5 and Form 6 of Annex, respectably. 

§15. Change of Mediation Date 

(1) Pursuant to [§16(3)] of the Regulation, when intending to change the mediation date 
due  to  an  urgent  circumstance  of  the  Commission  or  the  concerned  parties,  the 
Chairman may change the mediation date by obtaining the consent of concerned parties 
until one (1) day before the mediation date. (Amended on November 30, 2006) 

(2)  In  case  where  a  concerned  party  requests  the  change  of  mediation  date,  the 
Chairman  shall  rearrange  the  mediation  date,  provided  that  there  are  no  inevitable 
reasons, and notify the rearranged date to the concerned parties. 

6 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
§16. Nonappearance of Concerned Parties, etc. 

Pursuant to [§17(3)] of the Regulation, in case where the concerned party did not appear 
on the mediation date in disregard of the Commission’s request to appear, the Chairman 
may  make  the  decision  on  whether  or  not  to  proceed  with  the  mediation  meeting. 
(Amended on November 30, 2006) 

§17. Format of Mediation Decision Notice, etc.     

The  mediation  decision  notice,  dismissal  decision  notice  and  letter  of  acceptance  of 
mediation  decision  pursuant  to  [§18(5)]  of  the  Regulation  shall  be  prepared  in 
accordance with Form 7, Form 8 and Form 9 of Annex. (Wholly amended on February 
3, 2009) 

§18. Realization of Mediation, etc. 

(1) The consent statement pursuant to [§19(6)] of the Regulation shall refer to Form 10 
of Annex. 

(2)  Pursuant  to  [§19(6)]  of  the  Regulation,  when  the  mediation  of  case  is  or  is  not 
realized, the Chairman shall notify the Commission members such outcome. 

(Wholly amended on February 3, 2009) 

§19. Notification of the Result of the Follow-up Actions by Member 

(1) Pursuant to [§21(1)] of the Regulation, the concerned party who is the member shall 
report  the  progresses  of  follow-up  actions  such  as  the  damage  compensation, 
reinforcement  of  system  and  improvement  of  regulations  according  to  the  mediation 
recommendation  to  the  Commission  in  writing  within  twenty  (20)  days  from  the 
realization date of mediation. (Amended on November 30, 2009) 

(2) In case where the reporting of follow-up actions pursuant to Para.(1) is delayed due 
to  an unavoidable  circumstance, the concerned  member shall  report the  fact  about  the 
delay and the reasons to the Commission in writing. (Amended on November 30, 2006)

CHAPTER V. SUPPLEMENTARY PROVISIONS 

§20. Support for Legal Proceedings 

(1) Pursuant to [§22(2)] of the Regulation, in case where the petitioner wishes to seek 
the support for launching the legal proceedings, the request for support shall be made in 
writing. 

(2)  In case where the petitioner requests the support for legal  proceedings pursuant  to 
Para.(1), the Chairman shall make the decision whether or not to provide the support for 
legal proceeding for the concerned case and notify such decision to the petitioner. 

(3) The Chairman may establish a legal team to support the legal proceedings launched 
by the petitioner mentioned in Para.(1). 

(4) In case where the causes noted in any of the following items occurs after making the 
decision to provide the support for legal proceedings, the Chairman may terminate the 
concerned support: (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

1. Case  where it  was  found  that  the  document submitted  or  statement made  at  the 
time when the petitioner submitted the request for support for legal proceedings has 
been falsified; 

2. Case where the petitioner failed to fulfill the responsibilities of concerned party of 
legal proceedings without justifiable reasons; 

3. Case where it has been realized that the concerned support for legal proceedings 
would bring about no practical benefit or serve the public interest on the basis of the 
new  facts  found  during  the  course  of  providing  the  support  for  legal  proceedings; 
and       

4.  In  addition,  case  of  coming  under  the  reasons  that  the  Chairman  established 
independently. 

(5)  In  addition  to  Paras.(1)  through  (4),  the  details  relating  to  the  support  for  legal 
proceedings shall be in accordance with those determined separately by the Chairman. 

(Wholly amended on November 30, 2006) 

§20-2. Public Opinion Gathering 

Public opinion gathering pursuant to [§26-2] of the Regulation shall be governed by the 
provisions of [§5] of the Internal Regulations Management Regulation and [§6] of the 
Regulations Management Regulation.

§21. Notification of Illegal Actions, etc. 

Pursuant  to  [§27]  of  the  Regulation,  when  it  has  been  confirmed  that  the  officers  or 
employees  of the member who is  the respondent  had committed the act  infringing the 
laws  or  regulations  in  the  process  of  dealing  with  the  case,  the  Chairman  may  notify 
such finding to the respondent.    [November 30, 2006]

ADDENDA 
(January 24, 2005) 

§1. Effective Date 

This  Enforcement  Rules  shall  become  effective  on  the  date  the  Exchange  is 
incorporated. 

§2. Interim Measures for the Petition for Mediation, etc. 

The  petition  for  mediation  filed  and  the  notification  of  the  fact  about  the  receipt  of 
application for mediation sent by the Korea Stock Exchange and the KOSDAQ Market 
Committee  before  the  effective  date  of  this  Enforcement  Rules  shall  be  construed  as 
those carried out pursuant to this Enforcement Rules. 

ADDENDA 
(March 24, 2005) 

§1. Effective Date 

This Enforcement Rules shall become effective on March 24, 2005.       

§2. Exceptions to the Term of the Members of the First Deliberation Committee 

Notwithstanding the amended provisions of [§11(3)], in case of the term of the members 
of the Deliberation Committee who are appointed for the first  time since the effective 
date of this Enforcement Rules, the term of one half of the Committee members shall be 
one (1) year from the appointment date according to the decision made by the Chairman 
at the time of appointment.
