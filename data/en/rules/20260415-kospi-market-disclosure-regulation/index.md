PART  I.  REPORTING, ETC  BY  KOSPI  MARKET-LISTED  CORPORATIONS 

CHAPTER  I.  GENERAL  PROVISIONS 

§1.  Purpose 

The  purpose  of  this  Part  is  to  stipulate  the  matters  necessary  for  reporting,  disclosing  and 
managing the corporate information in the KOSPI Market established by the Korea Exchange 
(hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the  Exchange”)  pursuant  to  Article  391  of  the  Financial 
Investment Services and Capital Markets Act (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"). (Wholly 
amended on January 28, 2009) 

§2.  Definitions   

(1) The term “KOSPI market-listed corporation” in this Part means a corporation prescribed 
in  Article  9(15)3  of  the  Act,  which  is  listed  on  the  KOSPI  Market.  Provided  that,  the 
investment  companies  under  the  Act  and  the  companies  which  specialize  in  investing  and 
financing  for  infrastructure  under  the  Act  on  Public-Private  Partnerships  in  Infrastructure 
shall be excluded.   

(2)  The  term  "listed  foreign  corporation"  in  this Part  means  a  foreign  corporation  that  falls 
under any of the following subparagraphs:   

1. A foreign corporation that has issued the stocks listed on the KOSPI Market; and 

2.  In  cases  where  the  depository  receipts  (DRs)  (referring  to  the  depository  receipts 
prescribed in Article 4(8) of the Act; the same hereinafter) related to stocks are listed on 
the KOSPI Market, the foreign corporations which issued the relevant stocks. 

(3) The term "inquired disclosure" in this Part refers to the reporting of corporate information 
to the Exchange by a KOSPI-stock-listed corporation in response to the request for disclosure 
of such information which the Exchange has made to the relevant corporation pursuant to the 
provisions of Article 391(2)3 of the Act. 

(4)  The  term  "disclosure  officer"  in  this  Part  means  a  person  of  a  KOSPI-stock-listed 
corporation [in the case of entrusted management real estate investment trusts and corporate 
restructuring real estate investment trusts (including the case where a self-managed real estate 
investment trust entrusted its general clerical services to an institution entrusted with general 
clerical  services,  etc.)  referred  to  in  paragraph  (14),  it  shall  be  the  company  entrusted  with 
business  administration;  in  the  case  of  ship  investment  companies  under  paragraph  (18),  it 
shall be the ship management company] who falls under any of the following subparagraphs 
and is authorized to perform the reporting duties on behalf of the relevant KOSPI-stock-listed 
corporation.  Provided  that,  in  the  case  of  a  corporation  which  has  filed  an  application  for 
commencement of rehabilitation procedures (including the simple rehabilitation procedures;

procedures  under  the  Debtor  Rehabilitation  and  Bankruptcy  Act,  the  protective  custodian 
under  [§85]  or  the  custodian  under  Article  74  of  the  same  Act  shall  be  deemed  to  be  the 
disclosure officer. (Amended on April 18, 2012; July 22, 2015)   

1. The representative director or a full-time director (in the case of a corporation with the 
executive officer system, it refers to the chief executive officer or executive officers; the 
same hereinafter); and   

2.  As  a  full-time  employee  who  falls  under  the  provisions  of  Article  401-2(1)3  of  the 
Commercial  Act,  the  one  who  attends  the  board  meetings  or  other  meetings  where 
material management decisions are made. 

(5) The term "unfaithful disclosure" in this Part refers to a case where a KOSPI-stock-listed 
corporation  falls  under  any  of  the  cases  of  noncompliance  with  disclosure  obligations, 
reversal  of  disclosure  or  change  of  disclosure,  by  failing  to  faithfully  fulfill  the  reporting 
obligations  under  this  Regulation  or  reversing  or  changing  the  details  of  the  information 
already reported.   

(6)  The  term  “unfaithful  disclosure  corporation”  in  this  Part  refers  to  a  KOSPI-stock-listed 
corporation  that  has  been  designated  as  an  unfaithful  disclosure  corporation  pursuant  to 
[§35(1)] since it fell under the cases of unfaithful disclosure under paragraph (5). (Amended 
on November 24, 2021) 

(7) The term "largest shareholder”, “major shareholder” and “affiliated company” in this Part 
refers to a person mentioned in each of the following subparagraphs, respectively: (Amended 
on February 22, 2013; July 22, 2015; December 28, 2016; November 24, 2021)   

1. Largest shareholder: The largest shareholder prescribed in Subparagraph 6(a) of Article 
2 of the Act on Corporate Governance of Financial Companies. In such cases, “financial 
companies”  shall  be  construed  as  “KOSPI-stock-listed  corporations”  and  “outstanding 
voting  shares  (or  equities;  hereafter  the  same  shall  apply)”  shall  be  construed  as 
“outstanding shares”;   

2. Major shareholder: The major shareholder prescribed in Subparagraph 6(b) of Article 2 
of  the  Act  on  Corporate  Governance  of  Financial  Companies.  In  such  cases,  “financial 
companies” shall be construed as “KOSPI-stock-listed corporations”. Provided that, from 
among the professional investors under Article 9(5) of the Act, those who are stipulated 
in the Enforcement Rules of this Regulation (hereinafter referred to as “the Enforcement 
Rules”) and do not exert influence on the management of the relevant corporation shall be 
excluded;   

3. Affiliated company: The affiliated company (including foreign corporation) prescribed 
in  Subparagraph  12  of  Article  2  of  the  Monopoly  Regulation  and  Fair  Trade  Act 
(hereinafter referred to as “the Fair Trade Act”) 

(8) The term "stocks, etc." in this Part means the stocks or depository receipts. 

(9) The term "derivatives" in this Part means the derivatives prescribed in Article 5 of the Act.

following subparagraphs. Provided that, in the case of foreign companies, it shall refer to the 
company (hereinafter referred to as the “foreign holding company) whose primary business is 
to control the business activities of another company by holding the stocks of the company 
(In  cases  where  the  aggregate  value  of  stocks  of  subsidiaries  held  exceeds  50%  of  total

1. A holding company under Subparagraph 7 of Article 2 of the Fair Trade Act; 

2. A financial holding company under Article 2(1)1 of the Financial Holding Companies

3.  A  company  under  [§2(1)20(c)]  of  the  KOSPI  Market  Listing  Regulation  (hereinafter 
referred to as “the Listing Regulation”). 

(12) The term “subsidiary” in this Part refers to a company falling under any of the following 
subparagraphs. Provided that, the subsidiary of a foreign holding company shall refer to the 
subsidiary subject  to  consolidation in  accordance with  the  accounting standards adopted by 
the  foreign  holding  company.  (Amended  on  November  4,  2011;  February  22,  2013; 
November 24, 2021) 

1. A subsidiary prescribed in Subparagraph 8 of Article 2 of the Fair Trade Act; 

2. A subsidiary prescribed in Article 2(1)2 of the Financial Holding Companies Act; and 

3. A subsidiary under [§2(1)22(c)] of the Listing Regulation. 

(13)  The  term  “large-sized  corporation”  in  this  Part  refers  to  a  KOSPI  market-listed 
corporation of which total asset is KRW 2 trillion or more at the end of the latest fiscal year.   

(14) The term “real estate investment companies" in this Part refers to the self-managed real 
estate  investment  trusts,  the  entrusted  management  real  estate  investment  trusts  and  the 
corporate restructuring real estate investment trusts under Subparagraph 1 of Article 2 of the 
Real Estate Investment Company Act.   

(15)  The  term  “financial  creditor"  in  this  Part  refers  to  a  financial  creditor  provided  for  in 
Subparagraph  2  of  Article  2  of  the  Corporate  Restructuring  Promotion  Act.  (Amended  on 
December 28, 2016)

(Amended on December 28, 2016) 

(17) The term “council of the financial creditors" in this Part means the council of financial

(21)  The  term  “parent  company”,  “consolidated  subsidiary”  and  “principal  consolidated

[February 22, 2013] (Amended on July 22, 2015; December 19, 2018) 

1. Parent  company: A parent  company prescribed in Subparagraph 3 of Article 2 of the 
Act on the External Audit of Stock Companies, etc. (hereinafter referred to as the “Act on 
External  Audit”).  Provided  that,  in  cases  where  a  listed  foreign  corporation  prepares 
consolidated financial statements in accordance with the accounting standards adopted by 
the  corporation  including  the  international  accounting  standards,  the  parent  company 
according to those standards. 

2. Consolidated subsidiary: A subsidiary prescribed in Article 7(1) of the Act on External 
Audit. Provided that, in cases where a listed foreign corporation prepares the consolidated 
financial  statement  pursuant  to  the  accounting  standards  adopted  by  the  corporation, 
including  the  international  accounting  standards,  the  subsidiary  according  to  those 
standards. 

3. Principal subsidiary: From among the consolidated subsidiaries noted in subparagraph 
2, the one of which total asset in the latest fiscal year is at least 10/100 (2/1000 in the case 
of a large-sized company) of total asset of the parent company.   

(22) In addition to the definitions in paragraphs (1) through (21), the terms used in this Part 
shall have the same meaning as those given to them in the Act, the Enforcement Decree of 
the  Act,  the  Enforcement  Rule  of  the  Act  and  other  relevant  statutes,  and  the  rules  and 
regulations  concerning  the  business  of  the  Exchange.  (Amended  on  December  18,  2009;

§3.  Applicable  Standards   

(1)  The  financial  matters  applied  in  this  Part  shall  be  based  on  the  financial  statements 
(referring  to  the  consolidated  financial  statement,  in  the  case  of  a  KOSPI-stock-listed 
corporation which is a holding company or a parent company; the same hereinafter) revised 
according to the result of the audit conducted by an auditor prescribed in Article 2(7) of the 
Act on External Audit (hereinafter referred to as "CPA auditor"). Provided that, this provision 
shall not apply to the financial matters applied in [§7(1)2(e)(iii)]. (Amended on January 28, 
2009; April 21, 2010; February 22, 2013; December 19, 2018) 

(2)  Financial  matters  and  accounting  terminologies  in  this  Part  shall  conform  to  the 
accounting standards under Article 5 of the Act on External Audit. (Amended on January 28, 
2009; December 19, 2018) 

(3) When applying the financial matters in this Part, in cases where the annual report for the 
latest  fiscal year has not been submitted or the opinion of CPA auditor in  the latest annual 
report is adverse opinion, disclaimer of opinion or qualified opinion due to the limit placed on 
the scope of audit, the financial statement at the end of the year immediately preceding the 
latest  year  shall  be  deemed  to  be  the  financial  statement  at  the  end  of  the  latest  year. 
(Amended on January 28, 2009) 

(4) In applying this Part, the equity capital calculated in accordance with [§2(10)], the sales 
amount of the latest fiscal year (excluding the sales amount under [§7(1)2(e)(iii)]), the total 
assets, etc. at the end of the latest fiscal year, shall  apply for the period from the day when 
three (3) months have elapsed since the end of each fiscal year to the end of three (3) months 
after the end of the next fiscal year. Provided that, in the case of a listed foreign corporation 
that is applied with Article 176(2) of the Enforcement Decree of the Act, it shall be the period 
from the day when four (4) months have elapsed since the end of each fiscal year to the end 
of  four  (4)  months  after  the  end  of  the  next  fiscal  year.  (Amended  on  December  23,  2005; 
January 28, 2009; April 18, 2012; February 22, 2013)   

(5) When applying this Part to a KOSPI-stock-listed corporation whose equity capital is less 
than its capital stock as of the time of occurrence of the cause for reporting or disclosure, the 
capital  stock  shall  be  deemed  as  the  equity  capital.  (Amended  on  December  23,  2005, 
January 28, 2009; December 18, 2009) 

(6) Notwithstanding the provisions of paragraphs (1) and (2), in cases where a listed foreign 
corporation  has  prepared  its  financial  statement  in  accordance  with  the  international 
accounting standards (IAS) established by the  International  Accounting Standards Board or 
the generally accepted accounting principles in the United States of America (US GAAP) and 
has  it  audited  according  to  the  relevant  laws  of  home  country  or  the  country  where  the 
company’s stocks are listed, it may be based on the accounting standards/principles adopted 
for  the  preparation  of  the  relevant  financial  statement.  [December  23,  2005]  (Amended  on

§74.  Submission  of  Electronic  Document  on  behalf  of  Submitter   

(1) A submitter may have another person perform the task of submitting the reports, etc. by 
means of electronic document. 

(2)  The  person  who  submits  the  electronic  documents  on  behalf  of  a  submitter  (hereinafter 
referred  to  as  the  “submission  agent”)  shall  register  his/her  personal  information  with  the 
Exchange in advance as stipulated in the Enforcement Rules before the submission and shall 
do the same when there is any change in the registered information. Provided that, in cases 
where  the  submission  agent  has  registered  or  re-registered  the  changes  in  the  information 
registered with the Financial Supervisory Service pursuant to the Regulation on Issuance and 
Disclosure,  the  registration  with  the  Exchange  may  be  substituted  by  the  submission  of  a 
copy of registration documents submitted to the Financial Supervisory Service.   

(3) In cases where a submitter has entered into an agency agreement with a submission agent, 
the submitter shall submit a copy of the agreement to the Exchange without delay and shall 
do the same when there are any changes in the details of the agreement. 

§75.  Validity, etc.  of  Digital  Signature 

(1) When intending to submit the reports, etc. in a form of electronic document, a submitter 
shall affix a digital signature on them.   

(2)  In  cases  where  a  submitter  has  affixed  a  digital  signature  on  an  electronic  document 
pursuant to paragraph (1), it shall be deemed that the submitter has signed or affixed his/her 
seal on the document.   

(3) In the case of the reports, etc. that must be signed by more than one person, the submitter 
shall  keep  the  paper  copy  of  the  relevant  documents  for  the  duration  that  the  relevant 
documents must be disclosed pursuant to the related laws and the regulations of the Exchange 
and shall submit them without delay upon receiving a request from the Exchange. (Amended 
on January 28, 2009) 

(4) The registration and certification of the digital signature for signing electronic documents 
digitally pursuant to paragraph (1) and other matters relating to the digital signature shall be 
accomplished  in  accordance  with  the  manner  stipulated  in  the  Regulation  on  Securities 
Issuance and Disclosure.

ANNEXES 

Core Principles of Corporate Governance 

Classification 

Core Principles   

Shareholders 

Board of 
Directors 
(“the Board”) 

Audit Systems 

Shareholders’ 
Rights 

 Shareholders shall be able to receive sufficient and timely 
information, which is necessary to exercise their rights, and to 
exercise their rights through appropriate procedures. 

Equitable 
Treatment of 
Shareholders 

 Shareholders shall hold fair voting rights according to the 
class and number of shares owned; and the corporation shall 
make efforts to be equipped with a system that provides 
corporate information to the shareholders in a fair manner. 

Functions of the 
Board 

 The Board shall establish the business objectives and 
strategies in the best interests of the corporation and its 
shareholders, and effectively supervise the activities of the 
management. 

Composition of 
the Board   

 The Board shall be composed so as to facilitate effective 
decision-making and supervision of management; and the 
directors shall be appointed through a transparent process, 
which reflects the diverse opinions of shareholders. 

Responsibilities 
of Outside 
Directors 

 Outsider directors shall be able to independently participate 
in important corporate management decision-making, and to 
supervise and support the management as board members. 

Evaluation of 
Outside 
Directors’ 
Activities 

To promote active performance of duties by the outside 
directors, their activities shall undergo fair evaluation; and the 
decisions on their remuneration and reappointment shall be 
made on the basis of the outcomes of such evaluation. 

Operation of the 
Board 

 The Board shall be operated efficiently and rationally to 
ensure that the management decisions are made in the best 
interests of the corporation and shareholders. 

Committees of 
the Board 

 For an efficient operation, the Board shall establish internal 
committees which are responsible for the performance of 
specific functions and roles. 

Internal 
Auditing Bodies 

 Internal auditing bodies, such as the audit committee and 
auditors, shall perform their auditing duties faithfully by 
maintaining independence from the management and 
controlling shareholders, and the details of key activities of 
internal auditing bodies shall be disclosed. 

External 
Auditors 

 In order to make certain that the shareholders and other 
users have confidence in the corporate financial information, 
an external auditor must perform his/her auditing tasks fairly 
and independently from the corporation audited, including its 
management and controlling shareholders.
