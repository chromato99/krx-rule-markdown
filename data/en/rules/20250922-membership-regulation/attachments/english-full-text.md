CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS 

§1. Purpose   

The purpose of this Membership Regulation (hereinafter referred to as "the Regulation") 
is to stipulate the matters necessary with respect to the administration of members of the 
Korea Exchange (hereinafter referred to as "the Exchange"), including the membership 
category and qualifications, the member admission and withdrawal, and the rights and 
obligations  of  members,  pursuant  to  Article  387  of  the  Financial  Investment  Services 
and Capital Markets Act (hereinafter referred to as "the Act"). (Amended on January 28, 
2009) 

§2. Definitions   

(1) The terms used in this Regulation shall be defined as follows: (Amended on January 
28, 2009; April 29, 2020) 

1.  The  term  “member”  means  a  market  participant  who  is  allowed  to  trade  the 
securities  in  the  securities  markets  or  exchange-traded  derivative  products  in  the 
Derivatives  Market,  which  are  established  and  operated  by  the  Exchange,  in  their 
own name, irrespective of the owner of the account used; 

1-2. The term “trading participant” means a person who participates in trading on an 
alternative trading system. [March 4, 2025] 

2.  The  term  “Joint  Compensation  Fund”  means  the  Joint  Compensation  Fund  for 
Damages  prescribed  in  Article  394(1)  of  the  Act  and  is  classified  into  each  of 
following items: 

(a)  Basic  Joint  Compensation  Fund:  The  joint  compensation  fund  that  clearing 
members set aside in the Exchange in advance in order to compensate for damages 
incurred  from  the  failure  to  repay  the  liabilities  (hereinafter  referred  to  as 
“settlement failure in the securities markets, etc.”) with respect to the transactions 
(including  transactions  on  an  alternative  trading  system;  the  same  hereinafter  in 
[§3(2)],  [§13(2)],  and  [§21])  of  securities  in  the  securities  markets  and  the 
transactions  of  the  exchange-traded  derivative  products  in  the  derivatives  market; 
and (Amended on March 4, 2025) 

(b) Additional Joint Compensation Fund: The joint compensation fund that clearing 
members deposit with the Exchange at once in compliance with the demand of the 
Exchange  in  cases  where  the  basic  joint  compensation  fund  and  the  settlement 
reserve stipulated in [§54(3)1] of the Articles of Incorporation (hereinafter referred 
to as “settlement reserve”) fall short to fully compensate for the damages incurred 
from the settlement failure in the securities markets, etc. 

1 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
     
 
 
3.  The  term  “fidelity  guarantee  money”  means  the  fidelity  guarantee  money  of 
members under the provisions of Article 395 of the Act; and 

4. The term “business day” means any day other that a holiday, Saturday and Labor 
Day. 

(2) The terms in this Regulation, other than those defined in paragraphs (1), shall have 
the  same meanings given to them in the Act  and the regulations  of the Exchange that 
govern the Exchange’s business. (Amended on January 28, 2009; April 29, 2020) 

CHAPTER II. MEMBERSHIP CATEGORIES AND QUALIFICATIONS OF 

MEMBERS 

§3. Membership Categories   

(1)  The  membership  of  the  Exchange  shall  be  categorized  into  each  of  the  following 
subparagraphs according to the markets and the financial investment products in which 
they are entitled to participate: (Amended on January 28, 2009; September 2, 2015; July 
19, 2023) 

1.  Securities  member:  Any  member  who  is  entitled  to  take  part  in  the  trading  of 
securities in the securities markets;   

2. Equity securities member: Any member who is entitled to take part in the trading 
of  equity  securities  (excluding  collective  investment  securities)  in  the  securities 
markets; 

2-2.  Collective  investment  securities  member:  Any  member  who  is  entitled  to  take 
part in the trading of collective investment securities in the securities markets;   

3. Debt securities member: Any member who is entitled to take part in the trading of 
debt securities in the securities markets;   

4. Derivatives member: Any member who is entitled to take part in the trading in the 
derivatives market;   

5. Equity derivatives member: Any member who is entitled to take part in the trading 
of exchange-traded derivatives based on equity in the derivatives market; and 

6.  Currency-interest  rate  derivatives  member:  Any  member  who  is  entitled  to  take 
part  in  the  trading  of  exchange-traded  derivatives  based  on  currencies  or  debt 
securities in the derivatives market. 

(2)  Members  of  the  Exchange  shall  be  categorized  further  into  each  of  the  following 
categories according to the extent of responsibilities that a member has in fulfilling the 

2 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
settlement  obligations  subsequent  to  the  trading  of  securities  or  exchange-traded 
derivative products: (Amended on January 28, 2009) 

1. Clearing member: A member who is entitled to settle the securities and exchange-
traded derivatives transactions conducted in its own name or the trades of securities 
and  exchange-traded  derivatives  that  have  been  entrusted  by  non-clearing  members 
(including trading participants) in its own name. (Amended on March 4, 2025) 

2.  Non-clearing  member:  A  member  who  is  required  to  entrust  the  settlement  of 
securities  and  exchange-traded  derivatives  trades  conducted  in  its  own  name  to  a 
clearing member. 

§4. Qualifications of Members 

(1) An entity that is eligible to become a member of the Exchange shall be an entity that 
has obtained the authorization for investment trading business or investment brokerage 
business pursuant to Article 12(1) of the Act. (Amended on January 28, 2009)

Section 1. Member Admission   

§5. Application for Membership and Examination and Approval Thereof 

(1) An entity that wishes to become a member of the Exchange shall file an application 
for  each  category  of  membership,  as  stipulated  in  the  Enforcement  Rules  of  this 
Regulation (hereinafter referred to as “the Enforcement Rules”. (Amended on April 29, 
2020) 

(2)  An  applicant  eligible  for  filing  the  application  for  membership  stipulated  in 
paragraph  (1)  shall  be  the  entity  that  has  obtained  the  authorization  or  preliminary 
authorization for the investment trading business or investment brokerage business from 
the  Financial  Services  Commission  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the  FSC”)  or  has 
submitted a request for the relevant authorization. (Amended on January 28, 2009)     

(3)  The  Exchange  shall  examine  whether  or  not  the  applicant  satisfies  the  member 
admission  criteria  specified  in  [§6],  by  validating  the accuracy  of the  details  stated  in 
the  application  while  taking  into  account  the  opinions  presented  by  the  related 
institutions. (Amended on April 29, 2020) 

(4) To verify the details stated in the application form and interview the managers, the 
Exchange may carry out a site investigation.

(hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the  Board”).  In  such  cases,  the  Board  may  approve  the 
member admission with certain conditions attached.   

(6) In cases where a member participating in the securities markets wishes to participate 
in the Derivatives Market or a member participating in the Derivatives Market wishes to 
participate  in  the  securities  markets,  such  member  shall  submit  an  application  for 
membership pursuant to paragraph (1). (Amended on January 28, 2009) 

(7) Matters relating to the application for membership and other matters necessary for 
member admission shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules. (Amended on April 29, 
2020) 

§6. Criteria for Member Admission   

(1) To become a member, an applicant for membership shall satisfy each of following 
criteria, in accordance with the category of membership and the variety of securities or 
exchange-traded  products  that  the  relevant  applicant  wishes  to  handle:  (Amended  on 
January 28, 2009) 

1. To satisfy the financial requirements specified in [§7];   

2.  To  be  equipped  with  adequate  facilities,  including  computer  equipment,  required 
for efficient accomplishment of the business operation;   

3. To have the adequate manpower who have the expertise and integrity necessary for 
conducting business; and   

4. To have a sufficient social credibility.   

(2) Any entity that has been expelled from membership pursuant to [§11(2)] shall not be 
admitted again as a member before three (3) years have elapsed from the date of such 
expulsion. (Amended on April 29, 2020) 

(3)  Detailed  matters  concerning  admission  criteria  under 
the  provisions  of 
subparagraphs  2  through  4  of  paragraph  (1)  and  other  matters  necessary  for  member 
admission shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules. [April 29, 2020] 

§7. Financial Requirements 

Any entity that wishes to become a member shall meet all of the financial requirements 
specified  in  each  of  the  following  subparagraphs.  Provided,  That  the  provisions  of 
subparagraph 2 shall not apply to non-clearing members and the financial requirements 
for an applicant who is a concurrently-run financial investment business entity provided 
in Article 8 of the Act shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules. (Amended on April 
29, 2020; July 19, 2023)

Section 2. Membership Withdrawal   

§10. Voluntary Withdrawal   

(1) When a member intends to withdraw its membership, it shall file an application for 
membership withdrawal in the forms specified in the Enforcement Rules. (Amended on 
April 29, 2020) 

(2) In cases where a member files an application for membership withdrawal pursuant 
to  paragraph  (1),  the  date  of  such  withdrawal  shall  be  the  later  of  the  days  under  the 
following subparagraphs: (Amended on April 29, 2020) 

1. The day on which the period specified in the Enforcement Rules has elapsed from 
the date of application for withdrawal; and 

2. The business day following the day on which the member has settled all unsettled 
transactions  (including  unsettled  transaction  contracts  concluded  on  alternative 
trading  systems  and  unpurchased  agreements  in  the  repurchase agreements  market; 
the same hereinafter in [§14(2)]) or has closed out all open interests in the derivatives 
market,  and  has  cleared  any  obligations  established  on  the  Exchange  (including 
designated  clearing  members  in  the case of  a non-clearing  member).  (Amended  on 
March 4, 2025) 

(3) In cases where, before the withdrawal date of a member who has filed an application 
for  voluntary  withdrawal  under  paragraph  (1),  a  settlement  failure  occurs  caused  by 
other members, and the loss from the settlement failure has not been finalized until the 

6 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
withdrawal  date,  the  voluntary  withdrawal  member  shall  nevertheless  be  deemed  a 
member to the extent stipulated in the Enforcement Rules. [April 29, 2020] 

§11. Obligatory Withdrawal   

(1) In cases where a member falls under any of the following causes, such member shall 
be  deemed  to  have  withdrawn  its  membership.  In  such  cases,  any  member  who  falls 
under  the  cause  subparagraph  1  shall  be  deemed  to  have  withdrawn  only  from  the 
category of membership which is related to the cause. (Amended on January 28, 2009; 
April 29, 2020) 

1.  Revocation  of  the  authorization  for  investment  trading  business  or  investment 
brokerage business or closure or transfer of business; and 

2. Dissolution. 

3. [Deleted on April 29, 2020] 

(2) In cases where a member has been subject to expulsion from membership pursuant 
to [§136(1)1], the date of withdrawal of the member shall be according to the following 
subparagraphs: [April 29, 2020] 

1. In  cases where  an official objection has not been filed pursuant to [§38(4)], the 
next business day following the last day for filing an official objection; 

2. In cases where an official objection has been filed pursuant to [§38(4)], the next 
business day following the day the Exchange has completed the handling of the case 
according to the same paragraph. 

§12. Notification of Membership Withdrawal   

(1) In cases where a member has withdrawn its membership pursuant to [§10] or [§11], 
the Exchange shall notify such facts to other members.   

(2)  In  cases  where  a  member  who  withdraws  its  membership  pursuant  to  [§10]  and 
[§11] is a clearing member of the securities market, the Exchange may notify alternative 
trading systems of the withdrawal. [March 4, 2025] 

§13. Succession of membership   

(1) In cases where a member withdraws its membership as a result of a merger, split-off, 
split-and-merger,  or  business  transfer  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “merger,  etc.”),  upon 
obtaining  the  approval  of  the  Exchange,  the  company  succeeding  the  business  as  a 
result  of  each  relevant  event  of  the  merger,  etc.  may  succeed  the  membership  of  the 
withdrawing member. In such cases, surviving company, newly incorporated company 

7 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
or the company taking over the business that is not a member shall be deemed to have 
been admitted as a member. (Amended on December 30, 2015; April 29, 2020) 

(2) In the case of succeeding the membership pursuant to the provisions  of paragraph 
(1), it shall be deemed that any unsettled securities trades or unsettled exchange-traded 
derivative trades of the member who has withdrawn its membership as a result of each 
of the merger, etc. have been carried out by the company succeeding the business as a 
result of each of the merger, etc. (Amended on January 28, 2009; April 29, 2020) 

(3) In cases where the Exchange approves the succession of status of a clearing member 
of  the  securities  market  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1),  it  may  notify  alternative  trading 
systems of the fact. [March 4, 2025] 

§14. Trading Suspension and Settlement of Unsettled Trades   

(1) In cases where a member has filed an application for voluntary withdrawal pursuant 
to [§10(1)] or any of the causes for obligatory membership withdrawn under [§11] has 
occurred, the Exchange shall suspend the relevant member from trading of securities or 
exchange-traded derivatives products from the following day. (Amended on January 28, 
2009; December 30, 2015; April 29, 2020) 

(2) In cases where a member has been suspended from trading securities or exchange-
traded  derivative  products  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1),  the  Exchange  shall  settle  the 
unsettled  trades  of  the  relevant  member  by  transferring  the  rights  and  obligations 
relating  to  such  unsettled  trades  to  another  member  or  by  making  other  arrangement 
which the Exchange deems it necessary. (Amended on January 28, 2009; December 30, 
2015) 

(3) In cases where the Exchange suspends trading of a clearing member of the securities 
market pursuant to paragraph (1), it may notify alternative trading systems of the fact. 
[March 4, 2025] 

§15. Refund and Takeover of Joint Compensation Fund   

(1) In cases where a member who has withdrawn its membership is a clearing member, 
the Exchange shall refund the amount that the relevant member contributed to the Joint 
Compensation Fund within twenty (20) days from the withdrawal date. Provided, That, 
in cases where a natural disaster, warfare, disturbance, sudden and significant change in 
economic condition, or other incidents similar thereto have occurred, the date of refund 
may be set differently, and where the withdrawn member owes an outstanding balance 
to the Exchange, the Exchange shall use the contribution of the withdrawn member to 
pay off the outstanding  balance first, and  refund the remaining  amount. (Amended on 
December 30, 2015; April 29, 2020) 

(2) In cases where a member has withdrawn its membership, the Exchange shall refund 
the  Fidelity  Guarantee  Money  of  the  withdrawn  member  within  twenty  (20)  from  the 

8 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
withdrawal  date.  In  such  cases,  the  proviso  to  paragraph  (1)  shall  apply  mutatis 
mutandis. (Amended on December 30, 2015) 

(3) In cases where the membership of a member has been taken over pursuant to [§13], 
notwithstanding paragraph (1), it shall be deemed that the Joint Compensation Fund of 
the  relevant  member  is  transferred  to  the  surviving  company,  the  company  newly 
incorporated  after  the  merger  or  the  company  taking  over  the  business.  (Amended  on 
December 30, 2015)

§18. Payment, Deposit and Refund of Conversion Fees, etc.   

(1) Any non-clearing member who has obtained an approval for membership conversion 
to a clearing member shall pay to or deposit with the Exchange the amounts specified in 
each  of  the  following  subparagraphs:  (Amended  on  January  28,  2009;  December  30, 
2015)

1. Conversion fee: The amount obtained by subtracting the admission fee for non-

(2) The Exchange shall refund the amount that a clearing member, who has obtained the

relevant clearing member within twenty (20) days from the conversion date. (Amended

(3) In the case of conversion between the membership categories pursuant to [§16(1)2], 
the balance of admission fee (excluding the refund) and the Basic Joint Compensation 
Fund  shall  be  paid  to  the  Exchange  or  the  Exchange  shall  refund  the  balance  to  the 
member.  [January  28,  2009]  (Amended  on  January  27,  2012;  December  30,  2015; 
March 4, 2025) 

§19. Conversion Date, Cancellation of Conversion Approval and Notification 
Thereof   

(1) Membership conversion shall become effective on the day (hereinafter referred to as 
“the conversion  date”) after a  member  who  has  obtained  an  approval  for  membership 
conversion has satisfied the criteria for membership conversion stipulated in the proviso 
to  [§16(3)]  and  all  procedures  for  membership  conversion  as  established  by  the 
Exchange have been completed, including the payment and deposit of conversion fee, 
etc.  pursuant  to  [§18(1)]  and  the  entry  into  an  agreement  for  settlement  entrustment 
(applied  only  to  the  case  of  converting  the  membership  into  non-clearing  member) 
pursuant to [§22]. (Amended on January 28, 2009) 

(2) In cases where a member who has obtained an approval for membership conversion 
has  failed  to  meet  the  conversion  criteria  or  failed  to  complete  the  conversion
