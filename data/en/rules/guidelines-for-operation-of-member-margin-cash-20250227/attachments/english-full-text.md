CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS 

§1. Purpose 

The  purpose  of  this  Guideline  is  to  stipulate  the  matters  necessary  for managing  the  cash 
deposited  by  the  members  with  the  Korea  Exchange  as  a  member  margin  in  accordance 
with[§41]  of  the  Enforcement  Rules  of  the  Securities  Market  Clearing  and  Settlement 
Business Regulation, [§102-2] of the Enforcement Rules of the Derivatives Market Business 
Regulation, and [§58] of the Enforcement Rules of the OTC Derivatives Clearing Business 
Regulation. (Amended on February 27, 2025) 

§2. Definitions 

(1) The terms used in this Guideline shall have the meanings as follows: 

1.  The  term  “member”  refers  to  the  clearing  member  pursuant  to  [§3(2)1]  of  the 
Membership  Regulation  and  the  clearing  member  pursuant  to  [§2(1)3]  of  the  OTC 
Derivatives Clearing Business Regulation. 

2.  The term “margin” in this Guideline refers to member margins pursuant to [§27(1)] 
of  the  Securities  Market  Clearing  and  Settlement  Business  Regulation,  Market 
Business  Regulation  and  [§88(1)]  of  the  Derivatives  Market  Business  Regulation, 
and  the  member  margin  pursuant  to  [§81(1)]  of  the  OTC  Derivatives  Clearing 
Business Regulation.  

3.  The term “margin cash” in this Guideline refers to cash (including foreign currencies) 
deposited by the members with the Korea Exchange (hereinafter referred to as “the 
Exchange”) as a margin noted in subparagraph 2. 

4.  The term “settlement bank” in this Guideline refers to a bank that opened an account 
in the name of the Exchange to receive the margin deposits pursuant to [§29(1)] of 
the Securities Market Clearing and Settlement Business Regulation and [§91(1)] of 
the Derivatives Market Business Regulation, and [§85(1)] of the OTC  Derivatives 
Clearing Business Regulation. 

5.  The term “general operation” refers to the operation of margin cash by the department 
(hereinafter referred to as “management department”) that conducts the clearing and 
settlement business of the Exchange. 

6.  The term “special operation” refers to the operation of margin cash by the department 
(hereinafter  referred  to  as  “operation  department”)  that  conducts  the  financial  and 
accounting business of the Exchange. 

7.  The  term  “CCP  risk  management  department”  refers  to  the  department  that  is  in 
charge  of  the  business  of  CCP  risk  management  and  responsible  for  the  matters 
concerning the suspension of special operation, etc.

specially provided in this Guideline, follow as stipulated in the Financial Investment Services 
and  Capital  Markets  Act  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the  Act”)  and  the  business-related 
regulations of the Exchange. 

CHAPTER II. OPERATION OF MARGIN CASH 

Section 1. General Provisions 

§3. Principles of Operation 

(1)  The  Exchange  shall  operate  the  margin  cash  separately  from  the  properties  of  the 
Exchange and the Joint Compensation Fund prescribed in the Articles 323-14 and 394 of the 
Act. 

(2) The margin cash shall be managed by general and special operation methods for each 
currency according to the classification of each of the following subparagraphs:  

1. Securities market member margin cash 

2. Derivatives market member margin cash; and 

3. OTC derivatives member margin cash. 

(3) The margin cash shall be managed in a way to secure the cashability and liquidity while 
considering profitability. 

(4) In the case of operating the margin cash by depositing with a bank or a securities finance 
company (hereinafter referred to as “deposit operation”), the amount deposited with a single 
settlement bank or securities finance company shall not exceed 50 percent of the total amount 
for  deposit  operation.  However,  this  is  not  the  case  where  the  OTC  derivatives  member 
margin cash noted in paragraph (2)3 is no more than KRW 50 billion for consecutive twenty 
(20)trading days. 

§4. Margin Operation Consultative Council 

(1) The management department shall establish the Margin Operation Consultative Council 
(hereinafter referred to as “the Council”) to secure objectivity and transparency of margin 
cash management and to collect member opinions. 

2 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(2) The Council shall be composed of the following members: 

1. One (1) head of the management department; 

2. One (1) team leader in charge of the financial and accounting business; and 

3. No more than six (6) persons appointed by the President of the Clearing and Settlement 
Division  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “President”)  among  officers  and  employees 
responsible for the margin deposit or financing-related business of members. In such case, 
at least one (1) person from the members with the recent top margin cash balance shall 
be appointed for each subparagraph of [§3(2)]. 

(3)The term of office of the Council member noted in paragraph (2)3 shall be two (2) years 
and may be extended one (1) year twice when his/her term of office is terminated. However, 
the term of office expires in the event of loss of the position in his/her company. 

(4)The chairperson of the Council shall be a member under paragraph (2)1. 

(5)The chairperson of the Council shall represent the Council, presides over the meetings, 
and oversees the work of the Council. 

(6)The team leader in charge of relevant tasks within the management department shall be 
the secretary to manage the affairs of the Council. 

(7)In principle, the meeting of the Council shall be held once a quarter, but if the chairperson 
deems it necessary, the chairperson shall convene it separately. 

(8)Other matters not stipulated in this Guideline in relation to the composition and operation 
of the Council shall be determined by the President.  

§5. General Operation Amount 

Section 2. General Operation 

(1)  The  general  operating  amount  shall  be  the  remaining  amount  excluding  the  amount 
determined  for  the  special  operation  from  the  margin  cash  by  a  member’s  property  and 
customer’s property of member by currency . (Amended on December 16, 2022)  

(2)  Notwithstanding  paragraph  (1),  if  necessary,  the  management  department  may 
temporarily operate profits, etc. from the special operation in the general operation method. 

§6. Methods of General Operation 

(1) The general operation shall be conducted by depositing the margin cash in time deposits 
with a maturity of six (6) months or less with settlement banks or securities finance companies. 

3 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(2) In the case of depositing the margin cash in time deposit products pursuant to paragraph 
(1), the management department shall select a management institution through a competitive 
bidding  after  receiving  deposit  proposals  on  interest  rates,  classification  of    member’s 
property and customer’s property, etc. from financial institutions. (Amended on December 
16, 2022) 

(3) The management department may transfer the margin cash deposited between settlement 
banks  when  it  is  necessary  to  transfer  between  settlement  banks  due  to  concerns  about 
liquidity or concentration of deposits of a specific operating institution for the management 
of margin cash. 

Section 3. Special Operation 

§7. Special Operation Amount 

(1)  The  amount  of  special  operation  shall  be  the  amount  determined  by  the  management 
department within the limit of the lesser of the following amounts after separating members’ 
assets  and  customers’  assets  by  each  currency  according  to  the  classification  of  each 
subparagraph of [§3(2)]:  (Amended on December 27, 2021) 

1. An amount equivalent to 80 percent of the smallest daily margin cash deposit for the 
preceding 6 months, counting from  the month immediately preceding the last month of 
each quarter; and 

2. An amount equivalent to 70 percent of the daily average margin cash deposit for the 
preceding six months, counting from the month immediately preceding the last month of 
each quarter. 

(2) In the case where the amount of the special operation limit in paragraph (1) is 80 percent 
or more but not  more than 120 percent of the special operation amount of the immediately 
preceding quarter, the special operation amount of the immediately preceding quarter may be 
maintained and managed. 

§8. Methods of Special Operation 

(1)  The  special  operation  shall  be  conducted  by  the  methods  in  each  of  the  following 
subparagraphs: (Amended on July 11, 2023) 

1. Purchasing government bonds, municipal bonds, or monetary stabilization bonds of the 
Bank of Korea under Article 69 of the Bank of Korea Act; 

2. [Deleted on July 11, 2023] 

4 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
3. Lending to or depositing in securities finance companies; and 

4. Depositing in settlement banks. 

(2) The amount operated by the methods under subparagraphs 1 of paragraph (1) shall not 
exceed 50 percent of the special operation amount pursuant to 7(1). (Amended on July 11, 
2023) 

(3) In the case of depositing the margin cash in time deposit products of settlement banks or 
securities finance companies, the special management department shall select a management 
institution through a competitive bidding after receiving deposit proposals on interest rates, 
segregation  between  members’  property  and  customers’  property,  etc.  from  financial 
institutions. (Amended on December 27, 2021) 

(4) The special management department may deposit a certain amount of money in financial 
instruments that allow deposits and withdrawals at any time for the purpose of paying and 
receiving  the  special  operation  amount  and  handling  of  profits  resulting  from  the  special 
operation. 

§9. Procedures of Special Operation 

(1) The management department shall notify the special operation department and the CCP 
risk management department of the special operation amount under [§7(1)]by no later than 
eight (8) trading days (referring to the business day in the case of the OTC member margins; 
the same hereinafter) before the last trading day of each quarter. However, if the liquidity of 
the margin cash is insufficient or is expected to be insufficient, the previously notified special 
operation amount may be changed and notified again. 

(2)  The  special  operation  department  shall  establish  an  operation  plan  that  includes  the 
instruments and methods for operation within the amount notified pursuant to paragraph (1) 
and  implement  it  in  consultation  with  the  management  department  and  the  CCP  risk 
management department. 

§10. Request of Suspension of Special Operation 

(1)  In  case  where  the  liquidity  of  properties  acquired  by  depositing  the  margin  cash  is 
insufficient or expected to be insufficient, the CCP risk management department may request 
the special operation department to suspend all or part of the special operation in consultation 
with the special operation department and the management department. 

(2) The special operation department shall suspend the special operation without delay when 
it receives a request for suspension pursuant to paragraph (1). In this case, expenses incurred 
due to the suspension of special operation shall be covered by the operating profits.

CHAPTER III. ALLOCATION OF PROFITS AND COLLECTION OF EXPENSES 

§11. Calculation of Profits 

(1) The operating profits shall be the profits received by the Exchange during the quarter for 
each  margin  cash  noted  in  [§3(2)]  based  on  the  accrual  date  of  profits.  In  such  case,  for 
discount bonds for which interests are paid in advance from among the operation instruments 
under [§8(1)1 through 3], the maturity date or the disposition date shall be deemed to be the 
accrual date of profits. (Amended on December 27, 2021) 

(2) Corporate tax or local income tax withheld from the operating profits shall be paid pro 
rata to each member when they are refunded. In such case, the provision of [§12] shall apply 
mutatis mutandis to the allocation of the refunded withholding tax amount. 

(3)  The  special  operation  department  shall  notify  the  management  department  of  the 
information  on  the  profits  accrued  from  the  special  operation  such  as  operation  period, 
amount and details of the withholding tax thereof, etc., along with the status of margin cash 
operation on a quarterly basis.  

(4)  The  special  operation  department  shall  deposit  the  operating  profits  in  the  general 
operation account designated by the management department. 

§12. Pro Rata Allocation of Profits 

(1)  The  operating  profits  pursuant  to  [§11(1)]  shall  be  allocated  pro  rata  to  each  member 
according  to  the  cumulative  amount  (cumulative  amount  of  daily  balance  of  margin  cash 
deposited) of a member during the concerned operating period. In such case, in the case of 
the operating profits accrued from OTC derivatives member margin cash, “member” shall be 
construed  as  “clearing  account”;  the  same  hereinafter  in  this  Article).  (Amended  on 
December 27, 2021) 

(2)  The  pro  rata  allocation  of  profits  foreach  member  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1)  shall  be 
conducted based on the cumulative amount calculated for each the member’s property and 
the customer’s property by currency according to each subparagraph of [§3(2)]. In such case, 
for the operating profits of customers’ properties in the OTC derivatives member margin cash, 
the cumulative amount shall be calculated for the pro rata payment for each customer. 

(3)  The  pro  rata  allocation  amount  of  each  member  shall  be  the  aggregate  of  the  amount 
calculated  by  multiplying  the  amount  of  operating  profits  per  operating  product  by  the 
cumulative  amount  ratio  of  each  member  (rounded  to  zero  decimal  places)  during  the 
concerned operating period. In such case, the cumulative amount ratio shall be obtained by 
dividing the cumulative amount pursuant to paragraph (2) by the total cumulative amount of 
all members (rounded off below the 15th decimal place). (Amended on December 27, 2021) 

6 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(4) In the event that the total amount of profits allocated pro rata to each member is not the 
same as the total amount of the operating profits, KRW 1 shall be deducted (or added) from 
(or to) the allocated profit of each member in turn, starting from the member with the largest 
allocation amount by the time when the two amounts become equal. (Amended on December 
27, 2021) 

§13. Payment of Profits and Notification thereof 

(1) The amount of profit paid for each member shall be the amount obtained by deducting the 
operating expenses pursuant to [§14] from the pro rata allocation profit calculated pursuant 
to [§12]. In such case, the profit payment shall be made by including the profit in the margin 
cash according to the classification of [§12(2)] within 20 days from the first day of the quarter 
following the accrual of profit. 

(2) The timing of including the profit in the margin cash pursuant to paragraph (1) may be 
adjusted for the convenience of members’ accounting works, etc. 

(3) The matters relating to pro rata allocation of profits, withholding tax, operating expenses, 
etc. shall be notified to the relevant members by putting them into the Member Derivative 
System Terminal or Member Derivative System through the Exchange Derivative System or 
using other methods.  

§14. Calculation of Operating Expenses 

(1) The operating expense for each member shall be the value (decimal points are rounded 
off) obtained by multiplying the amount of profit allocated pro rata for each member pursuant 
to [§12]by the application rate specified in [Annex 1]. Provided that, the application rate shall 
be zero (0) in the case of a currency, which is a margin cash under the general operation, 
operated only in financial instruments that allow the deposit and withdrawal at any time.  

(2) The President may reduce operating expenses (reduction rate shall be within 50%) or set 
a different application rate in [Annex 1] in consideration of market conditions and rate of 
return. 

§15. Calculation of Custody Expenses 

(1) The custody expenses shall be calculated for foreign currency margin cash to which a 
negative interest rate (referring to the interest  rate in the cases where the relevant deposit 
product for each currency is below zero (0) percent; the same hereinafter) applies. In such 
case, the date on which  the settlement bank applied a negative interest  rate to the foreign 
currency margin cash deposited shall be deemed the date of incurrence of the expenses.  

(2) The custody expenses shall be expenses incurred during the quarter and be an amount 
billed by the settlement bank. 

7 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(3) The amount of custody expenses shall be allocated pro rata for each of proprietary account 
and customer account of a member, and the amount of custody expense for each member 
shall  be  the  sum  of  daily  expenses  (rounded  down  to  decimal  places)  calculated  by 
multiplying the deposit balance on the date of expense incurrence by the application rate. In 
such case, the custody expenses incurred from the OTC derivatives member margin cash shall 
be allocated pro rata for each clearing account. 

(4) The application rate (rounded to the fourth decimal place) noted in paragraph (3) shall be 
determined in consultation with the settlement banks considering the negative interest rates 
of each foreign currency, deposit insurance premium, settlement bank’s OTC swap operation 
expenses, etc. 

§16. Collection of Custody Expenses and Notification thereof 

(1) The collection of custody expense pursuant to [§15(3)] shall be made by deducting from 
the foreign currency margin cash of each member according to the classification of [§12(2)] 
within 20 days from the first day of the quarter following the incurrence of custody expense. 

(2) The timing of deducting the custody expense from the margin cash pursuant to paragraph 
(1) may be adjusted for the convenience of settlement bank and members’ accounting works, 
etc. 

(3)  The  matters  relating  to  expense  collection  such  as  calculation  of  custody  expenses, 
deduction details and custody expense rates to apply for the next quarter, etc. shall be notified 
to the relevant member by putting them into the Member Derivatives System Terminal or 
Member  Derivatives  System  through  the  Exchange  Derivatives  System  or  using  other 
methods. 

§17. Settlement for Withdrawing Members, etc. 

(1) When a member withdraws its membership (including a case where a clearing member 
converts  into  a  non-clearing  member),  the  margin  cash  deposited  shall  be  returned  to  the 
member concerned without delay.  

(2) The operating profit accrued from the margin cash of a withdrawing member shall be paid 
according  to  the  period  stipulated  in  [§13(1)]  after  deducting  the  operating  expenses. 
However, in cases where it is impossible to make the payment of profits due to the extinction 
of corporate personality, etc., the profits shall be added to other members’ profits allocated 
pro rata. 

(3) In cases where the custody expense pursuant to [§15] incurs, it shall be collected on the 
date  of  withdrawal  or  conversion  of  the  membership  or  on  the  date  of  withdrawal  of  the 
relevant  member  margin  cash.  In  such  case,[§16]  shall  apply  mutatis  mutandis  to  the 
collection method. 

8 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(4) The profits and expenses of a member withdrawing due to a merger, spin-off, merger after 
spin-off, or business transfer (hereinafter referred to as “merger, etc.”) shall be succeeded by 
the legal entity that is newly established or survives after the merger, etc.

§2.  Exceptions  in  Applying  Provisions  Concerning  Determination  of  Special  Operation 
Amount 

(1) Notwithstanding the amended provision of [§7(1)], the special operation amount operated 
in accordance with the previous provision at the time when this Guideline becomes effective 
shall be deemed as the special operation amount under the previous provision until the end 
of the operation period of the concerned operation product. 

(2) In the case of determining the special operation amount pursuant to the amended provision 
of [§7(1)], after calculating the special operation amount in paragraph (1) pro rata according to

the members calculated pursuant to the same amended provision, the relevant amount shall be 
deducted from the special operation amount of the members’ assets and the customers’ assets, 
respectively.
