§1. Purpose 

The purpose of this Operational Guidelines on Fair Disclosure (hereinafter referred to as 
the  “Operational  Guidelines”)  is  to  stipulate  the  matters  necessary  for  the  effective 
operation of fair disclosure system pursuant to [§20] of the KOSPI Market Disclosure 
Regulation (hereinafter referred to as “the Regulation”) (Amended on February 3, 2009; 
April 19, 2012) 

§2. Definitions 

The  terms  used  in  this  Operational  Guidelines  shall  have  the  same  meanings  given  to 
them in the Regulation.   

§3. Information subject to Fair Disclosure 

(1)  The  “future  business  plans  or  management  plans”  in  [§15(1)1]  of  the  Regulation 
shall refer to the plans for a period up to the next three (3) years, which concern each of 
the  following  matters  that  have  substantial  influence  on  the  overall  business  activities 
and  performance  of  a  company.    In  this  case,  the  detailed  description  of  such  plans, 
including  the  objectives,  activity  schedule,  size  of  estimated  investment  and  expected 
outcome, shall be reported. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

1.  New businesses planned;   
2.  Development of new markets;       
3.  Change in main business areas; 
4.  Change in company structure;       
5.  Production of new products;         
6.  Strategic alliances with other domestic and overseas corporations;       
7.  Development of new technologies; and 
8.  Change in the existing businesses (suspension, shutting down, disposal, etc.) 

(2)  The  “estimates  of  or  forecast  on  sales,  operating  income  (losses),  profits  (losses) 
before  taxes  from  continuing  operations  or  net  income  (losses)”  in  [§15(1)2]  of  the 
Regulation shall refer to the estimations or forecasts about the sales, operating  income 
(losses), profits (losses) before taxes from continuing operations or net income (losses) 
for a period up to the next three (3) years.    In this case, the assumptions and basis of 
such  estimations  or  forecasts  shall  clearly  be  stated  in  detail.  (Amended  on  May  14, 
2007; February 3, 2009) 

(3) The “business performance such as sales, operating income (losses), profits (losses) 
before taxed from continuing operations or net income (losses) that are included in the 
annual report, semi-annual report and quarterly report (hereinafter referred to as “annual

reports, etc.”) that are released before submitting the annual reports, etc.” in [§15(1)3] 
of  the  Regulation  shall  refer  to  the  business  results,  including  sales,  operating  income 
(losses), profits (losses) before corporate tax from continuing operations, or net income 
(losses),  stated  in  the  annual  reports,  etc.  that  are  not  yet  submitted  at  the  time  of 
releasing  the  information  subject  to  fair  disclosure,  and  in  cases  of  releasing  the 
monthly business results, it shall refer to the pertinent fair disclosure information among 
the  monthly  results.    In  this  case,  the  comparable  values,  such  as  the  performance 
indicators  of  the  previous  quarter  or  the  same  quarter  of  the  previous  year,  shall  be 
mentioned,  and  if  the  concerned  period  is  longer  than  a  quarter,  the  sales,  operating 
income (losses), profits (losses) before taxes from continuing operations, or net income 
(losses) for the concerned period shall be reported alongside.    However, this provision 
shall not apply to the case of an unavoidable circumstance due to the delay in  closing 
the account of subsidiaries or special features in  the accounting standard of concerned 
industry. (Amended on May 14, 2007; February 3, 2009) 

(4) “From among the matters related to those set forth in Articles [§7] through [§11], the 
matters  for  which  the  reporting  deadlines  have  not  yet  passed”  in  [§15(1)4]  of  the 
Regulation shall refer to, from among the matters corresponding to the criteria specified 
in Articles [§7] through [§11] of the Regulation, the matters that are not yet come under 
the disclosure obligation because the board of directors has not made its decision, or the 
matters not disclosed in accordance with Articles [§7] through [§11] of the Regulation 
although they clearly come under the disclosure obligation. (Amended on December 27, 
2005; February 3, 2009; April 19, 2012) 

§4. Prohibition of Roundabout Provision of Information subject to Fair Disclosure 

Providers of fair disclosure information shall not provide the information subject to fair 
disclosure  in  a  roundabout  manner  by  using  various  ratios  and  scales  of  increases  or 
decreases, etc. 

§5. Fair Disclosure Information Providers 

(1) For the officers under [§15(2)2] of the Regulation, it shall be regardless whether or 
not they are registered or in full-time status; for the directors under the same provision, 
the outside directors shall be included; and,  for the auditors under the same provision, 
the members of audit committee shall be included. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(2) “Any persons who hold an equivalent position” in [§15(2)2] of the Regulation shall 
refer to the persons who direct other persons to operate the business pursuant to [§401-
2(1)] of the Commercial Act. 

(3) The “employees” in [§15(2)3] of the Regulation shall refer to the persons, regardless 

2 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
of their designations, who have entered into an employment contract that is subject to 
the Labor Standards Act. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(4)  “Staff-members  of  the  department  that  handles  the  matters  concerning  the 
information subject to fair disclosure” in [§15(2)3] of the Regulation shall refer to the 
staff-members  of  the  department  that  actually  makes  and  implements  the  decisions 
about the works relating to the information subject to fair disclosure, who are directly or 
indirectly  engaged  in  such  works;  and  “staff-members  of  the  department  that  is 
responsible  for  disclosure”  shall  refer  to  the  staff-members  of  the  department  that 
actually makes the decisions on the contents and time of disclosure, etc. and the staff-
members  who  are  directly  or  indirectly  engaged  in  the  works  relating  to  disclosure. 
(Amended on February 3, 2009) 

§6. Receivers of Faire Disclosure Information 

(1) The “officers” in [§15(3)] of the Regulation shall refer to the persons who fall under 
Paragraphs (1) and (2) of [§5]. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(2)  The  “employees”  in  [§15(3)]  of  the  Regulation  shall  refer  to  the  persons  who  fall 
under [§5(3)]. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(3)  The  “relationship  of  delegation”  in  [§15(3)1]  of  the  Regulation  shall  refer  to  an 
agreement,  regardless  of  its  title,  that  states  that  the  delegatee  handles  the  affairs 
entrusted  to  him/her  by  the  delegation  of  the  delegator  and  falls  under  any  of  the 
followings: (Amended on December 27, 2005; February 3, 2009; April 19, 2012) 

1.  To request a lawyer to perform a lawsuit as an attorney; 

2.  To request an investment trader or an investment broker under the Act to handle 
the  tasks  relating  to  the  preparation  and  submission  of  documents  such  as  the 
securities registration statement; or 

3.  To request an underwriter or an accounting firm for such tasks as analysis of the 
value of securities. 

(4) The “partnership” in [§15(3)1] of the Regulation shall means, regardless of its name, 
that  two  (2)  or  more  companies  jointly  run  or  cooperate  in  technology,  production, 
capital,  etc.  in  performing  the  business  activities  such  as  sales,  product  development, 
and investment, etc. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(5)  The  “foreign  professional  investors”  in  [§15(3)3]  of  the  Regulation  shall  refer  to, 
regardless of their names, the financial institutions, investment companies, hedge funds 
and  other  companies  performing  similar  functions,  which  operate  financial  businesses 

3 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
by  obtaining  the  permits  or  licenses  pursuant  to  the  financial  laws  and  ordinances  of 
foreign countries. (Amended on February 3, 2009)           

(6) The “domestic and foreign corporations that conduct the same or similar businesses” 
in [§15(3)4] of the Regulation shall refer to the domestic and foreign companies that are 
not  a  broadcasting  or  press  company  according  to  the  laws,  but  in  reality  provide  the 
same  or  similar  services  that  are  performed  by  the  broadcasting  companies  or  press 
agencies by employing such means as Internet. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(7)  The  “securities  information  sites”  in  [§15(3)5]  of  the  Regulation  shall  refer  to  the 
Internet  sites  that  are  mainly  concerned  with  the  provision  of  information  about  the 
transaction of securities or KOSPI-listed corporations. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(8) The “holders of the stocks of a KOSPI-listed corporation, who are expected to trade 
the  concerned  stocks  by  exploiting  the  information  subject  to  fair  disclosure”  in 
[§15(3)6]  of  the  Regulation  shall  refer  to  the  holders  of  the  securities  issued  by  the 
concerned  KOSPI-listed  corporation,  for  which  they  have  not  made  a  written 
declaration  not  to  buy  or  sell  the  stocks  on  the  basis  of  the  information  received. 
(Amended on February 3, 2009; April 19, 2012) 

(9)  The  “persons  who  are  specified  by  the  Exchange”  in  [§15(3)7]  of  the  Regulation 
shall refer to the persons who fall under any of the followings: (Amended on February 3, 
2009; June 29, 2017) 

1.  A  person  who  provides  advisory  services  with  respect  to  the  exercise  of  voting 
rights; or 

2.  In  cases  where  the  provider  of  fair  disclosure  information  intends  to  selectively 
provide  the  fair  disclosure  information  to  the  persons  noted  in  subparagraphs  1 
through  6  under  [§15(3)]  of  the  Regulation,  a  person  who  can  easily  access  the 
means of providing the information to and obtain it. 

§7. Notification Deadlines, etc. 

(1) When a KOSPI-listed corporation intends to release the information subject to fair 
disclosure by organizing investor relations seminars or other meetings, it shall notify the 
Exchange of its intention before the opening of the seminars or meetings.   

(2) Notwithstanding the notification deadline set forth in the main sentence of [§15(4)] 
of  the  Regulation,  KOSPI-listed  corporations  shall  make  an  effort  to  notify  the 
Exchange  at  least  ten  (10)  minutes  before  releasing  the  information  subject  to  fair 
disclosure of the concerned information. (Amended on February 3, 2009)

(3) “In cases where such information was released due to a minor negligence or error” 
in the proviso to [§15(4)] of the Regulation  shall refer to  the  cases where there was  a 
misunderstanding  on  the  part  of  the  fair  disclosure  information  provider  that  the 
Exchange has already been notified of the information to be released or the information 
to be released is not subject to fair disclosure. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(4) When a KOSPI-listed corporation makes the notification according to the proviso to 
[§15(4)]  of  the  Regulation,  such  notification  shall  be  made  under  the  name  of  the 
representative director (in the case of a corporation with the executive director system, it 
refers  to  the  chief  executive  officer)  of  the  concerned  KOSPI-listed  corporation.       
(Amended on February 3, 2009; April 19, 2012) 

(5)  In  cases  where  a  provider  of  fair  disclosure  information  releases  the  information 
subject  to  fair  disclosure  that  is  not  notified  to  the  Exchange  at  an  investor  relations 
seminar,  press  conference,  informal  discussion  with  press,  conference  call,  etc.,  the 
concerned KOSPI-listed corporation shall notify such fair disclosure information to the 
Exchange without delay. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(6) In cases where a provider of fair disclosure information has released the information 
subject  to  fair  disclosure  to  the  receivers  of  fair  disclosure  information  during  an 
informal  discussion,  etc.  held  before  the  market  opening  time,  the  concerned  KOSPI-
listed corporation shall notify the Exchange of such release by ten (10) minutes before 
opening of pre-hours session of the same day (in cases where the pre-hours session is 
not opened, by thirty (30) minutes before the opening of regular session). (Amended on 
December 27, 2005; February 3, 2009) 

(7) In cases where a KOSPI-listed corporation has become obligated to disclose the fair 
disclosure pursuant to Articles [§15] through [§17] of the Regulation, but cannot use the 
Exchange’s electronic disclosure system due to the closing of the system’s notification 
receiving time, the notification shall be made by ten (10) minutes before the opening of 
pre-hours  session  of  the  next  trading  day  (if  the  pre-hours  session  is  not  opened,  by 
thirty (30) minutes before the opening of the regular session). (Amended on December 
27, 2005; February 3, 2009) 

§8. Notification Methods 

(1) In cases where a KOSPI-listed corporation makes a notification pursuant to [§17(1)] 
of the Regulation,  the notification  shall be prepared in  accordance with  the applicable 
forms noted in Articles [§7] through [§11] of the Regulation. (Amended on December 
27, 2005; February 3, 2009; April 19, 2012) 

(2) KOSPI-listed corporations shall include the name of the disclosure officer and staff- 
member  responsible  for  disclosure,  as  well  as  the  departments  and  contact  numbers 

5 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
relating  to  the  concerned  information  subject  to  fair  disclosure,  in  the  notification,  so 
that the investors may inquire about the specifics relating to the disclosed information 
without difficulties. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(3) “Where the details to be notified are extensive” in [§17(2)] of the Regulation shall 
refer  to  the  cases  where  the  volume  of  the  documents  relating  to  the  reporting  of  the 
information subject to fair disclosure, including the information released at the business 
presentations, exceeds 3 mega bits or the Exchange deems it to be extensive. (Amended 
on February 3, 2009) 

(4)  In  cases  where  a  KOSPI-listed  corporation  reports  the  summarized  information 
pursuant  to  [§17(2)]  of  the  Regulation,  such  KOSPI-listed  company  shall  state  the 
methods  of  accessing  the  original  documents,  such  as  the  address  of  company 
homepage, in the summary document. (February 3, 2009; April 19, 2012) 

§9. Exceptions in Applying Fair Disclosure Obligations 

(1) The “coverage for news reporting” in subparagraph 1 under [§18] of the Regulation 
shall refer to a series of activities taken by an employee of a newspaper or broadcasting 
company,  which  generates  newsworthy  information  by  collecting  and  synthesizing 
information  gathered  through  a  personal  visit  to  KOSPI-listed  corporations  or  contact 
with  the  officers  or  employees  of  KOSPI-listed  corporations,  in  order  to  satisfy  the 
public’s right to know.    In this case, when a KOSPI-listed corporation makes available 
fair  disclosure  information  through  a  news  release,  press  conference,  press  gathering, 
conference call, etc., the exemptions in application of fair disclosure obligation shall not 
apply. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(2) The “persons who are required to keep confidentiality” and the “persons who have 
clearly stated to keep the confidentiality” in items a and b of subparagraph 2 under [§18] 
of the Regulation shall refer to the persons who are under the obligation of any of the 
following contracts: (Amended on February 3, 2009; April 19, 2012) 

1.  Contract authorizing law firms or lawyers, which are subject to the Attorneys-at-
Law  Act,  to  provide  the  legal  advices,  act  as  a  proxy  for  the  delegated  affair,  and 
handle the legal proceedings.       

2.  Contract authorizing certified public accountants, who are subject to the Certified 
Public  Accountant  Act,  to  provide  consulting  services  relating  to  external  audit, 
management  or  securities  business;  to  be  an  agent  for  tax  reconciliation  and 
accomplishment  of  tasks  relating  to  foreign  capital  inducement;  to  appraise  the 
business  worth  for  mergers  and  assess  the  assets  or  debts  for  commencement  of 
rehabilitation  procedures;  and  to  carry  out  the  tasks  of  managing  overseas 
corporations, etc.

3.  Contract  authorizing  certified  public  tax  accountants,  who  are  subject  to  the 
Certified  Public  Tax  Accountants  Act,  to  be  an  agent  for  filings,  applications  and 
claims  relating  to  taxation;  to  prepare  the  documents  relating  to  taxation  such  as  a 
statement  for  tax  reconciliation;  to  act  as  an  agent  for  tax  matters,  including 
consultations and advices on tax matters; etc.       

4.  Underwriting agreements entered into with underwriting syndicates, including the 
lead managing underwriter who assumes the task of underwriting (limited to the case 
where the confidentiality obligation is clearly stated in the underwriting agreement).       

5.  Loan agreements with financial institutions (limited to the case where the officers 
and  employees  of  the  concerned  financial  institution  that  have  explicitly  agreed  to 
keep  the  confidentiality  through  a  written  statement).    However,  in  case  of  an 
agreement  relating  to  loans  and  other  routine  matters  made  with  the  financial 
institutions  pursuant  to  the  Banking  Act,  a  written  statement  to  keep  the 
confidentiality is not required.   

6.  Agreements  for  implementation  of  the  business  normalization  plan  that  was 
arranged  between  a  corporation  and  its  creditor  financial  institutions  in  accordance 
with  the  Corporate  Restructuring  Promotion  Act  (limited  to  the  case  where  the 
council of creditor financial institutions or the principal creditor bank has agreed to 
keep the confidentiality through a clearly written statement).       

7. Other contracts entered into with persons who have certificates of qualification or 
licenses in specific fields for the fulfillment of tasks detailed in the contracts, which 
require specialized knowledge (limited to the case where, in the course of performing 
the delegated tasks, the persons are under the obligation to keep the confidentiality in 
accordance with the relevant laws and regulations).     

(3) In the event where the persons specified in each item of subparagraph 2 under [§18] 
of the Regulation take actions on behalf of a KOSPI-listed corporation as an agent, such 
person shall become a provider of fair disclosure information. (Amended on February 3, 
2009) 

§10. Relationship with Timely Disclosure 

(1) In the event where the matters which have already been disclosed pursuant to [§15] 
and [§16] of the Regulation fall under Articles [§7] through [§11] of the Regulation, the 
concerned  KOSPI-listed  corporation  shall  report  such  fact  in  accordance  with  the 
applicable  regulation.  (Amended  on  December  27,  2005;  February  3,  2009;  April  19, 
2012) 

7 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(2) In the case of Paragraph (1), when the details of the information already disclosed 
were changed, the fact that the contents of the fair disclosure made previously has been 
changed shall be stated the in the report that is made pursuant to Articles [§7] through 
[§11] of the Regulation. In such case, it shall be deemed that the report specified in [§45] 
of the Regulation has been made. (Amended on December 27, 2005; February 3, 2009; 
April 19, 2012) 

§11. Due Care and Other Duties of KOSPI-listed Corporations 

(1) KOSPI-listed corporations shall take due care not to make mistakes or omissions of 
material  information  with  respect  to  the  preparation  of  fair  disclosure  documents. 
(Amended on December 27, 2005; September 23, 2008; February 3, 3009) 

(2) In preparing disclosure documents, KOSPI-listed corporations shall use terminology 
that  facilitates  the  understanding  of  details  of  the  disclosure,  and  shall  not  use 
terminology that exaggerates or distorts the details. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

(3)  In  the  cases  other  than  those  coming  under  [§18]  of  the  Regulation,  KOSPI-listed 
corporations  shall  make  the  efforts  necessary  to  fulfill  the  fair  disclosure  obligations, 
including the in-house training for the fair disclosure information providers, so that the 
providers can make appropriate responses to the inquiries about the information subject 
to  fair  disclosure  made  by  the  receivers  of  fair  disclosure  information.  (Amended  on 
February 3, 2009) 

(4) In addition to Paragraphs (1) through (3), KOSPI-listed corporations shall faithfully 
fulfill  and  comply  with  the  requirements  and  requests  that  the  Exchange  makes  in 
relation to the fair disclosure. (Amended on February 3, 2009)

ADDENDA 
(January 27, 2005) 

§1. Effective Date 

This  Operational  Guidelines  shall  become  effective  on  the  day  when  the  Korea 
Exchange is incorporated.   

§2. Abolition of the former Operation Guidelines on Fair Disclosure 

The  Operational  Guidelines  on  Fair  Disclosure  of  the  Korea  Stock  Exchange  shall  be 
abolished on the effective date of this Operational Guidelines. 

§3. Interim Measures 

The actions taken in accordance with the Operational Guidelines on Fair Disclosure of 
the Korea Stock Exchange shall be deemed to be the actions taken in accordance with 
this Operational Guidelines.
