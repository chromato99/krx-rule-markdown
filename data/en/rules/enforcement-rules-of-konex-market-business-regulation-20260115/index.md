CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS 

§1. Purpose 

The purpose of these Enforcement Rules shall be to stipulate the matters delegated by and 
the  matters  necessary  for  the  enforcement  of  the  KONEX  Market  Business  Regulation 
(hereinafter referred to as "the Regulation"). 

§2. Definitions 

(1) The terms used in these Enforcement Rules shall have the same meanings given to them 
in the Regulation. 

(2) “Criterion specified in the Enforcement Rules” in [§2(13)] of the Regulation shall refer 
to the case where the price of different class of stocks of the day exceeds twice the price of 
common stocks concerned. 

CHAPTER II. OPERATION OF THE MARKET 

§3. Change of trading hours 

(1) “Case where it is stipulated in the Enforcement Rules as it is deemed necessary for market 
management” in [§4(3)] of the Regulation shall refer to any of the following cases:   

1. In the case of failures of the KONEX Market systems, member systems, and/or other 
computer  systems  that  are  known  to  perform  similar  functions  thereof  (hereinafter 
referred to as “the computer system failure”);   

2. In the case of the first trading day of the year; or 

3. In addition, other cases where the Exchange deems it necessary to change the trading 
hours.   

(2) In the case of falling under paragraph (1)1, the trading hours in regular session shall, in 
principle, be extended for the duration of computer system failure, but the market closing 
time may be extended to the time that the Exchange deems it necessary. Provided that, if the 
system is not fully recovered by the market closing time, it shall be deemed that the trading 

1 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
was closed at the time the system failure occurred.   

(3) Failure of the member systems mentioned in paragraph (1)1 shall refer to the case when 
the sum  of the trades  executed by the members  who are experiencing  a  computer system 
failure  [referring  to  the  cases  where  members  are  unable  to  transmit  quotations  to  the 
KONEX Market (hereinafter referred to as “Market”) through the member computer system, 
but excluding computer system failures that are limited to specific offices of the members or 
specific  regions;  the  same  hereinafter]  amounts  to  or  exceed  75%  of  the  total  trading 
volume(based on the total volume of trades executed during twelve (12) months prior to the 
last  day  of  the  month  preceding  the  month  to  which  the  day  on  which  the  system  failed 
belongs and the cases of admission of new members as well as the membership withdrawal 
shall take into account; the same hereinafter). Provided that, in the case of total volume of 
trades of the members with the occurrence of system failure being under 75% of the total 
volume  of  trades,  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1)1,  the  Exchange  may  regard  it  as  a  failure  of 
member system when the Exchange deems necessary considering market circumstances of 
each following subparagraphs: 

1. Case of total volume of trades of the member with the occurrence of system failure 
per member being over 75% of the total volume of trades in the KOSPI or KOSDAQ 
Market; and   

2.  Case  of  normal  market  operation  being  troubled  due  to  the  occurrence  of  member 
system failure.   

§4. Designation of Trading Types 

“Case stipulated in the Enforcement Rules” in [§8(4)] of the Regulation shall refer to the case 
of trading in any of the following subparagraphs: (Amended on October 30, 2014) 

1. As a stock trade conducted pursuant to [§14(4)1 & 2] of the Regulation, when both the 
bid and offer quotations are submitted by a single member (in the case of member who 
received multiple member IDs pursuant to [§7(3)], it shall be limited to the quotations 
placed  under  the  same  member  ID;  the  same  hereinafter  in  [§33(2)])  and  the  relevant 
member has submitted the application for cash settlement to the Exchange in accordance 
with the [Annex: Form No. 10-2] three (3) days before placing the quotations, the trade 
the Exchange deems it necessary; and 

2.  The  trade  according  to  buy-in  by  the  Exchange  pursuant  to  [§22]  of  the  Securities 
Market Clearing and Settlement Business Regulations. (Amended on February 27, 2025)

§42. Methods of Correcting Transaction Errors 

(1)  The  Exchange  shall  correct  the  transaction  errors  according  to  each  of  the  following

transaction.   

2. For transaction errors made by members:

errors  are  corrected  after  the  relevant  member  take  over  the  error  transaction  as  a 
proprietary  account  transaction,  and  in  cases  of  the  errors  relating  to  classification  of 
customer account transaction and proprietary account transaction resulted from the failure 
in  the  operation  of  a  program  of  the  member  system,  the  correction  shall  be  made  as 
required.   

(2) When taking over an error transaction as a proprietary account transaction pursuant to 
paragraph  (1),  in  the  case  of  a  member  who  have  not  obtained  the  permit  for  investment 
dealing  business  in  Article  6(1)1  of  the  Act,  it  shall  be  accomplished  by  way  of  such 
member’s entrusting the trade to another member as a proprietary account transaction.   

(3) In the case of transaction errors made by a member, the correction shall be made only in 
cases where the relevant member has submitted a request for correction of transaction errors

occurred. (Amended on June 21, 2016)

Method of Calculating Base Price of Issus gone Ex-dividend and Ex-right   
(Pertaining to [§25(1)6] of the Enforcement Rules) 

(1) Case of ex-dividends 

1. Case of paying dividend with the stocks whose class is the same as the class of already 
listed stocks   

a. Base price of common stocks = (Closing price of cum dividend common stocks × 
Number  of  common  stocks  before  dividends)  /  (Number  of  common  stocks  before 
dividends + Number of common stocks allotted to shareholders of common stocks)   

b. Base price of preferred stocks = (Closing price of cum dividend preferred stocks × 
Number  of  preferred  stocks  before  dividends)  /  (Number  of  preferred  stocks  before 
dividends + Number of preferred stocks paid as dividends to shareholders of preferred 
stocks) 

2. Case of paying dividend with either the common stocks or different classes of stocks 
to the already listed common stocks and different classes of stocks 

a. Case of paying dividend with common stocks   

i. Base price of common stocks = (Closing price of cum dividend common stocks × 
Number of common stocks before dividend payment) / (Number of common stocks 
before  dividend  payment  +  Number  of  common  shares  paid  as  dividends  to 
shareholders of common stocks)   

ii. Base price of different classes of stock = [(Closing price of cum dividend different 
classes of stocks × Number of different classes of stocks before dividends) - (Base 
price  of  common  stocks  ×  Number  of  common  shares  paid  as  dividends  to  the 
shareholders of different classes of stocks)] / Number of different classes of stocks 
before dividends 

b. Case of paying dividend with different classes of stocks 

i. Base price of common stocks = [(Closing price of cum dividend common stocks × 
Number of common stocks before dividend) - (Issuing price of different classes of 
stocks×  Number  of  different  classes  of  shares  paid  as  dividend  to  shareholders  of 
common stocks)] / Number of common stocks before dividends

of different classes of stocks before dividend + Number of different classes of stocks 
paid as dividends to shareholders of different classes of stocks)   

(2) Case of ex-rights

b. Base price of different classes of stock = [(Closing price of cum rights different 
classes of stocks  × Number of  different  classes of stocks before  rights  offering) + 
(Issuing price of different classes of stocks× Number of different classes of stocks 
allotted to shareholders of different classes of stocks)] / (Number of different classes 
of stocks before rights offering + Number of different classes of stocks allotted to 
shareholders of different classes of stocks)

Agreement on Correction of Transaction Errors 

The Korea Exchange (hereinafter referred to as "the Exchange") and xxx Securities Company, 
Ltd. (hereinafter referred to as "the Company"), who is a member of the Exchange agree on 
the following terms and conditions for the correction of transaction errors occurred in the 
course of execution of trades on the KONEX Market by the Exchange and the errors occurred 
due to the failures of the Exchange computer system (hereinafter referred to as "transaction 
errors").   

Article  1.  In  cases  where  any  transaction  error  has  occurred  during  the  course  of  trade 
execution  by  the  Exchange,  the  Company  shall  assume  products  traded  by  such  error 
transaction as products in proprietary account transaction upon receiving the request from 
the  Exchange  in  accordance  with  [Annex:  Form  No.7  Written  Request  for  Correction  of 
Transaction Errors]. 

Article 2. The Company shall trade the products assumed upon receipt of the request from 
the  Exchange  by  way  of  offsetting  transaction  whose  quotations  participate  in  the 
determination  of  the  opening  price  on  the  following  day.  Provided  that,  when  placing 
quotations participating in the determination of the opening price was not  possible due to 
unavoidable reasons such as the failure of the Exchange computer system, the products shall 
be traded by way of offsetting transaction whose quotations participate in the determination 
of  the  closing  price.  The  results  of  such  offsetting  transaction  shall  be  reported  to  the 
Exchange in accordance with [Annex: Form No. 8 Notification of Correction of Transaction 
Errors] without delay. Provided that, in cases where the assumed products are not cleared by 
the offsetting transaction on the following day, the remaining products shall be traded in the 
same manner on the next trading day.   

Article 3. The Exchange shall settle the profits and losses incurred to the Company (includes 
transaction  tax,  all  expenses  incidental  to  such  transactions  and  the  dues  of  concerned 
member; the same hereinafter) resulting from the assuming and trading the products acquired 
by the transaction error according to the request by the Company in accordance with [Annex: 
Form No.9 Request for Settlement for Handling Transaction Errors] without delay. Provided 
that  any  profits  and  losses  resulted  from  the  negligence  of  the  Company  in  the  course  of 
handling  the  assumed  products  shall  be  settled  ex  post  according  to  the  request  by  the 
Exchange in accordance with [Annex: Form No. 10 Request for Settlement Adjustment for 
Handling Transaction Errors].   

Article 4. If there is any difference of opinions between the Exchange and the Company in 
settling the profits or losses incurred, regarding the method of settling profits and loss when
