Regulations on the Market Operation of Class X Beneficiary Certificates of 
a Multiple Class Fund 

Chapter I. General Provisions 

§1. Purpose 

The  purpose  of  this  Regulation  is  to  stipulate  matters  necessary  for  the  listing  review, 
management  of  listed  securities,  reporting  and  disclosure  of  listed  entities,  and  trading  of 
securities in the market for class X beneficiary certificates of a multiple class fund, which is 
established  and  operated  by  the  Korea  Exchange  after  receiving  the  designation  as  an 
innovative financial service from the Financial Services Commission pursuant to the Special 
Act on Support for Financial Innovation. 

§2. Opening of Market 

(1) The Korea Exchange (hereinafter referred to as the “Exchange”) shall establish the market 
for  class  X  beneficiary  certificates  of  a  multiple  class  fund  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  the 
“market  for  class  X  beneficiary  certificates  of  publicly  offered  funds”)  within  the  KOSPI 
Market established pursuant to Article 386 of the Financial Investment Services and Capital 
Markets Act (hereinafter referred to as the “Act”). 

(2)  The  Exchange  shall  operate  the  market  for  class  X  beneficiary  certificates  of  publicly 
offered funds for the period (hereinafter referred to as the “designation period”) during which 
the  market  is  designated  as  an  innovative  financial  service  pursuant  to  the  Special  Act  on 
Support for Financial Innovation (hereinafter referred to as the “Financial Innovation Act”). 

(3) The Exchange may terminate the operation of the market for class X beneficiary certificates 
of publicly offered funds upon expiration of the designation period. In this case, the Exchange 
shall provide prior notice of the termination and take necessary measures, such as delisting, etc. 

§3. Definitions   

(1) The terms used in this Regulation shall be defined as follows: 

1.  “Class X beneficiary certificates” refers to a type of beneficiary certificates (hereinafter 
referred to as “class X beneficiary certificate funds”) that is listed and traded on the 
market for class X beneficiary certificates of publicly offered funds, among beneficiary 
certificates issued by a multiple class fund established under Article 231 of the Act by 
a fund management company, who obtained the designation as an innovative financial 
service for the listing and trading of class X beneficiary certificates of a multiple class 
fund under the Financial Innovation Act. 

2.  “Authorized participant” refers to a person designated as an innovative financial service 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
provider for the listing and trading of class X beneficiary certificates in accordance with 
the Financial Innovation Act and designated by a fund management company for the 
purpose of performing the duties specified in each subparagraph of Article 247 of the 
Enforcement Decree of the Act. 

3.  “Liquidity providing member” refers to a member who can submit liquidity providing 

quotations in accordance with [§17(2)]. 

(2) The terms, other than those specified in paragraph (1), used in this Regulation shall be in   
accordance with  the  relevant  laws  and  regulations,  the  “KOPI  Market  Listing  Regulations” 
(hereinafter referred to as the “Listing Regulations”), the “KOSPI Market Business Regulations” 
(hereinafter referred to as the “Business Regulations”), and the “Membership Regulations.” 

Chapter II. Listing of Class X Beneficiary Certificates of a Multiple Class Fund 

§4. General Operation of Listing Class X Beneficiary Certificates 

(1)  With  respect  to  listing  by  application,  prohibition  of  partial  listings,  delisting  without 
application for delisting, and documents for listing, etc. of class X beneficiary certificates, [§4], 
[§6], [§8], and [§10] of the Listing Regulations shall apply mutatis mutandis. 

(2) A listing applicant (excluding applicants with securities already listed on the market for 
class X beneficiary certificates of publicly offered funds or the market specified in [§4(1)2] of 
the  Business  Regulations)  wishing  to  list  class  X  beneficiary  certificates  shall  enter  into  a 
listing agreement with the Exchange in the format prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. In 
such cases, [§16(2)] of the Listing Regulations shall apply mutatis mutandis. 

§5. Preliminary Listing Review 

(1) The initial listing applicant of class X beneficiary certificates shall submit an application 
for  preliminary  listing  review,  together  with  the  documents  prescribed  by  the  Enforcement 
Rules to the Exchange and undergo a preliminary listing review before applying for an initial 
listing in accordance with [§6]. 

(2)  The  initial  listing  applicant  of  class  X  beneficiary  certificates  shall  consult  with  the 
Exchange  in  advance  regarding  the  procedures  and  timing  of  listing  before  applying  for 
preliminary listing review. 

(3)  The  Exchange  shall  examine  the  application  for  preliminary  listing  review  and  the 
documents  submitted  by  the  initial  listing  applicant  in  accordance  with  paragraph  (1)  for 
preliminary listing review pursuant to paragraphs (2) and (3) of [§6]. 

(4) The Exchange shall notify, in writing, the initial listing applicant of the relevant class X 
beneficiary certificates of the result of the preliminary listing review within twenty (20) days 
(based on business days) from the date of having received the application for preliminary listing 
review  noted  in  paragraph  (1).  Provided  that,  in  case  where  it  is  necessary  to  correct  or 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
supplement  the  application  for  preliminary  listing  review,  etc.  or  an  additional  review  is 
necessary,  the  Exchange  may  extend  the  notification  period  by  notifying  the  initial  listing 
applicant of the reasons and estimated processing time. 

(5)  In  the  case  where,  after  having  notified  the  initial  listing  applicant  of  the  result  of  the 
preliminary listing review pursuant to paragraph (4), any of the following incidents occurs with 
respect to the initial listing applicant and it  is recognized that the incident  has a significant 
impact  on  the  result  of  the  review,  the  Exchange  may  not  recognize  the  validity  of  the 
preliminary listing review result: 

1. Where matters significant to business operations have occurred; 

2.  Where  it  has  been  identified  that  important  matters  on  investor  protection  are  falsely 
stated in or omitted from the application for preliminary listing review; or 

3. Where an application for initial listing of class X beneficiary certificates has not been 
submitted within three (3) months  from the date of having been notified of the result of 
preliminary listing review. 

(6) The Exchange shall, when it decides not to recognize the validity of the result of preliminary 
listing review pursuant to paragraph (5), notify the initial listing applicant of this fact in writing. 

(7) In the case where the initial listing applicant submits a written statement to the Exchange 
setting forth the reasons for withdrawal of the application for preliminary listing review, the 
Exchange shall terminate the preliminary listing review. 

§6. Initial Listing 

(1)  In  cases  where  the  initial  listing  applicant  of  class  X  beneficiary  certificates  intends  to 
initially  list  class  X  beneficiary  certificates  after  passing  the  preliminary  listing  review 
conducted  by  the  Exchange,  the  applicant  shall  submit  to  the  Exchange  the  application  for 
initial listing and supplementary documents prescribed by the Enforcement Rules within three 
(3) months from the date of having received the result of preliminary listing review. Provided 
that,  if  the  initial  listing  applicant  requests  an  extension  of  the  submission  deadline  due  to 
unavoidable reasons such as a sudden change in market conditions and the Exchange approves 
the request, the submission deadline may be extended once for up to three (3) months.   

(2) To list class X beneficiary certificates, all of the following review requirements shall be 
met: 

    1. Size of the class X beneficiary certificate funds: The trust principal or total net assets of 
class X beneficiary certificate funds to be listed must be at least KRW 7 billion, and the 
total number of class X beneficiary certificates to be issued or converted must be at least 
1 billion units; 

    2. Size of the multiple class fund: The trust principal or total net assets of the multiple class 
fund that has issued class X beneficiary certificates to be listed must be at least KRW 50 
billion. Provided that this may not apply to cases specified in the Enforcement Rules.

(3) The Exchange shall comprehensively consider the following factors to determine whether 
it is appropriate to list class X beneficiary certificates that meet the requirements of paragraph 
(2); 

1. The product structure, etc. must be legal under relevant laws and regulations; 

2.  The  continuity  of  class  X  beneficiary  certificates  must  be  recognized  in  light  of  the 

efficient operation of included assets, etc.; 

3. The initial listing applicant must demonstrate transparency in its internal control system 

and disclosure system related to the operation of class X beneficiary certificates; 

    4. Investor protection measures must be included in the fund agreement, etc., in preparation 

for delisting pursuant to subparagraphs 6 through 8 of [§9]; and 

    5.  In  addition,  the  listing  of  class  X  beneficiary  certificates  must  be  recognized  as  not

(4) After the review pursuant to paragraphs (2) and (3) is completed, the Exchange shall notify 
the initial listing applicant of whether or not the initial listing has been approved. 

(5) In cases where, as a result of the review pursuant to paragraphs (2) and (3), it is determined 
that the listing of the relevant class X beneficiary certificate is not appropriate for the realization 
of public interest and investor protection, the Exchange may refuse to list them. 

(6) Matters necessary for the submission of documents pursuant to paragraph (1) and the listing 
review pursuant to paragraph (3) shall be prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

§7. Supplementary Listing and Listing Change 

(1) If a listed entity wishes to issue new class X beneficiary certificates identical to those of an 
already listed issue, it shall submit an application for supplementary listing and accompanying 
documents  stipulated  by  the  Enforcement  Rules  to  the  Exchange  without  delay  after  the 
registration of securities issuance becomes effective. 

(2) In cases of changes in the name or quantity of the relevant class X beneficiary certificates 
(limited  to  changes  in  quantity  due  to  redemption),  the  listed  entity  shall  submit  to  the 
Exchange,  without  delay,  an  application  for  listing  change  and  accompanying  documents 
stipulated by the Enforcement Rules after the grounds for the listing change become effective. 

(3) Notwithstanding paragraphs (1) and (2), in the case where a listed entity wishes to 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
make a supplementary listing or a listing change due to a change in quantity, it shall submit the 
relevant application to the Exchange by the trading day immediately before the scheduled date 
of listing. 

§8. Designation of Administrative Issues 

(1) The Exchange shall, where a class X beneficiary certificate falls under any of the following 
subparagraphs, designate the relevant certificate as an administrative issue: 

1. Where the total trust principal amount as of the end of the first half of a class X beneficiary 
certificate (excluding securities that have not passed one (1) year from the listing date) is 
less than KRW 5 billion and the total net assets (the amount calculated by subtracting the 
total liabilities from the total assets of the class X beneficiary certificate fund; the same 
shall apply hereinafter) is less than KRW 5 billion; 

2. Where a listed entity receives a corrective order from the Financial Services Commission 

pursuant to Article 7 of the Financial Innovation Act; 

3.  Where  a  listed  entity  requests  the  Financial  Services  Commission  to  revoke  the 
designation  as  an  innovative  financial  service  pursuant  to  Article  8  of  the  Financial 
Innovation Act; 

4.  Where  a  listed  entity  receives  an  order  from  the  Financial  Services  Commission  to 
suspend innovative financial services pursuant to Article 11 of the Financial Innovation 
Act; or 

5.  Other  cases  where  the  Exchange  deems  that  designation  as  an  administrative  issue  is 

necessary for the sake of public interest and investor protection. 

(2) The Exchange shall, where any of the following cases apply after a class X beneficiary 
certificate  has  been  designated  as  an  administrative  issue  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1),  revoke 
such designation without delay: 

    1. In the case of paragraph (1)1: If the relevant ground has been resolved as of the end of the 
semi-annual period to which the date of designation of administrative issue belongs; 

2. In the case of paragraph (1)2: If the Exchange recognizes that the relevant corrective order 

has been complied with; 

3. In the case of paragraph (1)3: If it has been confirmed that the relevant withdrawal request 

has been canceled; 

4. In the case of paragraph (1)4: If it has been confirmed that the resumption of services has 
been permitted pursuant to Article 11, Paragraph 2 of the Financial Innovation Act; or 

5.  In  the  case  of  paragraph  (1)5:  If  it  has  been  confirmed  that  the  relevant  ground  for 
designating a class X beneficiary certificate as an administrative issue has been resolved. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(3) Necessary matters regarding the timing of designation and revocation of an administrative 
issue pursuant to paragraphs (1) and (2) shall be prescribed by Enforcement Rules. 

§9 Delisting 

In cases where a class X beneficiary certificate falls under any of the following subparagraphs, 
the Exchange shall delist the relevant certificate: 

1. Where the requirements of [§6(2)3(b)] are not met; 

2. Where all liquidity providing members of the relevant class X beneficiary certificate fail 
to  enter  into  a  liquidity  providing  agreement  with  another  liquidity  providing  member 
pursuant  to  of  [§6(2)3(b)]  within  one  (1)  month  from  the  date  on  which  the  liquidity 
providing member replacement criteria are met; 

3.  Where  the  class  X  beneficiary  certificate  falls  under  [§8(1)1]  and  is  designated  as  an 
administrative issue, the grounds for such designation persist as of the next semi-annual 
end; 

4.  Where  the  reporting  obligations  under  [§14]  are  intentionally,  grossly  negligently,  or 

habitually violated; 

5.  Where  the  grounds  for  termination  of  an  investment  trust  pursuant  to  Article  192, 

Paragraph 1 or 2 of the Act are met; 

6.  Where  the  designation  of  an  innovative  financial  service  of  a  listed  entity  is  revoked 

pursuant to Article 7 of the Financial Innovation Act; 

7. Where the designation period for an innovative financial service of a listed entity expires 

pursuant to Article 9 of the Financial Innovation Act; 

8. Where the operation of the market for class X beneficiary certificates of publicly offered 

funds is terminated; or 

9.  Other  cases  where  the  Exchange  recognizes  that  delisting  the  class  X  beneficiary 
certificate  is  necessary  for  the  purpose  of  realizing  the  public  interest  and  investor 
protection. 

§10. Suspension of Trading and Resumption Thereof 

(1)  In  cases  where  a  listed  entity  or  a  listed  security  falls  under  any  of  the  following 
subparagraphs, the Exchange may suspend the trading of the relevant security: 

    1. Where the grounds for delisting apply pursuant to each subparagraph of [§9]; or 

    2. Other cases where the Exchange deems it necessary for the public interest and investor 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
protection. 

(2) The period of trading suspension pursuant to paragraph (1) shall be until the date on which 
the grounds for the trading suspension are recognized as resolved. 

§11. Prior Notice and Public Announcement on Administrative Issues 

(1) In cases where a listed security is concerned to be designated as an administrative issue, the 
Exchange may make public notice on the fact thereof. 

(2)  In  the  case  of  designating  a  listed  security  as  an  administrative  issue  or  revoking  such 
designation,  the  Exchange  shall  officially  announce  the  details  thereof  via  electronic 
information delivery mechanism. 

(3) With respect to a listed security that is an administrative issue, the Exchange may assess its 
status for the listing management such as disclosure for investor protection and shortening of 
the delisting period. 

§12. Prior Notice and Public Announcement on Delisting 

(1)  In  cases  where  a  listed  security  falls  under  any  of  the  following  subparagraphs,  the 
Exchange may give prior notice that the listed security may fall under the grounds for delisting 
until such security actually falls under the grounds for delisting: 

    1. Where the conditions for liquidity provider replacement are met; 

    2. Where delisting is expected due to the grounds for termination of the investment trust 

under Article 223, Paragraph 3 or 4 of the Enforcement Decree of the Act; or 

    3. Where the fund agreement specifies the fund’s duration, and one (1) month has passed 

retroactively from the expiration date of such duration. 

(2)  In  cases  where  a  listed  security  falls  under  the  grounds  for  delisting  pursuant  to  this 
Regulation,  the  Exchange  shall  inform  the  relevant  entity  of  such  fact,  the  methods  and 
procedures for delisting, and other necessary matters in writing without delay. 

(3) When the Exchange intends to delist a listed security, it may make an official announcement 
of such delisting. 

(4) With respect to the prior notice on delisting noted in paragraph (1), in the case of having 
confirmed through objective materials that the grounds for such prior notice were resolved, the 
Exchange may terminate such prior notice from the date of confirmation of the relevant fact. 

§13. Public Announcement on Noncompliance with Obligations 

In cases where a listed entity fails to fulfill its obligations or to submit documents under this 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Regulation, the Exchange may officially announce such fact.   

Chapter III. Reporting and Disclosure of Listed Entities with Class X Beneficiary 
Certificates of a Multiple Class Fund 

§14. Reporting Obligations, etc. 

(1) In the event where the cause falling under any of the following subparagraphs has occurred, 
a listed entity shall report the details thereof to the Exchange by the deadline specified in each 
subparagraph: 

    1. Distribution of profits: Fifteen (15) days before the end of the trust accounting period; 

    2. Net asset value and details of asset composition prescribed in the Enforcement Rules: On 

a daily basis 

    3. Matters on included assets specified in the Enforcement Rules; Within one (1) month from 

the last day of each month; 

4. In cases where an application for delisting is expected due to the grounds for delisting of 
an investment trust under Subparagraph 3 or 4 of Article 223 of the Enforcement Decree 
of the Act: One (1) month prior to the scheduled date for delisting; 

5. In cases where the duration is stipulated in the fund agreement and delisting is scheduled 
as  the  expiration  of  such  duration  constitutes  the  grounds  for  the  termination  of  the 
investment trust: One (1) month prior to the scheduled date for delisting; 

    6. In any of the following cases: Without delay 

        (a)  Where  a  corrective  order  has  been  issued  by  the  Financial  Services  Commission 
pursuant to Article 7 of the Financial Innovation Act or such order has been complied 
with; 

        (b) Where a request for withdrawal of designation as an innovative financial service has 
been  submitted  to  the  Financial  Services  Commission  pursuant  to  Article  8  of  the 
Financial Innovation Act or such request has been canceled; 

        (c) Where an order  to suspend innovative financial services or a permission to resume 
such  services  has  been  issued  by  the  Financial  Services  Commission  pursuant  to 
Article 11 of the Financial Innovation Act; or 

        (d)  Where  designation  as  an  innovative  financial  service  of  a  listed  entity  has  been 

canceled pursuant to Article 7 of the Financial Innovation Act. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
    7. Where the period of designation as an innovative financial service of a listed entity expires 
pursuant to Article 9 of the Financial Innovation Act: One (1) month prior to the expiration 
date of the designated period; 

    8. In any other cases prescribed by the Enforcement Rules: Without delay, as cases where 

the Exchange deems such action necessary for listing management. 

(2) A listed entity shall submit business reports and financial statements to the Exchange by 
the statutory submission deadline. 

(3)  A  listed  entity  shall  appoint  employees  responsible  for  the  reporting  and  document 
submission  tasks  prescribed  by  paragraphs  (1)  and  (2),  and  designate  a  responsible  person 
among  these  employees.  In  such  cases,  when  designating  or  changing  such  employees  or 
responsible person, the relevant listed entity shall notify the Exchange of the details. 

Chapter IV. Market Operation, Trading, Clearing and Settlement, etc. 

§15. Application of Regulation 

(1)  Except  as  otherwise  provided  in  this  Regulation  and  Enforcement  Rules,  regulations 
applicable to the market for ETF funds and ETF funds, among the Business Regulation and the 
Securities Market Clearing and Settlement Business Regulation (hereinafter referred to as the 
“Clearing and Settlement Business Regulation”) (including the Enforcement Rules and related 
guidelines for each regulation), shall apply to the market for class X beneficiary certificates of 
publicly  offered  funds  and  class  X  beneficiary  certificates.  Provided  that  [§87(2)]  of  the 
Business Regulation shall not apply to class X beneficiary certificates.   

(2) Class X beneficiary certificates shall not be considered substitute securities under [§88] of 
the Business Regulation. 

§16. Limits on Quotation Prices 

(1) The quotation prices of class X beneficiary certificates shall neither be higher than the price 
obtained by adding the price change limit (hereinafter referred to as “upper limit price”) to the 
reference price (referring to the price used as the basis for determining the price change limit; 
the same hereinafter) determined by in the Enforcement Rules nor lower than the price obtained 
by subtracting the price change limit from the reference price (hereinafter referred to as “lower 
limit price). Provided that, if the price obtained by adding or subtracting the price limit from 
the reference price is not appropriate as the upper or lower limit price, the upper or lower limit 
price may be determined differently by the Enforcement Rules. 

(2) The price change limits noted in paragraph (1) shall be equal to the amount calculated by 
multiplying the reference price by 0.3 (Any amount less than the tick size of the reference price 
shall be disregarded; the same hereinafter in this Article). Provided, however, that where the 
obtained amount is less than the minimum tick size, it shall be set at the minimum tick size. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(3) In the cases prescribed by the Enforcement Rules, notwithstanding paragraph (2), the price 
change limits may be determined differently. 

§17. Liquidity Providing Members 

(1) A member, who has entered into an agreement for liquidity provision with a listed entity, 
may submit quotations for liquidity provision (hereinafter referred to as the “liquidity providing 
quotations”) for the relevant issues during the regular session. 

(2) The members who are eligible for submission of liquidity providing quotations pursuant to 
paragraph (1) shall meet each of the following requirements: 

1.  Be a clearing member authorized to conduct investment trading business and qualify as 
an authorized participant under Article 247 of the Enforcement Decree of the Act; 

2. Appoint an employee in charge of liquidity provision; 

3. In any of the following cases, at least one (1) year shall have elapsed since the occurrence 

of the relevant incident: 

    (a) Where the results of assessments carried out pursuant to [§20] show the lowest grade 

for three (3) consecutive times; or 

    (b) Where it is confirmed that the member has been subject to a criminal sanction or any 
measures equivalent to or heavier than business suspension or trading suspension for 
violating relevant securities-related statutes or the business regulations of the Exchange 
in the course of performing the tasks of liquidity provision. 

(3) In cases where a member fails to meet the requirements specified in paragraph (2)1, such 
member shall be prohibited from submitting liquidity providing quotations from the time of 
such failure, and in cases where a member fails to meet the conditions prescribed by paragraphs

§18. Obligation to Submit Liquidity Providing Quotations 

(1) In cases where the bid-ask spread or bid-ask spread ratio based on the best quotation price 
(referring to the lowest ask price and the highest bid price among quotations other than mid-
price quotations; the same hereinafter) during the trading hours of the regular session exceeds 
the limit specified in each of the following subparagraphs (including cases where there is no 
bid or ask quotation on one or both sides), liquidity providing members shall submit liquidity 
providing quotations within five (5) minutes of such occurrence.

(2) In order for the price of class X beneficiary certificates to converge to the net asset value

bid-ask spread to the bid quotation price.

§19. Method of Submitting Liquidity Providing Quotations 

(1) In cases where a liquidity providing member places liquidity providing quotations for class 
X beneficiary certificates, such member shall submit liquidity providing quotations through the 
separate account that has been established beforehand. 

(2) In cases where a liquidity providing member submits liquidity providing quotations for one 
side of the ask or bid quotations for class X beneficiary certificates, such member shall submit 
liquidity providing quotations for the other side without delay. Provided that this shall not apply 
to the cases specified in the Enforcement Rules. 

(3)  The  quantity  of  liquidity  providing  quotations  for  class  X  beneficiary  certificates, 
restrictions  on  submission  of  liquidity  providing  quotations,  and  other  necessary  matters 
regarding liquidity providing quotations for class X beneficiary certificates shall be determined 
by the Enforcement Rules. 

§20. Assessment of Liquidity Providing Members 

(1) The Exchange may assess the effectiveness of liquidity provision by members and publicly 
announce the outcome of such assessment, as prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. In such 
cases, if the results of assessment regarding compliance with the obligation to submit liquidity 
providing quotations has been fulfilled pursuant to [§18(1)] fall within the criteria prescribed 
by  the  Enforcement  Rules,  the  Exchange  may  ask  the  relevant  listed  entity  to  replace  the 
liquidity providing member.

§21. Quotation Unit and Trading Unit 

The quotation quantity unit, tick size, and trading unit of class X beneficiary certificates shall 
be specified by the Enforcement Rules.   

§22. Change of Trade Execution Methods, etc. by Issue 

(1) In cases where the provisional execution price determined by the quotations for class X 
beneficiary  certificates  falls  under  any  of  the  following  subparagraphs,  the  Exchange  may 
change the trade execution method for the relevant issue to the call auction under [§23] of the 
Business Regulations or expand the quotation range participating in call auctions (hereinafter 
referred to as “change of trade execution method, etc.”)., 

1. Where the price rises or falls by more than the rate specified in the Enforcement Rules 
based on the most recent price as of the time of receipt of the relevant quotation during 
the trading hours of a regular session (excluding the opening auction) and that of the after-
hours sessions (limited to after-hours call auction); and 

2. Where the price rises or falls by more than the rate specified in the Enforcement Rules 
based on the most recent price as of the time of receipt of the relevant quotation (limited 
to the price determined by a call auction pursuant to [§23] of the Business Regulations; 
during  the  opening  auction,  it  shall  refer  to  the  reference  price  of  the  day)  during  the 
trading hours of a regular session. 

(2)  Necessary  matters  concerning  the  change  of  the  trade  execution  method  referred  to  in 
paragraph (1) shall be determined by the Enforcement Rules. 

Chapter V. Supplementary Provisions 

§23. Listing Fees and Annual Dues 

(1) An applicant for the listing of class X beneficiary certificates shall pay a listing fee, and a 
listed  entity  shall  pay  annual  dues  as  long  as  the  securities  remain  listed.  Provided  that  the 
Exchange may exempt the payment of the listing fee or annual dues in cases it deems necessary. 

(2) With respect to the listing fees and annual dues noted in paragraph (1), the rate of the listing 
fee,  the  methods  for  calculation  and  payment,  the  scope  of  exemption  and  other  necessary 
matters shall be specified by the Enforcement Rules. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
§24. Enforcement Rules 

Matters necessary for implementing this Regulation shall be prescribed by the Enforcement

ADDENDA 
(Regulation No. 2319 / March 26, 2025) 

§1. Effective Date 

This Regulation shall become effective from the date specified in the Enforcement Rules of 
this Regulation. 

§2. Effective Period 

This Regulation shall remain in effect until the end of the period during which the listing and 
trading  of  class  X  beneficiary  certificates  are  designated  as  innovative  financial  services 
pursuant to the Special Act on Support for Financial Innovation. 

§3. Application of Provisions Concerning Initial Listing Application 

The provisions of Chapter 2 regarding the listing of class X beneficiary certificates shall also 
apply to entities that submit an application for preliminary listing review prior to the effective 
date of this Regulation.
