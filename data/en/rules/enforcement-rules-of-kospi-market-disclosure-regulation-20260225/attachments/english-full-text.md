§1. Purpose   

The  purpose  of  these  Enforcement  Rules  is  to  stipulate  matters  necessary  for  the 
enforcement of the KOSPI Market Disclosure Regulation (hereinafter referred to as “the 
Regulation”). (Amended on February 3, 2009; December 21, 2009) 

§2. Definitions   

(1) The terms used in these Enforcement Rules shall have the same meanings given to 
them in the Regulation and other relevant regulations of the Exchange.   

(2) “Those who are stipulated in the Enforcement Rules” in the proviso to [§2(7)2] of 
the  Regulation  refers  to  an  entity  falling  under  any  of  the  following  subparagraphs: 
(Amended on December 10, 2020)   

1. Entities under Subparagraphs 1 and 2 of Article 10(1) of the Enforcement Decree 
of the Financial  Investment Services and Capital Markets Act (hereinafter referred 
to as “the Enforcement Decree of the Act); 

2. Entities under Subparagraphs 1 through 10 and 13 through 16 of Article 10(2) of 
the Enforcement Decree of the Financial  Investment Services and Capital  Markets 
Act. Provided that, from among the entities under Subparagraph 8 of Article 10(2) 
of  the  Enforcement  Decree  of  the  Financial  Investment  Services  and  Capital 
Markets  Act,  the  investment  advisory  business  entities,  discretionary  investment 
business entities and trust business entities shall be excluded; and 

3.  Entities  under  Subparagraphs  3,  10,  11,  12  and  13  of  Article  10(3)  of  the 
Enforcement Decree of the Financial Investment Services and Capital Markets Act. 

(Wholly amended on February 3, 2009) 

§3. Methods of Document Submission   

The documents specified in [§6(1)] of the Regulation shall, unless otherwise stipulated 
in  the  Regulation,  be  submitted  to  the  Exchange  by  mail,  facsimile  or  electronic 
document, or in person. (Amended on February 3, 2009; April 19, 2012; February 26, 
2013)

§4-4. Market Price-Dividends Ratio, etc.   

“Market price-dividends ratio specified in the Enforcement Rules” in [§7(1)2(e)(v)] of 
the Regulation shall refer to the percentage of dividend per share price (in the case of 
dividend in kind, it shall be the amount converted into cash) obtained by averaging the 
closing  prices  in  the  KOSPI  market  for  one  (1)  week  retroactive  from  the  day  before 
two  (2)  trading  days  (in  cases  where  a  decision  on  dividends  is  made  before  the 
dividend  record  date,  it  shall  be  the  trading  day  immediately  preceding  the  date  of 
decision)  from  the  dividend  record  date  (if  the  day  is  a  holiday,  it  shall  be  the 
immediately preceding trading day). (Amended on April 19, 2012; February 26, 2013; 
March 30, 2023)

§4-5. Guidelines for Determining Material Information subject to Disclosure 

The guidelines referred to in [§7(3)] of the Regulation shall refer to any of the following 
subparagraphs:   

1. Where an event or a decision concerning each of the following items falls under 
the criteria specified in the relevant item. Provided that this provision shall not apply 
to the matters relating to the ordinary management activities. 

(a) For a matter on business operation, product activities, etc.: In cases where it is 
5/100 (25/1,000 for large-sized corporations) or more of sales amount of the latest 
fiscal year; 

(b) For a matter on the securities issued, claims and liabilities, losses and profits, 
closing  of  accounts,  etc.:  In  cases  where  it  is  5/100  (25/1,000  for  large-sized 
corporations) or more of equity capital; and 

(c)  For  a  matter  on  the  investment  activities,  etc.:  In  cases  where  it  is  5/100 
(25/1,000 for large-sized corporations) or more of equity capital or total assets as 
at the end of the latest fiscal year. 

2.  Where  an  event  or  a  decision  other  than  those  noted  in  subparagraph  1  brings 
about  or  is  expected  to  bring  about  a  significant  impact  on  the  management 
(including corporate governance, restructuring, survival, delisting, lawsuits, etc.) or 
properties of the relevant KOSPI-stock-listed corporation, taking into consideration 
the  size,  current  status,  type  of  business  and  other  characteristics  of  the  relevant 
corporation. 

[April, 28, 2016]

following subparagraphs: (Amended on February 26, 2013) 

1. Name of the corporation submitting the request and the date of request; 

2. Reasons for the request; 

3. Changes in the subsidiaries (limited to stock-listed corporations) and 

4. Other matters deemed necessary.

§5. Rumors and News   

“Rumors and news” in [§12(1)] of the Regulation shall be the corporate information of 
KOSPI-stock-listed  corporations  that  is  collected  by  the  Exchange  and  the  news 
published  in  the  general  daily  newspapers  or  special  economic  daily  newspapers 
prescribed in the Act on Promotion of Newspapers, etc., the circulation district of which 
is  nationwide.  (Amended  on  December  27,  2005;  September  11,  2006;  February  3, 
2009; August 28, 2015) 

§6. Request Time of Inquired Disclosure 

The time when the  request  for inquired disclosure is  made pursuant  to  [§12(1)]  of the 
Regulation shall be the time when the Exchange sends out the notification pursuant to 
[§7]. (Amended on February 3, 2009) 

§7. Methods, etc. of requesting Inquired Disclosure 

The request for inquired disclosure under [§13(1)] of the Regulation shall be made by a 
means  of  telephone,  facsimile  or  electronic  document.  In  cases  where  the  request  for 
inquired  disclosure  was  made  by  telephone,  the  details  of  the  request  shall  be 
documented  and  the  document  shall  be  sent  by  a  means  of  facsimile  or  electronic 
document without delay. (Amended on February 3, 2009; April 19, 2012) 

§7-2. Disclosure of Reports on Corporate Governance 

(1)  Pursuant  to  [§24-2(4)]  of  the  Regulation,  in  the  case  where  a  KOSPI-stock-listed 
corporation  files  a report on corporate governance,  it shall  include a written statement 
on filing of the report on corporate governance under [Annex: Form No. 8]. (Amended 
on November 25, 2021; November 21, 2023; July 16, 2025) 

(2)  The  Exchange  may  prescribe  and  present  the  detailed  matters  necessary  for 
describing  the  report  on  corporate  governance.  In  such  cases,  KOSPI-stock-listed 
corporations  shall  faithfully  state  the  presented  matters  and  report  it.  (Amended  on 
December 10, 2020) 

(3) In cases where a KOSPI-stock-listed corporation fails to follow paragraphs (1) and 
(2) in filing the report on corporate governance, the Exchange may deem that the filing 
has  been  made  without  stating  the  material  matters  referred  to  in  [§24-2(2)]  of  the

§7-4. Basic Exchange Rates, etc. to be used by Listed Foreign Corporations 

(1)  “Basic  exchange  rate,  etc.  set  forth  in  the  Enforcement  Rules”  in  [§25(4)]  of  the 
Regulation shall refer to the basic exchange rate or arbitrated rate of exchange under the 
Foreign Exchange Transactions Act (hereinafter in this Article referred to as the “basic 
exchange rate, etc.”) of the day in Korea that corresponds to the day when the cause of 
reporting has occurred. Provided that, from among the details of the relevant matters to 
be  reported,  the  financial  details  in  the  financial  statement  shall  be  applied  with  the 
exchange rate noted in  each of the  following  subparagraphs: (Amended on November 
11, 2011) 

1.  For  the  matters  concerning  financial  details  in  the  financial  statement  or 
consolidated financial statement: The basic exchange rate, etc. as of the end of fiscal 
year for which the relevant  financial statement is  prepared.  Provided that, in  cases 
where the equity capital has changed due to the increase or decrease in capital stock 
and  capital  surplus  since  the  end  of  the  latest  fiscal  year,  the  basic  exchange  rate, 
etc.  as  of  the  base  date  of  the  increase  or  decrease  in  capital  stock  and  capital 
surplus,  such  as  the  payment  date  of  capital  increase  and  the  base  date  for  capital 
reduction.   

2.  For  the  matters  concerning  financial  details  in  the  income  and  loss  statement, 
comprehensive income and loss statement, consolidated income and loss statement 
or  consolidated  comprehensive  income  and  loss  statement:  Average  of  daily  basic 
exchange  rates  of  the  fiscal  year  for  which  the  relevant  financial  statement  was 
prepared.

§7-5. Qualification of Disclosure Agent, etc. 

(1) “Qualification requirements  set  forth  in the  Enforcement  Rules” in  [§26(1)] of the 
Regulation shall refer to the requirements under each subparagraph of paragraph (2). 

(2)  “A  person  who  meets  the  requirements  set  forth  in  the  Enforcement  Rules”  in 
[§26(2)]  of  the  Regulation  shall  refer  to  a  person  who  meets  each  of  the  following 
requirements: (Amended on September 25, 2019) 

1. To have an address or residence in Korea; 

2. To be fluent both in Korean and the language of home country. Provided that this 
provision shall not apply to the case of appointing such person as an assistant; 

3. To meet any of the following items: 

(a) A person who has more than one (1) year of experience as a disclosure staff 
(including disclosure agents; the same hereinafter in this Paragraph) of a KOSPI-
stock-listed  corporation  (including  KOSDAQ-listed  corporations;  the  same 
hereinafter in this Paragraph); and   

(b) A person who has completed the training course for certification of disclosure 
professionals  which  is  offered  by  the  Exchange  to  enhance  the  expertise  of 
disclosure  staff.  In  such  cases,  details  necessary  for  certification  of  disclosure 
professionals shall be set forth in [Annex 4]. 

4. As the disclosure staff of a KOSPI-stock-listed corporation, the total accumulated 
demerit  points  imposed  for  having  been  designated  as  an  unfaithful  disclosure 
corporation during the latest one (1) year shall not exceed fifteen (15). 

[November 11, 2011]

obligations.

range  of  four  (4)  points  in  accordance  with  the  table  entitled  “Guidelines  for 

Deliberation of Disciplinary Actions”. Provided that the maximum demerit points 

shall be 14 and the minimum zero (0).

[Annex 4] Standards for Certification of Disclosure Professionals 
[September 25, 2019] 

Standards for Certification of Disclosure Professionals 

1. Certification of disclosure professionals 

(a)  The  Exchange  shall  certify  a  person  who  has  completed  the  trading  courses 
referred  to  in  subparagraph  2  and  passed  the  prescribed  evaluation  as  a  disclosure 
professional. 

(b) The Exchange shall issue a “certificate of disclosure professional” to a person who 
is certified as a disclosure professional under item (a). 

2. Certification courses and evaluation of disclosure professionals 

(a) The Exchange may offer training courses on corporate disclosure, etc. (hereinafter 
referred to as “disclosure professional certification course”) within twenty (20) hours 
per year for disclosure staff,  etc. of KOSPI-stock-listed corporations for  certification 
of disclosure professionals. 

(b) The disclosure professional certification course shall be offered to the persons who 
have  completed  the  training  courses  under  [§24(1)1]  of  the  Enforcement  Rules  and 
passed the evaluation. 

(c) The criterion for completion of the disclosure professional certification course shall 
be eighty (80) percent or higher of attendance for the relevant course. 

(d)  The  criterion  for  passing  the  disclosure  professional  certification  course  shall  be 
sixty (60) points based on one hundred (100) points. 

(e)  Those  who  have  completed  the  disclosure  professional  certification  course 
pursuant  to  item (c) can take the evaluation for one (1) year from  the date of course 
completion. 

(f)  The  Exchange  may  decide  when  to  offer  the  disclosure  professional  certification 
course, taking into account the needs for the course, etc. 

(g)  The  Exchange  may  entrust  the  training  programs  for  certification  of  disclosure 
professionals to the Korea Listed Companies Association. 

3. Follow-up training 

56 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(a) The Exchange shall provide follow-up training for disclosure professionals within 
eight (8) hours. 

(b) Disclosure professionals shall complete the follow-up training under item (a) once 
in  the  year  which  includes  the  date  three  (3)  years  has  elapsed  based  on  the  year  in 
which they have been certified as disclosure professionals, and additionally complete 
the follow-up training once every three (3) years. 

(c) In the follow-up training under item (a), a separate evaluation may be omitted. 

(d) Items (f) and (g) of subparagraph 2 shall apply mutatis mutandis with respect to the 
provision of follow-up training under item (a). 

4. Management of disclosure professionals 

(a) The Exchange may award commendations and pay monetary rewards to those who 
have  completed  the  disclosure  professional  certification  course  with  excellent 
performance. 

(b)  The  Exchange  shall  prepare  and  maintain  a  list  of  disclosure  professional 
certifications. 

(c) Where a person who was certified as a disclosure professional fails to complete the 
follow-up training under subparagraph 3, the Exchange may revoke the certification of 
disclosure  professional  of  the  person.  In  such  cases,  the  person  whose  disclosure 
professional  certification  has  been  revoked  can  re-acquire  the  certification  of 
disclosure  professional  only  when  he/she  completes  the  disclosure  professional 
certification course under subparagraph 2 again and passes the evaluation. 

5. Preferential treatment for disclosure professionals 

(a)  The  Exchange  may  grant  additional  points  in  applying  the  evaluation  standards 
under [§6] of the “Selection Standards of Outstanding Disclosure Corporations, etc. of 
KOSPI  Market”  when  a  person  who  has  been  certified  as  a  disclosure  professional 
performs the duties as disclosure staff, etc. of a KOSPI-stock-listed corporation. 

(b) The  Exchange may  not  apply item  (a) in  cases where the disclosure  professional 
certification is revoked pursuant to subparagraph 4(c).   

6. Others 

Other necessary detailed matters shall be determined by the head of the department in 

charge of disclosure of the KOSPI Market Division.
