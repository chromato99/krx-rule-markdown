CHAPTER 1. General Provision 

§1. Purpose 

For the efficient distribution and discovery of fair price of petroleum products by way 

of competitive auction in the electronic trading (hereinafter referred to as “e-trading”) of 

petroleum products, which is operated by the Korea Exchange (hereinafter referred to as 

the “Exchange”), the purpose of this Regulation is to stipulate the methods of quotation 

input, trade execution and settlement, admission and withdrawal of participants, the 

rights and obligations of participants, and other matters that are necessary for e-trading 

of petroleum products.         

§2. Definitions 

(1) The terms used in this Regulation shall have the meaning given to them in each 

of the following Items: 

1. The term “e-trading” shall refer to, as an e-trading of spot petroleum operated 

by the Exchange, an auction carried out pursuant to [§37 and §38].    However, 

the  transaction  noted  in  each  of  the  following  Subitems  shall  be  regarded  as  a 

negotiated trading and be excluded from the auction:     

a. Transaction where the same person is the seller as well as the  purchaser; 

and 

b.  Transaction  of  which  seller  has  not  fulfilled  the  settlement  obligation 

pursuant to [§51(1)2]. 

2. The term “participant” shall refer to, as a petroleum business operator pursuant 

to [§3], the entity that has obtained from the Exchange the rights to participate in 

trading of petroleum product.   

3.  The  term  “trading  system”  shall  refer  to  the  system  that  the  Exchange 
develops  and  operates  for  the  purpose  of  handling  the  functions  such  as 
quotation receipt and matching and settlement of transactions.         

1 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
4. The term “participant‟s terminal device” shall refer to the terminal device that 

the  participant  uses  to  connect  to  the  trading  system  by  downloading  and 

installing the quotation input program supplied by the Exchange for the purpose 

of inputting quotation details.     

5. The term “quotation input program” shall refer to the program used to connect 

to  the trading system  from  the participant‟s terminal  device to  handle the tasks 

related to trading of petroleum, including inputting, correcting and cancelling the 

quotation  details,  verification  of  trading  and  settlement  details,  price  data  and 

market notices and delivery of notification and confirmation of receipt thereof.     

6. The term “quotation” shall refer to the sell or buy intention that the participant 
expresses to conduct a trade. 

7. The term “product” shall refer to the product name classified according to the 

type  of  petroleum  products,  application  of  temperature  correction  or  selling  of 

products of other refineries and the trade name per region or petroleum storage 

facility.      (Amended on November 15, 2012) 

8. The term “closing of trade” shall refer to the time when trading hours noted in 

[§20] is ended. 

9. The term “market surveillance” shall  refer to  such activities as  the overseeing 

and  analyzing  the  quotation  and  trading  conditions  and  circumstances,  and 

investigating and inspecting such materials as the business and financial books and 

documents of related participants to confirm if the participants complies with the 

use  agreement  that  the  participant  entered  into  with  the  Exchange  (hereinafter 

referred to as the “Use Agreement”) and this Regulation,         

(2)  Unless  otherwise  defined  in  this  Regulation,  other  terms  used  in  this  Regulation 

shall have the meaning given to them in the Use Agreement.

§4. Application for Participant Admission and Review and Approval Thereafter 

(1) The company intending to become a participant shall complete the application for

admission requirements noted in [§5].     

(3) The Exchange may undertake an onsite investigation to verify the details stated in 

the application, conduct the face-to-face interview with the applicant, etc.

2.  As  of  the  application  date  for  admission,  because  of  such  operations  as  the 
manufacturing  or  sale  of  petroleum  product,  the  applicant  shall  not  have  been

Section 2. Withdrawal of Participant 

§8. Voluntary Withdrawal 

(1) In case of intending to withdraw the participantship, the participant shall prepare and 

submit the completed withdrawal request noted in Annex 4 to the Exchange. 

(2)  Upon  receiving  the  withdrawal  request  pursuant  to  Para.(1),  the  Exchange  shall 

determine and notify the withdrawal date after the participant has fulfilled all his/her 

financial  liability,  including  the  settlement  of  trade  conducted  by  the  concerned 

participant.    In this case, the Exchange shall notify the concerned participant via the 
method specified by the participant.     

§9. Obligatory Withdrawal of Participant   

In case where because of the occurrence of cause noted in any of the following Items, 

the report pursuant to [§14] has been received from the participant or the participant 

has been notified of the withdrawal date, it shall deem that the concerned participant 

has withdrawn its  participantship:  (Amended on July 13, 2012;  November 15, 2012; 

May 16, 2013) 

1.  Case  where  the  registrations  of  all  petroleum  businesses  pursuant  to  the 

Petroleum  Business  Act  have  been  cancelled  or  all  business  locations  have  been 

closed down;         

2.  Case  where  the  all  concerned  petroleum  businesses  have  been  succeeded  by 

another business operator as a result of transfer or merger of businesses or death of 

owner of concerned business;      (Amended on July 13, 2012) 

3. Case where the suspension or close of all petroleum businesses pursuant to the 

Petroleum Business Act has been reported;        (Amended on July 13, 2012) 

8 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
4. Case where the changes in the registration details or reported details (limited to 

the case of changing the name of petroleum business operator or trade name) of all 

petroleum  businesses  pursuant  to  the  Petroleum  Business  Act  have  been  re-

registered;      (Amended on July 13, 2012; November 15, 2012) 

5.  Case  of  becoming  insolvent  or  commencing  the  rehabilitation  procedure,  case 

where the bankruptcy has been applied or case where the cause for dissolving the 

company has occurred; and     

6.  Case  where  the  Exchange  has  notified  the  withdrawal  date  after  making  the 

decision  on  withdrawal  of  participantship  pursuant  to  this  Regulation.      In  this 

case,  the  Exchange  shall  notify  the  participant  using  the  method  that  the 

participant has specified.       

§10. Halting of Trade, Closure of Participant’s ID, etc. 

(1) In case of receiving the request for withdrawal pursuant to [§8(1)] or compulsory 

withdrawal of participant pursuant to [§9], the Exchange may suspend the concerned 

participant from trading from the next trading day.         

(2) In case where the participant pursuant to Para. (1) has failed to fulfill its liability 

resulted from trading, the Exchange shall first appropriate the good faith deposit that 

the concerned participant has deposited with the Exchange and other cash that are due 

to  the concerned participant  for the  reimbursement  of liability,  and then the residual 

amount shall be returned to the concerned participant.         

(3)  In  case  where  the  withdrawal  of  participant  pursuant  to  [§8  or  §9]  has  been 

confirmed, the Exchange shall close up the ID of concerned participant from the date 

noted in each of the following Items: 

1. In case of receiving the notification about the fact pursuant to [§9(1)1 through 

5] from the participant, the next trading date; and   

2.  In  case  of  notifying  the  withdrawal  date  pursuant  to  [§8(2)  and  §9(1)6],  the 
concerned withdrawal date. 

9 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(4)  Notwithstanding  Paras.  (2)  &  (3),  in  case  where  the  concerned  participant  has 

failed  to  settle  its  liability  resulted  from  trading  until  the  date  noted  in  each  Item  of 

Para.  (3),  the  Exchange  may  permit  the  concerned  participant  to  conduct  the  trade 

within the limit related to the settlement of liability, and in this case, the use of ID of 

concerned participant may be allowed.         

Section 3. Rights and Obligations of Participant 

§11. Trading of Petroleum Product   

(1) The participant  may  trade petroleum  products by  connecting to  the trading system 

using the quotation input program installed in the participant‟s terminal device.         

(2)  Notwithstanding  Para.  (1),  the  participant  that  is  a  petro  station  may  request  the 

Exchange to  handle the  trade on its behalf via telephone or facsimile.    In this case, 

before submitting to the Exchange the first request for quotation input, the participant 

shall  enter  into  an  agreement  for  quotation  input  on  behalf  of  participant  with  the 

Exchange, which stipulates such matters as the scope and method of quotation input 

and other necessary matters.      [July 13, 2012] 

(3) When receiving the request for inputting the quotation details pursuant to Para.(2), 

the  Exchange  shall  record  the  details  of  request  and  receipt  time,  and  input  the 

requested  details  into  the  quotation  input  program  without  delay.    In  this  case,  the 

Exchange shall keep the record of concerned matter.        [July 13, 2012] 

§12. Compliance with Laws and Regulations 

The  participant  shall  comply  with  the  relevant  laws,  Use  Agreement  and  this 

Regulation as well as the disciplines and measures imposed according thereto.

§13. Principle of Fairness and Good Faith 

In relation to trading, the participant shall not engage in any activity that disturbs the 

public order or is contrary to the principle of good faith.           

§13-2. Distribution of Tax Benefits, etc. to End-Consumers 

(1)  The  participants  who  trade  the  petroleum  products  eligible  for  the  quota  tariff 

pursuant to [§71] of the Customs Act or refunds of dues, etc. pursuant to [§19] of the 

Petroleum Business Act (hereinafter referred to as the “tax benefits, etc.”) shall make 

an effort to sell the petroleum products at the price where the most of concerned tax 

benefits are distributed to the end consumers.       

(2) The petroleum importer and exporter or authorized dealer that sells the petroleum 

products  to  the  petro  station,  etc.  through  the  methods  without  using  the  quotation 

input  program  shall  make  an  effort  to  sell  the  petroleum  products  to  the  counter 

party  of  concerned  trade  at  the  price  where  the  most  of  concerned  tax  benefits  are 

distributed to the end consumers.   

[November 15, 2012]

Section 3. Execution of Trade 

§36. Principle of Auction 

(1) The trade shall be conducted in accordance with the auction method.   

(2) Auction shall be divided into the single price auction and multiple price auction.     

(3) The priority of quotations in auction shall be determined as specified in each of the 

following Items:       

1.  An  ask  quotation  with  a  lower  price  shall  have  priority  over  an  ask  quotation 

with a higher price and a bid quotation with a higher price shall have priority over 

a bid quotation with a lower price; and 

2. Among the quotation  with  same price, based  on the time of quotation receipt, 

the quotation received earlier shall have priority over the one received later.       

§37. Single Price Auction 

(1)  For  each  product,  the  opening  price  and  closing  price  of  trading  day  may  be 

determined using the single price auction method.     

(2)  The  scope  of  quotations  participating  in  the  single  price  auction  shall  be  the 

quotations received during the period (hereinafter referred to the “quotation hours for 

single  price  auction”)  noted  in  each  of  the  following  Items  and  of  the  quotations 

received earlier and those quotations that are not matched:       

1. In case of determining the opening price of the day, for thirty (30) minutes from 

10:00 O‟clock to 10:30 O‟clock. 

2.  In case of determining the closing price of the day, for ten  (10) minutes from 
16:50 to 17:00 O‟clock.    (Amended on July 13, 2012)

ask quotations and bid quotations noted in each of the following Items match, where 

the matching quantity is the largest, and the trade shall be executed in accordance with 

the quotation priority noted in [§36(3)]. 

1. Total quantity of ask quotations at prices lower than the matching price and total 

quantity of bid quotations at prices higher than the matching price; and   

2. For the matching price, the quantity specified in each of following Subitems:       

a. Of the ask quotations or bid quotations, total quantity of any of one side; and     

b. For the other side, “0” (zero) or the quantity larger than the trading quantity 

unit.       

(4) In case where the matching price noted in Para.(3) is more than two (2), if there is 

a  price  same  as  the  previous  execution  price,  that  price  shall  be  the  matching  price;

previous execution price shall be the matching price.   

(5) If it deems necessary, the Exchange may change the quotation receiving hours for 

single price auction noted in Para.(2).    In this case, the Exchange shall post such fact 

on its homepage and quotation input program.

(3)  In  case  where  the  purchaser  has  filed  an  objection  pursuant  to  Para.  (1),  the

CHAPTER VI. Market Surveillance and Disputes Mediation   

§53. Prohibited Actions of Participant, etc. 

The participant and its officers and employees shall be prohibited from engaging in an 

action noted in any of the following Items:       

1.  Submission  of  matched  orders  without  having  an  intention  of  disposal  or 

acquisition or submission of orders for wash trade without having an intention of 

transferring the ownership;       

2.  Continuous  or  sequential  submission  of  quotations  participating  in  the 

determination of market price, thus changing or fixing the market price, including 

the manipulation of the size of price increase or decrease;   

3.  False  expression  with  the  intention  of  causing  the  price  change  or  expression 

leading to misunderstanding or spreading untruthful facts or rumors;   

4. After causing price increase or decrease or fixing the price, conducting a large 

quantity trade;         

47 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
5.  Conducting  an  excessive  trade  or  submitting  a  quotation  that  has  an  adverse 

impact  or  leads  to  a  misunderstanding  or  concern,  particularly  in  view  of  the 

supply and demand in the market;         

6. Conspiring with other participants with the intention of causing the price surge 

or plunge or fixing the price; and 

7. In addition, the conduct that undermines the fair trading system or tarnishes the 

public confidence in the market. 

§54. Market Surveillance 

(1) The Exchange may oversee the trading situation for the discovery of fair price and 

reliable trading. 

(2) In case of overseeing the trading situation pursuant to Para. (1), the Exchange may 

request  the  participant  to  submit  the  report  on  the  operation  or  financial  condition 

related  to  the  trade  and  reference  materials,  statements  on  facts  and  circumstance 

related  to  the  trade  and  attend  to  make  a  statement,  and  may  let  the  employees  of 

Exchange  to  investigate  into  the  operation,  financial  condition,  books  and  other 

documents related to the trade.     

(3) The employee of Exchange who undertakes the investigation pursuant to Para. (2) 

shall carry with him/her the certificate showing such authorization and present it to the 

person concerned.         

§55. Measures Taken Against Participant According to Result of Investigation   

In  case  where  the  fact  corresponding  to  any  of  the  following  Items  was  found  as  a 

result of investigation into the participant pursuant to [§54], the Exchange may take a 

measure corresponding to [§15(2)] against the concerned participant. 

1.  Case  of  infringing  the  laws  or  the  Use  Agreement  and  this  Regulation 
(hereinafter referred to as the “trade-related laws and regulations”);   

48 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
2. Case of falsely preparing the report or materials to be submitted pursuant to the 

trade-related  laws  and  regulations  or  neglecting  the  submission  of  report  or 

material;     

3. Case of obstructing or rejecting the oversight pursuant to [§54]; and 

4.  In  addition,  when  it  was  recognized  that  an  action  harming  the  fair  trading 

system was taken while trading.       

§56. Dispute Mediation 

(1)  When  there  is  a  dispute  with  another  participant  in  relation  to  the  trade,  the 

participant may request the Exchange to mediate such dispute.       

(2)  When  a  request  for  mediation  of  dispute  pursuant  to  Para.(1)  is  received,  the 

Exchange  may  investigate  into  the  dispute  by  visiting  the  concerned  parties, 

requesting to appear, conducting onsite investigation or inquiring about the facts and 

information, or may request the concerned parties to verify and confirm or submit the 

relevant materials. In this case, the concerned parties shall not refuse to comply with 

the request, provided that there is no justifiable reason.     

(3) In case where it deems that an agreement between the concerned parties would be 

better for the amicable resolution of dispute, the Exchange may, verbally or in writing, 

recommend an agreement before commencing the mediation of case.       

(4) When both of the concerned parties accept the mediation decision of the Exchange, 

it shall deem that the mediation has been accomplished.       

(5)  When  it  deems  necessary,  the  case  may  be  mediated  by  establishing  a  dispute 

mediation committee.
