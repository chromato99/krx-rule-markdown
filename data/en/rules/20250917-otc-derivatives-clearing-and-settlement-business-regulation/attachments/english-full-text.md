CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS 

§1. Purpose 

The  purpose  of  this  Regulation  shall  be  to  stipulate  the  matters  necessary  for  Korea 
Exchange  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the  Exchange”)  as  a  financial  investment  product 
clearinghouse  [hereinafter  referred  to  as  the  “CCP  (central  counterparty)”]  in  performing 
the tasks related to the business of financial investment products clearing, including trades 
subject  to  clearing,  qualification  of  a  clearing  member,  assumption  and  fulfillment  of 
obligations,  clearing  submission,  and  member  margin,  pursuant  to  Article  323-11  of  the 
Financial Investment Services and Capital Market Act (hereinafter referred to as “the Act”).

following  subparagraphs:  (Amended  on  June  3,  2014;  October  21,  2015;  November  30,

referred  to  as  the  “swap  trading”),  which  was  carried  out  outside  the  market 
(hereinafter  referred  to  as  the  “OTC”)  established  by  the  entity  who  obtained  the 
permission for exchange pursuant to Article 373-2 of the Act.

§3. Non-business day   

(1) The Exchange shall regard the day falling under any of the following subparagraphs as a 
holiday when no business is conducted:   

1. Public holidays under the Rules on Holidays of Public Agencies;   
2. Labor day under the Designation of Worker’s Day Act; and   
3. Saturdays.   

(2)  In  the  case  of  a  shutdown  of  overall  functions  of  social  infrastructure,  including  the 
natural  disaster,  war,  blackout  of  electricity  and  communication  system  failure,  and  cases 
where it deems necessary to suspend the clearing operation for more than one (1) day, the 

3 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Exchange shall not carry out the clearing operation by declaring a temporary holiday within 
the necessary limit.   

(3)  In  cases  where  the  clearing  operation  is  suspended  pursuant  to  paragraph  (2),  the 
Exchange  shall  notify  the  reasons  for  suspension  to  the  clearing  members  and  clients  in 
advance.   

§4. Suspension and Resumption of Clearing Operation   

(1) In cases where it deems difficult to carry out the clearing operation in a normal manner 
due to reasons such as the failure of Exchange OTC derivatives settlement system, etc., the 
Exchange may suspend a part or the whole of the clearing operation.   

(2) In the case of suspending the clearing operation pursuant to paragraph (1), the Exchange 
may  change  the  deposit  deadline  of  margin,  the  delivery  deadline  of  net  cash  settlement 
amount, etc.   

(3) In cases where, after the clearing operation was suspended pursuant  paragraph (1), the 
cause  for  such  suspension  has  been  resolved,  the  Exchange  shall  resume  the  clearing 
operation without delay.   

(4) In the case of suspending the clearing operation pursuant to paragraph (1) or changing 
the deadline pursuant to paragraph (2), the Exchange shall notify the reasons therefor to the 
clearing members and clients.

§5. Clearing Eligible Transactions and Prohibition of Discriminatory Handling 

(1) Clearing eligible transactions shall be classified as follows: (Amended on November 30, 
2016) 

1. KRW interest rate swap transactions: This is the OTC derivatives trades that meet all 
of  the  requirements  specified  in  [§6(1)]  and  of  which  the  contract  amount  is 
denominated  in  Korean  won,  and  shall  be  categorized  as  follows:  (Amended  on 
September 17, 2025) 

  (a) KRW IRS Transactions: The interest rate swap transactions based on the floating 
interest rate noted in [§6(1)3(a)]; and [September 17, 2025] 

  (b)  KRW  OIS  Transactions:  The  overnight  index  swap  transactions  based  on  the 
floating interest rate noted in [§6(1)3(b)]. [September 17, 2025] 

4 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
2. Foreign currency interest rate swap transactions: The interest rate swap transactions, 
as  the  OTC  derivatives  trades  that  meet  all  of  the  requirements  noted  in  [§6(2)],  of 
which  contract  amount  is  denominated  in  foreign  currencies  specified  in  the 
Enforcement Rules. 

(2)  The  Exchange  shall  not  discriminate  against  a  clearing  eligible  transaction  executed 
bilaterally  or  on  or  subject  to  the  rules  of  an  unaffiliated  electronic  matching  platform  or 
trade  execution  facility  without  rational  reasons  during  the  clearing  operation.  [May  27, 
2015] 

§6. Requirements of Clearing Eligible Transactions 

(1) The KRW interest rate swap transactions shall satisfy all of the requirements specified 
in the following subparagraphs:   

1. It shall be a transaction executed in accordance with an ISDA Mater Agreement and 
ISDA Definitions; 

2.  It  shall  be  a  transaction  where  the  fixed  interests  are  exchanged  for  the  floating 
interests for the contract amount;   

3.  Clearing  eligible  transactions  under  each  of  the  following  subparagraphs  shall  be 
concluded  at  a  floating  interest  rate  prescribed  in  that  subparagraph:  (Amended  on 
September 17, 2025) 

    (a)  KRW  IRS  Transactions:  The  CD  interest  rate  at  the  time  specified  in  the 
Enforcement  Rules  on  the  business  day  immediately  preceding  the  first  day  of  the 
interest calculation period; and [September 17, 2025] 

    (b)  KRW  OIS  Transactions:  The  annual  rate  of  return  calculated  according  to  the 
method stipulated in the Enforcement Rules based on the KOFR at the time specified 
in the Enforcement Rules. [September 17, 2025] 

4.  The  settlement  amount  shall  be  denominated  in  the  currencies  specified  in  the 
Enforcement Rules; and 

5. In addition, the requirements specified in the Enforcement Rules shall be satisfied.   

(2) The foreign currency interest rate swap transactions shall satisfy all of the requirements 
noted in the following subparagraphs:   

1. It shall be a transaction executed in accordance with an ISDA Mater Agreement and 
ISDA Definitions; 

2.  It  shall  be  a  transaction  where  the  fixed  interests  are  exchanged  for  the  floating 
interests for the contract amount;

Exchange.

Section 2. Admission of Clearing Member 

§9. Application for Clearing Membership   

(1)  Any  entity  that  wishes  to  obtain  the  clearing  membership  shall  file  an  application  for 
clearing membership to the Exchange in the application form specified in the Enforcement 
Rules, for each category of clearing membership.   

(2)  When  an  entity  that  voluntarily  withdrew  from  the  clearing  membership  pursuant  to 
[§14]  intends  to  apply  for  clearing  membership,  the  period  specified  in  the  Enforcement

necessary. (Amended on September 17, 2025) 

(3) Matters necessary for application for clearing membership and other necessary matters 
related to the admission as clearing member shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.   

§10.  Requirements  for  Clearing  Member  Admission,  Review  of  Application  and 
Admission Approval   

(1)  Any  entity  intending  to  become  a  clearing  member  of  the  Exchange  shall  satisfy  the 
criteria noted in each of the following subparagraphs:   

1. To meet the financial requirements under [§11];   

2.  To  be  equipped  with  equipment  and  facilities,  including  the  electronic  facilities, 
necessary for the efficient accomplishment of tasks of clearing member;   

3. To have human resources sufficient  to carry out  the tasks of clearing  member in a 
reliable and efficient manner; and

(2) Upon receiving an application for clearing membership, the Exchange shall review if the 
applicant meets the admission criteria and notify the applicant the review result within three 
(3) months of the application date.   

(3) The Exchange may undertake an onsite investigation to verify the details stated in the 
application. 

(4) In the case of finding a deficiency in the application submitted, the Exchange may ask 
the  applicant  to  supplement  the  application.  In  such  cases,  the  Exchange  may  extend  the

Section 3. Withdrawal from Clearing Membership 

§14. Voluntary Withdrawal   

(1)  When  intending  to  voluntarily  withdraw  from  the  clearing  membership,  a  clearing 
member  shall  request  the  withdrawal  from  membership  to  the  Exchange  in  the  form 
specified in the Enforcement Rules.   

11 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(2)  In  cases  where  a  request  for  withdrawal  from  clearing  membership  pursuant  to 
paragraph (1) is filed, the membership shall be withdrawn on the day later of the days in the 
following subparagraphs:       

1. The day when the number of days specified in the Enforcement Rules  has elapsed 
from the date of request for the membership withdrawal; and   

2. The business day when the clearing member has closed out all cleared transactions.   

§15. Compulsory Withdrawal           

In  cases  where  the  cause  noted  in  any  of  the  following  subparagraphs  has  occurred  to  a 
clearing member, it shall be deemed that the membership of the relevant clearing member 
has compulsory been withdrawn: 

1.  Termination  of  authorization  for  investment  trading  business  of  OTC  derivatives 
with  the  interest  rate  as  an  underlying  asset  acquired  pursuant  to  Article  12(1)  of  the 
Act or a shutdown or transfer of investment dealing business;   

2. Dissolution; and   

3. Expulsion from the membership.   

§16. Close-out Cleared Transactions   

The  Exchange  shall  close  out  the  cleared  transactions  of  clearing  member  whose 
membership  has  been  deemed  withdrawn  pursuant  to  [§15]  using  a  method  that  it  deems 
appropriate, including auction.   

§17. Notification of Request for Withdrawal or Withdrawal from Membership 

In the event that a clearing member requests to voluntarily withdraw its membership or has 
withdrawn  from  its  membership,  the  Exchange  shall  notify  such  fact  to  other  clearing 
members and clients without delay. 

§18. Succession of Clearing Membership   

(1)  The  membership  of  the  clearing  member  that  withdrew  its  membership  because  of  a 
merger or a business transfer may be succeeded by the surviving company or newly created 
company  subsequent  to  the  merger  or  by  the  company  taking  over  the  business,  with  the 
approval of the Exchange.   

(2) In the case of taking over the membership of a clearing member pursuant to paragraph 
(1), the cleared transactions of the member that withdrew its membership shall be deemed 

12 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
to  be  the  cleared  transactions  of  the  surviving  company  or  the  company  newly  created 
subsequent to the merger or the company taking over the business.   

§19. Termination of Clearing Participation   

In cases where a clearing member requests the membership withdrawal pursuant to [§14(1)] 
or a clearing member has been deemed withdrawn from the membership pursuant to [§15], 
the Exchange shall terminate the clearing participation by the relevant clearing member on 
the  next  day.  Provided  that,  in  cases  where  it  deems  necessary,  the Exchange  shall  allow 
clearing  participation  of  the  relevant  clearing  member  for  a  limited  period  deemed 
necessary.   

§20. Liability of Clearing Member Withdrawing Membership   

(1) In cases where a settlement default by other clearing members occurs before the date of 
voluntary  membership  withdrawal  of  a  clearing  member  and  the  loss  caused  by  such 
settlement  default  is  not  finalized  before  the  voluntary  withdrawal  date,  the  clearing 
member  that  requested  for  the  voluntary  withdrawal  of  membership  shall  be  deemed  a 
clearing member within the extent specified in the Enforcement Rules.   

(2)  In  cases  where  a  settlement  default  by  other  clearing  members  has  occurred  before  a 
compulsory  withdrawn  clearing  member  under  [§15]  closes  out  the  entire  quantity  of 
cleared transactions pursuant to [§16] and the loss caused by such settlement default has not 
been  finalized  before  the  relevant  cleared  transactions  are  entirely  closed  out,  the 
compulsory  withdrawn  clearing  member  shall  be  deemed  a  clearing  member  within  the 
extent specified in the Enforcement Rules.   

§21. Return and Succession of Contribution to OTC Derivatives JCF 

(1) The Exchange shall return the OTC derivatives JCF contributed by the clearing member 
withdrawing  its  membership  within  twenty  (20)  days  of  the  membership  withdrawal  day. 
Provided  that,  in  cases  where  the  relevant  clearing  member  owes  the  Exchange  any 
outstanding  payment,  the  Exchange  shall  return  the  balance  after  first  appropriating  the 
fund for the payment of arrears.   

(2)  Notwithstanding  the  main  sentence  of  paragraph  (1),  in  the  case  of  being  deemed  a 
clearing  member  pursuant  to  [§20],  the  contribution  to  the  OTC  derivatives  JCF  shall  be 
returned within twenty (20) days from the date when the loss caused by a settlement default 
of other clearing member was finalized and fully compensated.   

(3) In  cases  where the membership of clearing  member is succeeded pursuant to [§18], it 
shall be deemed that the contribution made by that clearing member to the OTC derivatives 
JCF  has  been  succeeded  by  the  surviving  or  newly  created  company  subsequent  to  the 
merger or by the company taking over the business.

Section 4. Conversion of Clearing Membership 

§22. Request for Conversion of Clearing Membership and Conversion Criteria   

(1)  In  cases  where  a  general  clearing  member  intends  to  convert  its  membership  to  a 
proprietary  clearing  member  or  a  proprietary  clearing  member  intends  to  convert  its 
membership to a general clearing member, the relevant clearing member shall submit to the 
Exchange the request for conversion of membership according to the form specified in the 
Enforcement Rules (referred to as the “conversion of clearing membership” hereinafter in 
this Section).   

(2)  The  proprietary  clearing  member  that  wishes  to  convert  its  membership  to  general 
clearing member shall satisfy the admission criteria of general clearing member pursuant to 
[§10].   

§23. Review and Approval of Request for Conversion of Clearing Membership   

(1)  The  provisions  of  [§9(3)]  shall  apply  mutatis  mutandis  to  the  review  of  request  for 
conversion of clearing membership.   

(2) The request for conversion of clearing membership shall be approved by a resolution of 
the Board. In such cases, the conversion request may be approved while imposing specific 
conditions.   

§24. Payment of Conversion Fee   

A proprietary clearing member that obtained the approval for membership conversion to a 
general  clearing member shall pay to  the Exchange the amount (hereinafter referred to  as 
the “conversion fee”) determined by subtracting the proprietary clearing member admission 
fee from the general clearing member admission fee as of the approval date.   

§25. Cancellation and Notification of Conversion Approval and Conversion Date   

(1)  The  validity  of  membership  conversion  shall  be  effective  on  the  day  (hereinafter 
referred  to  as  “conversion  date”)  after  when  the  clearing  member  that  obtained  the 
membership  conversion  approval  completes  the  membership  conversion  procedures  for 
clearing member that the Exchange specifies, including the implementation of conditions on 
conversion pursuant to the proviso to [§23(2)] and payment of conversion fee pursuant to 
[§24].   

(2) In cases where the clearing member that obtained the membership conversion approval 
fails  to  fulfill  the  conditions  on  membership  conversion  or  to  complete  the  membership 
conversion procedures pursuant to paragraph (1) by the date specified by the Exchange, it 
shall  be  deemed  that  the  relevant  clearing  member  has  withdrawn  the  membership 
conversion request.

Section 5. Obligations of Clearing Members 

§26. Setting aside of OTC Derivatives Joint Compensation Fund   

(1) Each clearing member shall set aside the OTC derivatives JCF in the Exchange.   

(2) The Exchange shall calculate the total amount to be jointly contributed by all clearing 
members in order to fulfil the settlement obligation in a timely manner on the day when a 
settlement default occurs by two (2) clearing members with the biggest settlement risk and a 
party  specified  in  the  Enforcement  Rules  under  an  extreme  but  plausible  circumstance 
(hereinafter referred to as “total contribution amount of the OTC derivatives JCF”), on the 
basis of the last business day of every month or the date set forth in the Enforcement Rules 
(hereinafter referred to as “base date for calculation of OTC derivatives JCF”) according to 
the methods set forth in the Enforcement Rules. Provided that, in the case stipulated in the 
Enforcement  Rules,  it  may  temporarily  exceed  or  fall  short  of  the  amount.  (Amended  on 
December 5, 2018) 

(3)  The  Exchange  shall  calculate  the  amount  of  OTC  derivatives  JCF  that  each  clearing 
member is required to contribute (hereinafter referred to as the “required OTC derivatives 
JCF  contribution  amount”)  on  the  base  date  for  calculation  of  OTC  derivatives  JCF  in 
accordance with provisions of [§29]. (Amended on December 5, 2018) 

(4)  Each  clearing  member  shall  set  aside  the  required  OTC  derivatives  JCF  contribution 
amount calculated according to paragraph (3) in the Exchange within the period specified in 
the Enforcement Rules counting from the base date for calculation of OTC derivatives JCF. 
In such cases, the amount of the OTC derivatives JCF set aside by each clearing member in 
the  Exchange  to  meet  the  required  OTC  derivatives  JCF  contribution  amount  (hereinafter 
referred to as the “OTC  derivatives JCF contribution amount”) shall  not include the OTC 
derivatives JCF contribution amount that the Exchange allocates and manages separately to 
compensate  the  losses  incurred  by  settlement  defaults  that  already  occurred,  but  shall 
include  the  gains  or  losses  under  the  provisions  of  [§27(3)].  (Amended  on  December  5, 
2018) 

(5)  Notwithstanding  paragraphs  (2)  and  (3),  during  the  cap  period  noted  in  [§116],  the 
Exchange shall not calculate the total contribution amount of the OTC derivatives JCF and 
the required OTC derivatives JCF contribution amount. (Amended on December 5, 2018) 

(6)  When  calculating  the  total  contribution  amount  of  the  OTC  derivatives  JCF  and  the 
required  OTC  derivatives  JCF  contribution  amount  of  a  clearing  member,  the  Exchange 
shall notify the amounts to the clearing member without delay. (Amended on December 5,

§32. Compliance with Acts and Regulations

Clearing members in clearing participation shall  observe due care and diligence  and shall 
not engage in any actions that violate the principle of good faith.

physical  facilities  and  size  of  risk  born  as  a  company  clearing  the  financial  investment 
product transactions.

§42. Procedures, etc. for taking Measures against Clearing Members 

(1)  In  cases  where  the  grounds  for  taking  measures  under  [§39]  have  occurred,  the

(2) The clearing member may file an official objection within seven (7) days of receiving

attach a plan for improving financial conditions when submitting the opinion statement.   

(3)  The  Exchange  shall  process  the  measures  against  clearing  members  within  one  (1) 
month from the date on which the notification of the occurrence of the grounds for taking 
measures was made pursuant to paragraph (1).   

(4) In cases where a clearing member who have been notified of the measures under [§40] 
has an objection, the clearing member may file an official objection within one (1) month 
from the date of the receipt of such notification. In such cases, the Exchange shall process 
the  relevant  objection  within  one  (1) month  from  the date  of  the  receipt  of  the  objection, 
unless there is any special reason.   

(5) No clearing member can file an objection again for the matter that has been processed 
through filing of official objections under paragraph (4).   

(6)  Matters  necessary  concerning  the  plan  for  improving  financial  conditions  under 
paragraph  (2)  and  the  official  objection  under  paragraph  (4)  shall  be  stipulated  in  the 
Enforcement Rules.

procedures and the criteria and delegation of authority in taking an action or requesting to

mutandis thereto according to its nature.   

§43. Summary Fines   

(1) In cases where, as a minor violation among miscellaneous illegitimate conducts such as 
noncompliance with reporting obligation, a speedy processing of case deems necessary, the 
Exchange may impose a summary fine of less than KRW 2 million.   

(2)  In  cases  where  the  clearing  member  does  not  respond  to  the  imposition  of  summary 
fines  noted  in  paragraph  (1)  or  fails  or  neglects  to  pay  the  summary  fines,  the  Exchange 
may take an action or request to take an action pursuant to [§40].   

(3) The  imposition  of  summary  fines  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1)  and  measures  pursuant  to 
paragraph  (2)  shall  be  decided  by  the  resolution  of  the  Market  Oversight  Commission. 
Provided that the imposition of summary fines shall be delegated to the Chairman of Market 
Oversight Commission.   

(4) Matters necessary for the imposition of summary fines and other necessary matters shall 
be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.   

§44. Notification of Measures 

In the case of expelling a clearing member from the membership, suspending the whole or a 
part of entitlements of a clearing member or suspending a clearing member from clearing 
participation,  or  revoking  any  of  the  measures  taken,  the  Exchange  shall  notify  other 
clearing members of such fact without delay.

(2)  The  specifications  of  the  cleared  transactions,  in  addition  to  those  specified  in  [§52],

down the request for assumption of obligations:   

1.  Case  where  it  deems  inappropriate  to  accept  the  request  for  assumption  of 
obligations  in  view  of  such  facts  as  the  financial  conditions  and  the  size  of  cleared 
transactions of the client; and   

2. Case where it deems necessary for the protection of public interests or market order.   

(3) In the case of intending to reject the request for assumption of obligations pursuant to

rejection.

CHAPTER VI. SETTLEMENT OF CLEARED TRANSACTIONS 

Section 1. Settlement between a Clearing Member and the Exchange 

§64. Daily Settlement and Settlement Rates   

(1)  For  each  cleared  transaction  per  clearing  member  and  per  clearing  account,  the 
Exchange  shall  conduct  the  daily  settlement  based  on  the  net  present  value  determined 
using the settlement rate.   

(2) The settlement  rate  noted in paragraph (1) shall be the interest rate that the Exchange 
determines and publishes for the maturities specified in the Enforcement Rules (hereinafter 
referred to as the “rate based maturity”) on each business day.   

(3)  When  it  deems  necessary  for  the  determination  of  settlement  rate,  the  Exchange  may 
request  the  clearing  member  to  submit  the  rate  by  specifying  the  rate  based  maturity.  In 
such cases, the clearing member shall respond to the request without delay.   

(4) Matters concerning the determination and publication of the settlement rate, calculation 
of  net  present  value  and  other  necessary  matters  shall  be  stipulated  in  the  Enforcement 
Rules.   

§65. Receipt and Delivery of Daily Net Settlement Amount 

34 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(1) For each cleared transaction, the clearing member shall deliver to and receive from the 
Exchange  the  amount  noted  in  each  of  the  following  subparagraphs  in  the  settlement

2015; November 30, 2016) 

1. The amount equivalent to the net present value of the day of the cleared transaction 
for which the Exchange registered the assumption of obligation on the day (hereinafter 
referred to as the “net settlement amount of the day”);   

2.  The  amount  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  the  “renewed  net  settlement  amount”) 
determined  by  subtracting  the net  present  value  of previous  business  day  (hereinafter 
referred  to  as  the  “net  present  value  of  the  previous  day”)  from  today’s  net  present 
value for other cleared transitions;   

3. The interest  (hereinafter referred to as the “price alignment  interest”) incurred as a 
result of delivery and receipt of net settlement amount;   

4.  In  cases  where  the  next  business  day  is  the  interest  payment  day,  the  difference 
(hereinafter referred to as the “coupon”) in the interests exchanged between the fixed 
rate payer and floating rate payer; and   

5. The amount (hereinafter referred to as the “unrealized interest difference”) calculated 
by subtracting the unrealized interest on the previous business day from the unrealized

(2) The calculation of the net settlement amount of the day, renewed net settlement amount,

Section 2. Net Settlement and Settlement Deadline 

§66. Determination of Net Cash Settlement Amount and Settlement Deadline   

(1) The Exchange shall fix the balance (hereinafter referred to as the “net cash settlement 
amount”) resulting from netting the total amount payable and total amount receivable in the 
net settlement amount of the day, renewed net settlement amount, price alignment interest,   
coupon,  unrealized  interest  difference  and  negotiated  settlement  amount  for  each  cleared 
transaction with the same currency, date, deadline and floating interest rate for settlement,

as  the  settlement  amount  to  be  delivered  and  received  between  the  Exchange  and  the 
clearing members. (Amended on November 30, 2016; September 17, 2025) 

(2) The settlement deadline of net cash settlement amount noted in paragraph (1) shall be 
16:00 on the day after the calculation day of net cash settlement amount.   

(3)  The  Exchange  shall  pay  the  net  cash  settlement  amount  if  the  net  cash  settlement 
amount  of  clearing  member  is  larger  than  zero  and  shall  receive  the  net  cash  settlement 
amount if the net cash settlement amount of clearing member is less than zero.   

(4) In cases where it deems necessary for a reliable clearing operation, the Exchange may 
change the settlement date and settlement deadline noted in paragraph (2).   

(5) The calculation method of net cash settlement amount noted in paragraph (1) shall be 
stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.   

§67. Notification of Settlement Details   

The Exchange shall notify the details of net cash settlement amount determined pursuant to 
[§66(1)] (hereinafter referred to as the “settlement details”) to the clearing member by way 
of  transmitting  them  to  the  Member  OTC  derivatives  settlement  system  or  Member  OTC 
derivatives  settlement  terminal  from  the  Exchange  OTC  derivatives  settlement  system  or 
other methods.   

§68. Delivery and Receipt of Net Cash Settlement Amount   

Clearing members shall deliver the net cash settlement amount pursuant to the notification 
of  settlement  details  received  to  the  Exchange  by  the  settlement  date  and  settlement 
deadline noted in  [§66(2)], and the Exchange shall  pay the net  cash settlement amount to 
clearing members after the settlement deadline.   

§69. Method of Delivery and Receipt of Net Cash Settlement Amount   

(1)  Clearing  members  shall  deliver  the  net  cash  settlement  amount  by  transferring  the 
amount from the bank account under the name of clearing member to the account under the 
name  of  the  Exchange  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  the  “settlement  bank  account  of  the 
Exchange”) that was opened for the deposit of member margin and settlement of net cash 
settlement amount with the settlement bank.   

(2) The Exchange shall  deliver the net cash settlement amount by way of transferring the 
amount  from  the settlement  bank  account  of  the  Exchange to  the bank  account  under  the 
name of  clearing  member  when  there is  a payment  request  from  the  clearing  member.  In 
such cases, the clearing member shall be prohibited to make a payment request of net cash 
settlement  amount  in  the  amount  in  excess  of  the  amount  specified  in  the  Enforcement 
Rules.

settlement amount shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.   

§69-2. Settlement Finality of Net Cash Settlement Amount   

Clearing members may not cancel the net cash settlement amount after it is transferred to 
the  account  of  the  Exchange  at  the  settlement  bank  pursuant  to  [§69(1)].  (Amended  on

§70. Settlement Instructions   

In the case of delivering the net cash settlement amount to the clearing member pursuant to

account  under  the  name  of  clearing  member  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  the  “settlement 
instruction”).   

§71. Settlement Bank Management 

(1) The Exchange shall  monitor the extent of implementation of settlement instruction by 
the settlement bank on an ongoing basis.     

(2) To conduct a reliable and efficient settlement operation, the Exchange shall periodically 
verify such facts as the financial conditions of settlement banks.   

(3) The designation and termination of settlement bank and other matters necessary for the 
management of settlement bank shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.   

Section 3. Analysis and Management of Settlement Risks and Liquidity Provision 

§72. Analysis and Management of Settlement Risks, etc. 

(1)  In  cases  where  it  deems  difficult  to  efficiently  fulfill  the  settlement  obligation,  the 
clearing member shall notify such fact to the Exchange without delay.   

(2)  For  the  purpose  of  conducting  a  reliable  and  efficient  settlement  operation,  the 
Exchange shall analyze and manage the settlement risk of clearing members and report it to 
the  Risk  Management  Committee  noted  in  Article  28(4)  of  the  Articles  of  Incorporation. 
(Amended on September 17, 2025) 

(3)  The  details  of  notification  noted  in  paragraph  (1),  management  and  analysis  of 
settlement risk of clearing member noted in paragraph (2) and other necessary matters shall 
be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.

§73. Liquidity Provision, etc. for Settlement Guarantee 

(1) In cases where a clearing member has failed to deliver the net cash settlement amount 
by  the  settlement  deadline noted  in  [§66(2)],  the  Exchange shall  immediately  provide  the

(2)  In  the  case  of  providing  the  liquidity  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1),  the  Exchange  shall

(3) The provision of liquidity, type and usage of financial resources pursuant to paragraph 
(1) and other necessary matters shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.   

§74. Loss caused by Settlement Delay   

(1)  The  clearing  member  that  fails  to  deliver  the  net  cash  settlement  amount  by  the

systems,  and  where  the  Exchange  deems  that  liquidity  is  difficult  to  provide  pursuant  to 
[§73(1p)], the amount of loss caused by settlement delay may not be paid.   

(2)  Methods  of  calculation  and  payment  of  loss  caused  by  settlement  delay  noted  in 
paragraph (1) and other  matters necessary  for the imposition of loss caused by settlement 
delay shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.   

§75. Bearing of Expenses related to Settlement   

Clearing members shall bear all expenses, such as bank service fees, incurred in relation to 
the settlement with clearing member or settlement default by the clearing member. In such 
cases, clearing member may have the relevant client bear all expenses incurred in relation to 
the client.

CHAPTER VII. MARGINS   

Section 1. General Provisions 

§80. Classification and Calculation of Margins 

40 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(1) Margins shall be classified into member margin that the clearing member deposits with 
the  Exchange  to  guarantee  the  fulfillment  of  its  obligation  to  the  Exchange,  and  client 
margin that the client deposits with the general clearing member to guarantee the fulfillment 
of  its  obligation  to  general  clearing  member  with  whom  it  has  entered  into  the  Client 
Clearing Agreement.   

(2)  The  Exchange  shall  calculate  the  member  margins  by  settlement  currency  of  each 
clearing  account  and  the  general  clearing  members  shall  calculate  the  client  margins  by 
settlement  currency  of  each  member’s  account  for  client.  (Amended  on  November  30, 
2016) 

Section 2. Deposit of Member Margins 

§81. Deposit of Member Margins         

(1) Clearing member shall deposit, as member margin, the amount that is no less than the 
sum  of  the  amount  specified  in  the  Enforcement  Rules  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  the 
“member  risk  margin  amount”)  and  the  amount  equivalent  to  the  maximum  net  loss  that 
may result in cases where the interest rate for cleared transaction varies by a certain level 
(hereinafter referred to as the “member net exposure margin amount”) by the time specified 
in the Enforcement Rules (hereinafter referred to as the “reference time for calculation of 
member net exposure margin”), which is after the deadline for requesting the assumption of 
obligation.   

(2) The  deposit  deadline  of  member  margin  calculated  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1)  shall  be 
12:00 of the business day after the calculation day.   

(3) Member margin, which the clearing member is required to deposit with the Exchange in 
advance when the Exchange registers the assumption of obligation, shall be stipulated in the 
Enforcement Rules. (Amended on May 27, 2015) 

(4)  In  cases  where  it  deems  necessary  for  the  market  management,  the  Exchange  may 
change  the  reference  time  for  calculation  of  member  net  exposure  margin  noted  in 
paragraph (1) and the deposit deadline noted in paragraph (2).   

(5) The calculation methods of member net exposure margin and member risk margin and 
other matters necessary for member margin shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.   

§82. Deposit of Intraday Member Margins   

(1)  In  cases  where  the  assess  value  of  the  deposited  asset  as  at  the  time  specified  in  the 
Enforcement  Rules  on  each  business  day  is  less  than  the  clearing  amount  of  member 
maintenance margin, the clearing member shall deposit to the Exchange the amount higher 
than the intraday member margin amount that takes into account the intraday net settlement 

41 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
loss, etc. specified in the Enforcement Rules, including the intraday settlement loss as the 
intraday member margin. (Amended on November 30, 2016) 

(2) The deposit deadline of intraday member margin determined pursuant to paragraph (1) 
shall be 14:00 on the calculation day. Provided that, in the case of intending to request the 
assumption of obligation, it shall be the earlier of 14:00 of calculation day or the time when 
the request for assumption of obligation is made after receiving the notification of intraday 
member margin.   

(3) In cases where the clearing member is required to deposit an intraday member margin 
pursuant  to  paragraph  (1),  the  member  margin  pursuant  to  [§81(3)]  shall  be  separately

CHAPTER IX. DEFAULT MANAGEMENT PROCESS 

Section 1. Measures against Defaulting Clearing Members 

§101. Measures in Case of Settlement Default of Clearing Members 

(1) In cases where it is deemed that a clearing member fails or is likely to fail to fulfill the 
settlement obligation or to deposit member margins (hereinafter referred to as “settlement 
default, etc.” in this Section), the Exchange may take actions noted in each of the following 
subparagraphs against the relevant clearing member, by specifying a certain period of time 
or until the cause therefor is eliminated. 

1. To suspend whole or a part of the clearing participation;   

2.  To  suspend  payments  of  whole  or  a  part  of  the  net  cash  settlement  amount  or  the 
member margin that the clearing member is to receive from the Exchange, or whole or 
a  part  of  the  cash  or  securities  that  the  clearing  member  is  to  receive  from  the 
Exchange; and   

3. In addition, to take measures specified in the Enforcement Rules.   

(2) “Cases where a clearing member is likely to fail to fulfill the settlement obligation or to 
deposit  member  margins”  in  paragraph  (1)  shall  refer  to  a  case  falling  under  any  of  the 
following subparagraphs:   

1. Where there is a request for payment suspension or commencement of rehabilitation 
procedures by the clearing member;   

2. Where the check or bill issued has defaulted or the transaction with banks has been 
suspended;   

3.  Where  the  Financial  Services  Commission  has  taken  the  action  to  suspend  the 
business operation;   

4. Where a foreign investment company falls under the cause equivalent or similar to 
subparagraphs  1  through  3  in  case  of  a  branch  office,  etc.  of  a  foreign  financial 
investment company;   

51 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
5. Where a financial fraud or non-performing loan involving a large sum has occurred;   

6. Where there is a sharp deterioration in liquidity; and   

7.  In  addition,  as  a  case  equivalent  to  the  provisions  of  subparagraphs  1  through  6, 
where it is likely to fail to fulfill the settlement obligations.   

(3) In the case of having been imposed of measures noted in paragraph (1) due to settlement 
default,  the  net  cash  settlement  amount,  member  margin  or  other  cash  and  securities  of 
which the payment has been suspended pursuant to paragraph (1)2 may be appropriated for 
the collection of claim or be used to offset against the net cash settlement amount that the 
relevant clearing member is required to deliver to the Exchange. In such cases, it shall be 
deemed that the profit for the credit and claim period that the clearing member collects from 
the Exchange is lost.   

(4)  Clearing  member  subject  to  the  measures  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1)  shall  notify  the 
details of the measures and other matters that the Exchange deems necessary to the client 
without delay.   

(5) When taking the measures against the clearing member pursuant to paragraph (1), and 
when it deems necessary, the Exchange may dispatch its employee to the relevant clearing 
member  pursuant  to  the  stipulation  in  the  Enforcement  Rules  to  provide  the  support 
required for efficient settlement operation. In such cases, the relevant clearing member shall 
provide  the  employee  dispatched  by  the  Exchange  with  the  space,  workforce,  equipment 
and other resources necessary for supporting the settlement operation.   

(6)  The  notification  of  details  of  measures  imposed  and  other  necessary  matters  shall  be 
stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.

Section 4. Default Waterfall 

§112. First Use of Assets of Defaulting Clearing Members 

(1)  In  the  case  of  such  events  as  the  settlement  default  by  the  clearing  member,  the 
Exchange  shall  compensate  the  loss  using  the  margin  deposited  by  the  relevant  clearing 
member or other cash or securities that the relevant clearing member is entitled to receive 
(hereinafter referred to as the “deposits by the clearing member”). Provided that, when the 
settlement  default  of  a  clearing  member  is  not  caused  by  its  client,  and  if  the  clearing 
member presents proof of such fact to the Exchange, the compensation shall not be made 
using the margin and other assets of the client, which the clearing member has deposited to 
the Exchange.   

(2) In cases where it is difficult to promptly inject liquidity using the assets deposited by the 
default  clearing  member,  the  Exchange  may  use  the  resources  such  as  the  line  of  credit 
established  with  the  securities  finance  companies  or  financial  institutions  engaged  in 
banking business. In such cases, the Exchange shall recover the line of credit used with the 
deposits by the clearing member noted in paragraph (1).   

(3) The line of credit noted in paragraph (2) and other necessary matters shall be stipulated 
in the Enforcement Rules.   

§113. Loss Compensation using OTC Derivatives JCF and Settlement Reserve   

(1)  In  cases  where  it  is  not  possible  to  fully  compensate  the  loss  pursuant  to  [§112],  the 
Exchange shall compensate the loss using the defaulting clearing member’s contribution to 
the OTC derivatives JCF.   

(2)  In  cases  where  the  loss  cannot  be  fully  compensated  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1),  to 
compensate the loss resulting from non-fulfillment of obligation subsequent to the clearing 
of cleared transactions, the Exchange shall compensate the loss using a part (maximum of 
50/100 of settlement reserve) of the cash, etc. reserved on its own (hereinafter referred to as 
the “settlement reserve”).   

(3) If the loss cannot be fully compensated pursuant to paragraph  (2), the Exchange shall 
compensate  the  loss  using  the  OTC  derivatives  JCF  contributed  by  the  non-defaulting 
clearing members (if the settlement default has occurred during the receipt period noted in 
[§26(4)], the amount that the non-defaulting clearing members are required to contribute to 
the  OTC  derivatives  JCF).  In  such  cases,  the  contributions  of  non-defaulting  clearing 
members  shall  be  used  according  to  the  sequence  noted  in  each  of  the  following 
subparagraphs,  but  the  sequence  specified  in  the  Enforcement  Rules  shall  apply  to  the 
clearing members in the same category:   

1.  Contributions  of  the  clearing  members  who  did  not  participate  in  the  auction 
(hereinafter referred to as the “non-participating clearing members”);

members”); and   

3.  The  contributions  of the clearing  member  who  participated  in  the  auction  and  was

(4)  In  cases  where  the  loss  cannot  be  fully  compensated  pursuant  paragraph  (3),  the

(5) In cases where it is difficult to promptly inject the liquidity using the financial resources

CHAPTER X. MANAGEMENT OF CLEARING OPERATION 

§118. Employer’s Liability of the Exchange 

The Exchange may delegate a part of clearing operation to a third party. In such cases, in 
the event that the party that accepted the delegation from the Exchange inflicts any damage 
on a clearing member or a client in the course of performing the delegated task, Article 756 
of the Civil Act shall apply mutatis mutandis thereto.   

§119. Position Limits of Cleared Transactions   

(1)  Each  clearing  member  shall  manage  the  holding  size  of  cleared  transactions  not  to 
exceed the position limit ratio (referring to the ratio of the total contract amount of cleared 
transactions  of  the  relevant  clearing  member  to  the  total  contract  amount  of  cleared 
transactions  of  all  clearing  members)  based  on  the  contract  amounts  specified  in  the 
Enforcement Rules.   

(2) Clearing members shall manage the position size of cleared transactions not to exceed 
the position limit ratio based on the member net exposure margin (referring to the ratio of 
net  exposure  member  margin  amount  of  relevant  clearing  member  to  the  net  exposure 
member margin amount of all clearing members) specified in the Enforcement Rules.   

(3) The Exchange may impose the measures specified in the Enforcement Rules, such as the 
charge  of  extra  net  exposure  member  margin,  to  the  clearing  member  that  violates  the 
position limit specified in paragraphs (1) and (2).   

(4) The methods for calculating the total contract amount noted on paragraph (1) and the net 
exposure member margin amount noted in paragraph (2) and other necessary matters shall 
be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules. [November 30, 2016]
