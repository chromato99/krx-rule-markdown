CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS 

§1. Purpose 

The purpose of this Guideline is to stipulate the necessary matters with respect to the methods, 
procedures and etc. by which the Korea Exchange determines the variables for calculation of 
margin  amount  including  the  margin  rates  of  the  Exchange’s  securities  markets  and 
derivatives  market  pursuant  to  [§33]  and  [Annex  5]  of  the  Enforcement  Rules  of  the 
Securities  Market  Clearing  and  Settlement  Business  Regulation  and  [§94],  [§134]  and 
[Annex 19] of the Enforcement Rules of Derivatives Market Business Regulation. (Amended 
on February 15, 2019; May 6, 2021; March 4, 2025) 

§2. Definitions 

(1) The definitions of terms used in this Guideline are as follows: (Amended on February 15, 
2019) 

1. The term “securities markets” refer to the KOSPI Market, the KOSDAQ Market and 
the KONEX Market established by the Korea Exchange (hereinafter referred to as “the 
Exchange”)  for  the  trading  of  securities  pursuant  to  the  [§8-2(4)1]  of  the  Financial 
Investment Services and Capital Markets Act (hereinafter referred to as “the Act”). 

2.  The  term  “derivatives  market”  refers  to  the  Derivatives  Market  established  by  the 
Exchange for the trading of Exchange-traded derivatives pursuant to the [§8-2(4)2] of 
the Act. 

3. The term “securities type” refers to the stocks, depository receipts of foreign stocks, 
stocks of real estate investment companies, stocks of ship investment companies, stocks 
of investment companies, stocks of infra fund companies, listed beneficiary certificates, 
collective investment securities of exchange-traded funds (including foreign exchange-
traded funds; hereinafter the same applies), exchange-traded notes, stocks of investment 
company  for  business  development  collective  investment  vehicle,  investment  trust 
beneficiary certificates of business development collective investment vehicle, equity-
linked warrants, subscription warrants, and subscription right certificates. (Amended on 
April 17, 2026) 

4. The  term  “market  index”  refers  to  the  indices  calculated  by  the  Exchange,  such  as 
Korea Composite Stock Price Index (KOSPI) of the KOSPI Market, KOSDAQ Stock 

4 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Price Index (KOSDAQ Index) of the KOSDAQ Market, and KONEX Stock Price Index 
(KONEX Index) of the KONEX Market. 

5. The term “sector index” refers to  the indices  by industrial  sector, calculated by the 
Exchange, of the KOSPI Market and KOSDAQ Market. 

6. The term “issue price” refers to the closing price of an issue established in the regular 
sessions, the special quotation price in the case where there is no transaction matched for 
the issue on the day, or the base price in the case where there is no special quotation price 
applicable. 

7.  The  term  “margin  calculation  variables”  refers  to  the  variables  for  calculation  of 
margin amount such as the margin rates of the Exchange’s securities markets (including 
the transactions at multilateral trading companies; the same hereinafter) and derivatives 
markets. (Amended on March 4, 2025) 

(2) Other terms used in this Guideline shall have the same meanings given to them in the Act 
and the business regulations of the Exchange except as otherwise provided in this Guideline. 
(Amended on February 15, 2019) 

§3. Basic Principles 

(1)  The  Exchange  shall  determine  margin  calculation  variables  in  consideration  of  the 
international standards such as the Principles for Financial Market Infrastructures (“PFMIs”) 
published jointly by the Committee on Payments and Markets Infrastructures of the Bank for 
International  Settlements  (CPMI)  and  the  International  Organization  of  Securities 
Commissions (IOSCO). 

(2)  The  Exchange  may  determine  the  margin  calculation  variables  by  taking  into  account 
various situations such as the characteristics of each product, market situations and objective 
quantitative information, etc.   

CHAPTER II. MARGIN CALCULATION VARIABLES OF SECURITIES MARKETS 

§4. Securities Markets Margin Rates 

(1) The member margin rates of an issue traded in the securities markets shall be determined 
for each securities type to which the concerned issue belongs. 

5 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(2) The member margin rates by securities type shall be determined by taking into account 
various factors such as the characteristics of the securities type and market conditions, etc., 
and adjusted in a timely manner based on the price volatilities of the issues belonging to the 
concerned securities type or the related indices such as the market index and sector index, etc. 
(hereinafter referred to as the “related index”). 

§5. Measurement of Price Volatility of Securities Markets Issue and Related Index 

(1) The price volatility of the issue or the related index noted in [§4(2)], which serves as the 
basis for the calculation of margin rates shall be measured in accordance with [Annex 1] and 
[Annex 2]. (Amended on November 25, 2021) 

(2)  Notwithstanding  Paragraph  (1),  the  Exchange  may  determine  the  price  volatility 
differently in cases when it deems necessary for market management. 
[May 6, 2021] 

§6. Calculation of Margin Rates by Securities Type 

The member margin rates shall be the value calculated by the method specified in [Annex 3] 
based on the price volatility pursuant to [§5]. 

[November 25, 2021] 

§7. Regular Change of Securities Market Margin Rates 

(1) The Exchange shall change the member margin rates in the securities market on the first 
Monday (if it falls on a market holiday, it shall be the next trading day; hereinafter referred 
to as the “regular change date” in this Article) of every month. In such cases, the details of 
the changes shall be notified to members, etc. at least three (3) trading days prior to the regular 
change date. (Amended on May 6, 2021; August 16, 2022) 

(2)  The  member  margin  rates  changed  in  accordance  with  Paragraph  (1)  shall  be  a  value 
obtained based on the price volatility measured on the 7th trading day prior to the regular 
change date (hereinafter referred to as the “regular calculation base date” in this Article) and 
decided by the Margin Rates Adjustment Council (hereinafter referred to as the “Council” in 
this Article. (Amended on August 16, 2022) 

(3)    The Council meeting for the regular change of the margin rates in the securities market 
shall be held on the 4th trading day preceding the regular change date. [August 16, 2022]

regular  calculation  base  date  and  the  Council  meeting  date  if  he/she  deems  it  inevitable. 
[August 16, 2022] 

§8. Irregular Change of Securities Market Margin Rates 

The Exchange may change the member margin rates by reviewing the adequacy of member 
margin rates from time to time when it deems necessary for market management due to an 
abrupt  change  in  the  market  situation,  etc.  In  such  cases,  the  Exchange  shall  notify  the 
members, etc. of the details of the change without delay. 

§9. Unit of Change of Securities Market Margin Rates 

In the case of changing member margin rates pursuant to [§7] and [§8], the minimum increase 
or decrease unit shall be 0.01 percent point.

§12. Regular Change of Derivatives Market Margin Rates 

(1) The Exchange shall change the member margin rates in the derivatives market on the first 
Monday (if it falls on a market holiday, it shall be the next business day; hereinafter referred 
to as the “regular change date” in this Article) of every month. In such cases, the details of 
the changes shall be notified to  members, etc. at  least  three (3) business  days prior to  the 
regular change date. (Amended on May 6, 2021; August 16, 2022; June 9, 2025) 

(2)  The  member  margin  rates  changed  in  accordance  with  Paragraph  (1)  shall  be  a  value 
obtained  based  on  the  price  volatility  measured  at  the  time  of  market  closing  on  the  7th 
business  day  prior  to  the  regular  change  date  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  the  “regular 
calculation base date” in this Article) and decided by the Margin Rates Adjustment Council 
(hereinafter referred to as the “Council” in this Article. (Amended on August 16, 2022; June 
9, 2025) 

(3)    The Council meeting for the regular change of the margin rates in the derivatives market 
shall be held on the 4th business day preceding the regular change date. [August 16, 2022] 
(Amended on June 9, 2025) 

(4)  Notwithstanding  Paragraphs  (2)  and  (3),  chairperson  of  the  Council  may  change  the 
regular  calculation  base  date  and  the  Council  meeting  date  if  he/she  deems  it  inevitable. 
[August 16, 2022] 

§13. Irregular Change of Derivatives Market Margin Rates 

The Exchange may change the member margin rates by reviewing the adequacy of member 
margin rates from time to time when it deems necessary for market management due to an 
abrupt change in market situation, etc. In such cases, the Exchange shall notify the members, 
etc. of the details of the change without delay. 

§14. Temporary Adjustment of Derivatives Market Margin Rates during Holidays 

(1) In cases where the market holidays continue for three (3) consecutive days or longer and 
the Exchange deems it necessary for market management, the member margin rates may be 
temporarily adjusted. (Amended on February 15, 2019; June 9, 2025) 

(2) When making a temporary adjustment of the member margin rates during holidays, the 
Exchange shall notify the members of the adjustment schedule and the extent of adjustment 
by  10  business  days  and  five  (5)  business  days  prior  to  the  adjustment  date,  respectively. 
(Amended on June 9, 2025) 

8 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(3) The temporary adjustment of member margin rates during holidays shall not apply to the 
calculation of the customer margins and the credit risk loss equivalent under [§94-2(1)] of 
the “Enforcement Rules of Derivatives Market Business Regulation”. (Amended on February 
15, 2019) 

§15. Unit of Change 

In the case of changing member margin rates, the minimum change unit shall be 0.10 percent 
point for the index and equity derivatives, 0.01 percent point for the interest rate derivatives 
and the currency derivatives and 0.10 percent point for the commodity derivatives. 

§16. Customer Maintenance Margin Rates and Customer Margin Rates 

(1)  The  rates  of  the  customer  maintenance  margins  shall  be  the  same  as  the  rates  of  the 
member margins. 

(2) The customer margin rates shall be set at 1.5 times the customer maintenance margin rates. 
However,  when  it  is  deemed  necessary  for  market  management,  the  Exchange  may  set  it 
differently. (Amended on May 6, 2021) 

§17. Composition of Product Group 

The Exchange shall establish product groups to which the margin reduction is applied in 
accordance with [Annex 4] by comprehensively considering the quantitative analysis of 
correlations among underlying assets’ rates of return (referring to the value obtained by 
dividing the difference between the closing prices of the underlying asset on the concerned 
day and the day before by the closing price of the day before; hereinafter the same shall 
apply in this Chapter) and the qualitative factors such as the similarity of underlying assets’ 
characteristics and the industry sectors, etc.     

[Wholly amended on December 29, 2022] 

§18. Determination of Price Correlation Rates 

The  Exchange  shall  set  the  price  correlation  rate  of  a  product  group  in  accordance  with 
[Annex 4] by considering the level of movement to the same direction of the rates of return 
among underlying assets in the same product group. 

[Wholly amended on December 29, 2022] 

9 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
§19. Relative Scale Rates 

The  Exchange  shall  establish  the  relative  scale  rates  in  accordance  with  [Annex  4]  by 
considering the multipliers and the prices of underlying assets of each product in the same 
product group. 

[Wholly amended on December 29, 2022] 

§20. Change of Variables for Calculation of Margin Reduction Amount 

(1) The Exchange shall review the adequacy of the composition of product groups, the price 
correlation rates, and the relative scale rates (hereinafter referred to as the “reduction amount 
calculation variables”) twice a year and change the reduction amount calculation variables 
when  it  is  deemed  necessary.  However,  the  Exchange  may  not  conduct  the  review  of  the 
adequacy  of  the  reduction  amount  calculation  variables  in  cases  where  the  change  in  the 
market situation is insignificant. 

(2) The Exchange shall notify the details of the change to the members, etc. at least by three 
(3)  business  days  prior  to  the  application  date  of  the  change  in  the  reduction  amount 
calculation variables. (Amended on June 9, 2025) 

§21. Futures Spreads Margin Rates 

The member margin rates of futures spreads shall be set considering the differences in price 
movements  among  delivery month futures contracts,  price levels  of the underlying assets, 
and the characteristics of products, etc. 

§22. Minimum Margin per Contract 

The minimum member margin per contract shall be calculated considering the cost of spreads, 
etc. for the disposition of open interests.

§23. Operation of Council 

(1) The Margin Rates Adjustment Council (hereinafter referred to as “the Council”) shall be 
in  operation  for  the  deliberation  of  matters  on  review  and  change  of  margin  rates  of  the 
securities and derivatives markets. 

10 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(2) The Council shall be composed of a chairperson and council members as classified in 
each of the following subparagraphs and have a secretary in charge of handling the matters 
on council business including preparation of meeting materials. (Amended on September 28, 
2018; February 15, 2019; March 27, 2019; April 16, 2021; May 6, 2021) 

1. Chairperson who is the officer in charge of CCP risks pursuant to [§7-2(2)] of the Risk 
Management Committee Operation Regulation;   

2.  Council  members  who  are  the  heads  of  the  department  in  charge  of  clearing  and 
settlement, CCP risk management department, and CCP risk assessment department; and 

3. Secretary who is the head of the team in charge of margin-related tasks. 

(3) The Council may take opinions from the heads of the following departments or outside 
experts when necessary: (Amended on May 6, 2021) 

1.  Securities market: Heads of departments related to system and operational affairs of 

the securities markets; and 

2.  Derivatives market: Heads of departments related to system and operational affairs 

of the derivatives market. 

(4) Notwithstanding the provisions of [§6] and [§11], the Council may set the margin rates 
by comprehensively considering such factors as the domestic and global market situations, 
outlook on the price volatilities and influence on the settlement stability, etc. (Amended on 
March 4, 2025) 

(5) Notwithstanding Paragraph (1), the Council meeting may be replaced by a written meeting 
in  consideration  of  the  scope  of  adjustment  of  the  member  margin  rates,  the  number  of 
products subject to member margin rates change, etc., and in the case of an inevitable situation 
such as an urgent irregular change of the member margin rates, the meeting of the Council 
may be omitted. (Amended on March 27, 2019) 

§24. Management of Margin Calculation Variables 

The  matters  necessary  for  the  determination  of  the  margin  calculation  variables  including 
those not stipulated in this Guideline shall be determined by the head of the department in 
charge of managing the margin calculation variables.

§25. Assessment of Methods for Determining Securities Market Margin Rates 

(Amended on May 6, 2021) 

(1) The Exchange shall periodically check the appropriateness of the methods for determining 
the member margin rates in the securities markets through ex-post verification of whether the 
amount  of  the  securities  markets  member  margin  per  clearing  member  is  sufficient  to 
compensate the actual losses. 

(2) In order to assess the adequacy of Paragraph (1), the Exchange may, in respect of each 
trading  day,  calculate  the  number  of  days  in  which  the  loss  amount  (it  shall  be  the  loss 
equivalents incurred as of the market closing times of the concerned day and two (2) trading 
days  thereafter)  of  each  clearing  member  exceeds  the  net  exposure  margin  amount  of  the 
securities  markets  during  the  immediately  preceding  250  trading  days  and  change  the 
methods for determining the member margin rates in consideration of the foregoing number 
of days calculated in cases where it deems necessary for market management. (Amended on 
May 6, 2021) 

§26. Assessment of Methods for Calculating Derivatives Market Margin Rates 

(1) The Exchange shall periodically check the appropriateness of the methods for determining 
the member margin rates of the derivatives market through an ex-post verification of whether 
the amount of the derivatives market member margin per clearing member is sufficient to 
compensate the actual losses. (Amended on May 6, 2021) 

(2) Pursuant to Paragraph (1), the Exchange may change the methods for determining the 
member margin rates in cases where it deems necessary for market management considering 
the number of days in which the loss amount (it shall be the loss equivalents incurred as of 
the market closing times of the concerned day and two (2) trading days thereafter) of each 
clearing member exceeds the member margin amount of the derivatives market during the 
immediately preceding 250 trading days in respect of each trading day. (Amended on May 6, 
2021)

The member margin rate of the derivatives market shall be the larger value of the base member 
margin rate and the minimum margin rate calculated for each underlying asset or the relevant 
product by applying Subparagraph 1 mutatis mutandis.

1. Composition of Products, etc. 

    A. Product group subject to calculation of margin reduction amount 

    The product groups for calculating the margin reduction amount are classified as follows 

based on the underlying assets of the derivatives: 

(1) Interest rate product group; 
(2) Currency product group; 
(3) Stock index product group; 
(4) Equity product group (categorized by industry); and 
(5) Commodity product group. 

B. Selection of Underlying Assets by Product Group 

    When the Exchange conducts the regular review of variables for calculating the margin 
reduction amount pursuant to [§20(1)], it shall select the underlying assets belonging to the 
relevant product group in the following order: 

        (1) To compose the underlying assets belonging to each product group considering the 
market classification under [§3] of the Derivatives Market Business Regulation and the 
industry classification method under the Global Industry Classification Standard (GICS). 
Provided that, if there is only one (1) underlying asset in a product group, the relevant 
product group is excluded from the calculation of margin reduction amount; 

        (2)  To  set  the  sample  period  corresponding  to  the  250,  500,  and  750  trading  days 
immediately before the review date of variables for calculating margin reduction amount; 
and 

        (3) To  calculate,  for  each  underlying  asset  (X),  the  same  directional  probability  with 
other underlying assets (Y) in the same product group for each sample period according
