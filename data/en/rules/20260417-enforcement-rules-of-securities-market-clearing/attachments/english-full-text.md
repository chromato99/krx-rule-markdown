CHAPTER I. COMMON PROVISIONS 

§1. Purpose 

The  purpose  of  these  Enforcement  Rules  is  to  stipulate  the  matters  necessary  for  the 
enforcement of the Securities Market Clearing and Settlement Business Regulation.   

§2. Definitions 

The meanings of terms used in these Enforcement Rules shall be in accordance with the 
provisions  of  the  Securities  Market  Clearing  and  Settlement  Business  Regulation 
(hereinafter referred to as the “Regulation”). 

CHAPTER II. CLEARING AND SETTLEMENT IN SECURITIES MARKET 

Section 1. General Provisions 

§3. Exclusions from Application of  Clearing and  Settlement to Securities  Finance 
Companies, etc. 

(1) “Cases stipulated in the Enforcement Rules” in the proviso to main sentence of [§5(1)] 
of the Regulation shall refer to cases where, as a case that a securities finance company 
fails to complete its settlement obligation, the Exchange deems it necessary to ensure the 
stability of clearing and settlement. 

(2) Pursuant to [§5(1)2] of the Regulation, transactions financed by a securities finance 
company include transactions conducted by clearing members to repay funds or securities 
borrowed from the securities finance company. 

§4.  Clearing  Member’s  Request  for  Correction  of  Error  Trades  in  Transactions 
Financed by Securities Finance Company, etc. 

(1)  Pursuant  to  [§5(5)]  of  the  Regulation,  the  request  for  correction  of  error  trades  in 
transactions financed by a securities finance company and the contribution of the joint 
compensation fund shall be made by the clearing member who is the counterparty with 
whom  a  delegation  agreement  is  deemed  to  have  been  established  with  the  securities 
finance company in accordance with the latter part of [§5(1)2] of the Regulation.   

(2)  In the case where the  Exchange uses the  joint  compensation  fund for the payment 
amount  that  a  securities  finance  company  has  not  made  (hereinafter  referred  to  as 
“unsettled amount” in this article), the joint compensation fund usage amount by clearing

member shall be the amount calculated by proportionally allocating the variable reserve 
for  transactions  financed  by  securities  finance  company  (hereinafter  referred  to  as 
“variable reserve for transactions financed by securities finance company”) accumulated 
by each clearing member as of the date of using the joint compensation fund (any amount 
less  than  KRW  10,000  shall  be  discarded).  In  this  case,  if  the  total  amount  of  the 
calculated amount is less than the unsettled amount, clearing members shall add KRW 
10,000 each in the order of the member with the largest amount discarded because it is 
less than KRW 10,000 (if discarded amounts are the same, they shall be in the order of 
the largest variable reserve) until the unsettled amount is reached. 

(3) If the total amount of variable reserve for transactions financed by securities finance 
company for each member calculated in accordance with paragraph (2) is less than the 
unsettled  amount,  the  amount  used  by  each  clearing  member  for  the  corresponding 
shortfall  shall  be  the  amount  (any  amount  less  than  KRW  10,000  shall  be  discarded) 
calculated in proportion from the amount produced by subtracting the variable reserve for 
transactions financed by securities finance company of the relevant clearing member from 
the variable reserve of the securities market joint compensation fund contributed by each 
clearing member as of the date of use. In this case, the latter part of paragraph (2) shall 
apply. 

§5. Details of Target Trades 

(1) “Case stipulated in the Enforcement Rules” in [§6(1)2] of the Regulation shall refer 
to  the case where the settlement  date of a trade noted in  paragraph (2)1 is  the reserve

previous trading day). 

(2) “Cases stipulated in the Enforcement Rules” in [§6(2)] of the Regulation shall refer 
to any of the following subparagraphs: 

    1. Case of trades at the KRX KTB noted in [§55(3)] of the KOSPI Market Business 

Regulations; and 

    2. Case of trades of debt securities other than those in subparagraph 1. 

(3)  Trades  noted  in  paragraph  (2)1  shall  be  T+1  settlement  trades,  and  those  noted  in 
paragraph (2)2 and buy-in trades by the Exchange noted in [§22] of the Regulation shall 
settled on the same day as the trade date.

multilateral trading company shall be as follows:

Section 2. Methods of Clearing and Settlement in Securities Market 

§7. Notification of Settlement Details of Transactions Financed by Securities Finance 
Company 

Pursuant  to  [§9(4)]  of  the  Regulation,  any  clearing  member  (excluding  the  securities 
finance  company)  who  conducted  the  trades  financed  by  securities  finance  companies 
shall  notify  the  Exchange  the  settlement  details  specified  in  each  of  the  following 
subparagraphs  subsequent  to  conducting  the  trades  financed  by  securities  finance 
companies on the day the trade is conducted. In such cases, when there is a change of 
settlement details as a result of the correction of transaction error, the settlement details 
of the trade financed by securities finance companies shall be notified to the Exchange 
by the day following the trading day. 

1. Trade execution date and settlement date; 

2. Issue name; 

3. Settlement details noted in each of the following items:   

(a) In the case of sale to be settled with the borrowed securities (hereinafter referred 
to as “the selling trade through securities lending”) : quantity and amount of sale         

(b) In the case of purchase conducted to payback the securities borrowed: purchased 
quantity and sale amount at the time of selling trade through securities lending of the 
relevant securities       

(c) In the case of purchase to be settled with the cash loan (hereinafter referred to as 
“the buying trade through margin loan”): purchased quantity and amount borrowed 
(referring to the amount obtained by subtracting the amount of the collateral noted

4. Matters that are deemed necessary for the clearing and settlement of the trade financed

§9. Calculation Method of Settlement Securities and Settlement Amounts for Trades 
in KRX KTB   

(1) Pursuant to [§9(4)] of the Regulation, in cases where the number (referring to the par 
value; the same hereinafter in this Article) of settlement securities subject to netting noted 
in [§9(1)2] of the Regulation exceeds KRW 10 billion, the amount shall be determined 
by splitting it less than KRW 10 billion.   

(2) The amount of settlement payment for settlement securities ascertained pursuant to 
paragraph (1) shall be determined according to method noted in each of the following 
subparagraphs:   

1. Settlement payment in cases where the number of settlement securities is KRW 10 
billion: 

The amount shall be determined first by multiplying ten (10) billion by the quantity 
obtained by dividing total settlement payment of the relevant issue by total number of 
settlement securities. In such cases, if the result has an amount less than KRW 10,000, 
amounts less than KRW 5,000 shall be discarded, and amounts of KRW 5,000 or more 
but less than KRW 10,000 shall be rounded up to KRW 10,000.   

2. Settlement payment in cases where the number of settlement securities is less than 
KRW 10 billion: 

The amount shall be determined by subtracting the sum of settlement payment noted 
in  subparagraph 1  from  the  total  settlement  payment of the  relevant  issue.  In such 
cases, if the total settlement payment of the relevant issue is multiples of KRW 10 
billion, the settlement payment determined very last shall be deemed to be less than 
KRW 10 billion.

§13. Calculation Method of Maximum Quantity of Receivable Security, etc. 

(1) The maximum quantity of receivable security noted in the former part of the part other 
than each paragraph of [§12(1)] shall be the amount resulting from subtracting the amount

4 per issue subject to netting. In such cases, the entire amount noted in subparagraph 3 
shall be included when calculating the maximum quantity of receivable security for each 
issue subject to netting:   

1. Settlement payment that the relevant clearing member has paid into the payment 
settlement account; 

2.  Sum  of  the  appraisal  amount  of  settlement  securities  that  the  relevant  clearing 
member has delivered into the securities settlement account;

§31. Special Cases Concerning Securities Settlement   

(1) Cash or a similar issue that substitutes the issue to be settled at the settlement deadline

(2) In the case of making the settlement in cash instead of the original issue, the amount

Exchange Transaction Regulation: 

1. The amount obtained by multiplying the quantity not settled by the closing price of 
the original issue on the trading day; 

2. The amount obtained by multiplying the amount under subparagraph 1 by 20% (5% 
for debt securities); and 

3. The amount obtained by multiplying the quantity not settled by the closing price of 
the original issue on the date of cash payment (in cases where the  delivery is made 
before  the  determination  of  the  closing  price  of  the  day,  the  closing  price  of  the 
previous day shall be used; the same hereinafter in this Article).   

(3) In the case of intending to settle with a similar issue, instead of the original issue, the

quantity of such issue shall be the number obtained by dividing the amount determined 
in accordance with paragraph (2) by the closing price of the delivery day of the relevant

multiplying  the  quantity  of  each  issue  by  the  closing  price  on  the  delivery  day  of  the

1. Member margins 

Member margins shall be calculated by aggregating the margin amounts produced by a 
member’s self-account group and customer account group, respectively, and the margin 
amount of each (self and customer) account group shall be calculated by aggregating the 
net  exposure  margins  and  variable  margins.  Provided  that,  in  cases  where  the  margin 
amount is negative, the amount shall be counted as zero (‘0’) and the member margin of 
a  customer  account  group  shall  be  the  amount  adjusted  (hereinafter  referred  to  as 
“adjusted  member  margin  of  customer  account  group”)  by  multiplying  it  by  a  certain 
number (hereinafter referred to as “adjustment factor”). 

Member  Margin  =  Self-account  group’s  member  margin  +  Adjusted  member  margin  of 

customer account group 

Self-account Group’s Member Margin = Max{Self-account group’s net exposure margins + 
Variable margin, 0} 

Adjusted Member Margin of Customer Account Group = Customer account group’s member 
margin × Adjustment factor 

Customer Account Group’s Member Margin = Max{Customer account group’s net exposure 

margin + Variable margin, 0} 

2. Net exposure margins 

Net exposure margin shall be an amount equivalent to the net loss that may occur from 
the time when trading hours in the securities market end (in the case of the net exposure 
margin  for  calculation  of  intraday  member  margins,  it  shall  the  time  of  calculating 
intraday  member  margins;  the  same  hereinafter  in  this  Subparagraph)  to  the  time  of 
disposal of the quantity of net purchase (or sale),when the price or value of stocks, etc. 
(hereinafter referred to as “securities” in this Annex) changes by a certain level, and the 
amount of net exposure margin shall be the larger of the amounts obtained by aggregating 
the net exposure margins per issue calculated based on the time when trading hours in the 
securities market  end  for each of  self-account group and customer  account  group of a 
member  by  the  net  purchase  and  the  net  sale  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “net  exposure 
margins for net purchase” and “net exposure margins for net sale”, respectively)

intraday member margin base price per issueand‘-1’.
