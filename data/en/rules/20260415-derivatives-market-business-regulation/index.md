PARTI. GENERAL PROVISIONS 

CHAPTER I. COMMON PROVISIONS 

§1. Purpose 

The  purpose  of  this  Regulation  is  to  stipulate  the  matters  necessary  for  the  transaction, 
settlement and entrustment of exchange-traded derivatives in the derivatives market established 
by the Korea Exchange pursuant to Article 393(2) of the Financial Investment Services and 
Capital Markets Act. 

§2. Definitions 

(1) The terms used in this Regulation shall have the meanings as follows:(Amended on January 
16, 2011; July 3, 2013; August 27, 2014; June 8, 2016; July 10, 2019; July 22, 2020; December 
7, 2022) 

1. The term “derivatives trading” refers to the trading of the exchange-traded derivatives 
prescribed in Article  5(2) of the  Financial  Investment Services  and Capital  Markets Act 
(hereinafter referred to as “the Act”) in the derivatives market (hereinafter referred to as 
“the  Market”)  established  by  the  Korea  Exchange  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  "the 
Exchange"). 

2.  The  term  "futures  trading"  refers  to  a  derivatives  trade  that  falls  under  any  of  the 
followings that are carried out in the Market in accordance with the standards and methods 
stipulated in this Regulation: 

(a) A transaction in which the relevant parties agree to deliver and receive the underlying 
asset at a particular price at a specific time in future; and 

(b) A  transaction  in  which  the  relevant  parties  agree  to  deliver  and  receive  the  cash 
amount  calculated  from  the  difference  between  the  pre-agreed  price  or  interest  rate, 
indicator, unit or index (hereinafter referred to as “the value”) of underlying asset and the 
price or value (hereinafter referred to as “the final settlement price”) of underlying asset 
at a specific time in the future. 

3. The term "options trading" refers to a derivatives trade that is carried out in the Market 
according to the standards and methods stipulated in this Regulation, in which one of the 
relevant parties  agrees to handover the right (hereinafter referred to as “the options”) to 
execute a trade that falls under any of the following transactions according to the intention 
expressed by the counter party and the counter party agrees to pay the price to the other 
party of the relevant parties: 

(a) A transaction of underlying asset; 

1 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(b) A transaction in which the cash amount calculated from the difference between the 
exercise  price  and  the  price  or  value  of  the  underlying  asset  on  the  date  the  option  is 
exercised (hereinafter referred to as “the base price for exercise-settlement”) is delivered 
and received; 

(c) A transaction of futures noted in paragraph (1)2(a); and 

(d) A transaction of futures noted in paragraph (1)2(b). 

4. The term “KOSPI Market” refers to the KOSPI stock market, from among the securities 
markets established by the Exchange pursuant to Article 386 of the Act. 

5. The term  “KOSDAQ  Market”  refers to  the  KOSDAQ  stock market, from among  the 
securities  markets  established  by  the  Exchange  pursuant  to  Article  386  of  the  Act. 
(Amended on June 3, 2014) 

6. The term “member” means a member who is eligible to participate in derivatives trading, 
in whole or in part, in the Market pursuant to [§3(1)] of the Membership Regulation, and 
shall be classified as follows: 

(a) Clearing member: A member, among the clearing members noted in [§3(2)1] of the 
Membership Regulation, who settles under its own name the trades conducted in its own 
name or the trades for which settlement has been entrusted to it by non-clearing members.   

(b) Non-clearing member: A member, among the non-clearing members noted in [§3(2)2] 
of the Membership Regulation, who is allowed to conduct the trades in its own name in 
the Market but is required to entrust the settlement of trades to a clearing member.   

(c) Designated clearing member: A clearing member who has entered into an agreement 
for the settlement of trades entrusted to it by non-clearing members. 

7. The  term  “investment  broker”  means  an  entity  that  has  obtained  the  authorization  to 
engage  in  the  investment  brokerage  business  for  all  or  a  part  of  the  exchange-traded 
derivatives pursuant to Article 12 of the Act. 

8.  The  term  “investment  trader”  means  an  entity  that  has  obtained  the  authorization  to 
engage in the investment trading business for all or a part of the exchange-traded derivatives 
pursuant to Article 12 of the Act. 

9. The term “underlying asset” means the asset against which a futures contract is traded in 
the case of futures trade, and the asset against which the trade is executed according to the 
intention expressed (hereinafter referred to as “the option exercise”) by the buyer in  the 
case of options trade. 

10. The term “exercise price” refers to the pre-determined price or value of underlying asset 
for the trade accomplished by the option exercise.

§62. Contracts, etc. of Intra-product Spreads 

(1) Contracts of intra-product spreads shall be labeled by the futures spread between two (2) 
contracts specified in the Enforcement Rules when trading the futures contracts that are based 
on the same underlying asset and multiplier.(Amended on August 27, 2014; July 10, 2019) 

(2) The price in the intra-product spread trading shall be determined by subtracting the price of 
near month contract (referring to a contract of which the last trading day is closest to the present; 
the same hereinafter) from the price of farther month contract (referring to a contract of which 
the last trading day is furthest from the present; the same hereinafter). Provided that, , in the 
27 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
case of interest rate derivative products, the price shall be determined by subtracting the price 
of farther month contracts from the price of near month contracts. (Amended on August 27, 
2014; July 10, 2019) 

(3) In the intra-product  spread trading, the short position shall mean the buying  of the near 
month contract and selling of the farther month contract. Provided that, in the case of interest 
rate derivatives, it shall refer to the selling of the near month contract and buying of the farther 
month contract. (Amended on August 27, 2014; July 10, 2019) 

(4)  In  the  intra-product  spread  trading,  the  long  position  shall  mean  the  selling  of  the  near 
month contract and buying of the farther month contract. Provided that, in the case of interest

§72. Principles of Individual Auction 

(1) The trades shall be executed by the method of individual auction. 

(2) The individual auction shall be categorized into the periodic call auction and the continuous 
auction. 

(3) The priority of quotations for individual auction shall be determined in accordance with 
each of the following subparagraphs: (Amended on August 27, 2014)

quantity, and in case of quotations with the same quantity, the quotation received earlier
