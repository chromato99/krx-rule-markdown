CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS 

§1. Purpose 

The  purpose  of  this  Regulation  is  for  the  Korea  Exchange  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  the 
“Exchange”)  designated  as  a  trade  repository  pursuant  to  the  Regulations  on  Financial 
Investment  Business  to  prescribe  the  matters  necessary  for  reportable  transactions,  users, 
and reporting, recordkeeping, providing and publishing of trade information, and etc. for the 
sake of enhancing transparency and stability of the Over-The-Counter (hereinafter referred 
to as “OTC”) derivatives market. 

§2. Definitions   

(1) The terms  used in  this  Regulation have their  meanings  given in  each  of the following 
subparagraphs: 

1.  The  term  “KRX-TR”  means  the  Exchange  designated  as  a  trade  repository  by  the 
Financial Services Commission (hereinafter referred to as the “FSC”) pursuant to [§5-
50-2] of the Regulations on Financial Investment Business; 

2. The term “LEI” means a Legal Entity Identifier that identifies a legal entity engaging 
in a financial transaction and is issued in accordance with the ISO 17422 set forth by 
the International Organization for Standardization; 

3. The term “TR system” means a computer system the KRX-TR installs and operates 
to conduct its trade repository services;   

4.  The  term  “UPI”  means  a  unique  product  identifier  that  is  internationally  used  for 
recognizing  and identifying individual  OTC derivatives products and is  issued by the 
Derivatives Service Bureau (DSB) Limited (hereinafter referred to as the “DSB”);   

5. The term “UTI” means a unique transaction identifier that is assigned to each OTC 
derivatives  transaction  for  recognizing  and  identifying  individual  OTC  derivatives 
transactions and is issued in accordance with the Technical Guidance set forth by the 
Committee on Payments and Market  Infrastructure and the International  Organization 
of Securities Commissions;   

6. The term “trade date” means a day when a reportable transaction is concluded for the 
first time pursuant to mutual consent by the parties thereto;   

7.  The  term  “trade  information”  means  information  related  to  reportable  transactions 
that the KRX-TR collects from users;     

1 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
8.  The  term  “trade  repository  service”  means  a  service  that  the  KRX-TR  conducts  in 
relation  to  collecting,  recordkeeping,  providing,  publicly  disclosing  and  etc.  of  trade 
information; 

9.  The  term  “financial  market  infrastructure  institution”  means  an  institution  that 
operates  a  multilateral  system  used  for  the  purposes  of  clearing,  settling  or  recording 
payment, securities, derivatives, and other financial transactions; 

10. The term “related statutes” means statutes related to trade repository services such 
as the Financial Investment Services and Capital Markets Act, the Act on Real Name 
Financial  Transactions  and  Confidentiality,  and  the  Credit  Information  Use  and 
Protection Act; 

11. The term “reportable transaction” means a transaction that shall be reported to the 
KRX-TR  pursuant  to  [§5-50-3(1)]  of  the  Regulations  on  Financial  Investment 
Business;   

12. The term “asset class” means a collection of derivatives products that are grouped 
together based on the types of underlying assets and are classified as follows: 

(a) Interest rate; 

(b) Foreign exchange; 

(c) Equity; 

(d) Credit; and 

(e) Commodity; 

13.  The  term  “user”  means  a  person  who  intends  to  report  information  related  to 
reportable transactions to the KRX-TR or view and/or use trade information kept by the 
KRX-TR by accessing the TR system; and   

14. The term “OTC derivatives transaction” means the transaction of OTC derivatives 
pursuant to [§5(3)] of the Financial Investment Services and Capital Markets Act;   

(2)  Terms  other  than  those  defined  in  paragraph  (1)  in  this  Regulation  have  the  same 
meanings given to them in the related statutes and the regulations pertaining to the business 
of the KRX-TR unless otherwise specified herein. 

§3. Non-business Day 

(1) The KRX-TR suspends trade repository services on the day corresponding to any of the 
following subparagraphs: 

1. Holidays pursuant to the Rules on Holidays of Public Agencies; 

2 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
2. Labor Day pursuant to the Designation of Workers’ Day Act; and 

3. Saturdays 

(2) In the case of natural disasters, wars, incidents, a shutdown of overall functions of social 
infrastructure including electricity and telecommunication system, and other cases where it 
is  deemed  necessary  to  suspend  the  overall  trade  repository  services  for  one  (1)  or  more 
days, the KRX-TR may decide not to carry out the trade repository services by designating 
a temporary non-business day.   

(3) In case where the trade repository services are suspended pursuant to paragraph (2), the 
KRX-TR notifies the reasons for the suspension, and etc. to the users in advance.

CHAPTER II. USERS 

Section 1. User Registration 

§4. User Classification   

(1) Users are classified as follows: 

1.  Reporting  entity:  A  person  who  has  obligations  to  report  trade  information  of 
reportable transactions and falls under any of the followings: 

(a)  Financial investment business entity; or 

(b) Financial investment product clearinghouse (hereinafter referred to as the “CCP”) 

2. Reporting agency: A person to whom a reporting entity in subparagraph 1 delegates 
its trade reporting task. 

3.  Information  user: A person who does not  have reporting obligations but  intends to 
view  and/or  use  trade  information  kept  in  the  KRX-TR  and  falls  under  any  of  the 
followings: 

(a) FSC; 

(b) Financial Supervisory Service (hereinafter referred to as the “FSS”);   

(c) Bank of Korea (hereinafter referred to as “BOK”); or 

(d) A party to a reportable transaction   

§5. Application and Approval for User Registration, etc.   

(1) Any person intending to register as a user of the KRX-TR shall apply for the KRX-TR 
user registration in accordance with the form prescribed by the Enforcement Rules of this 
Regulation (hereinafter referred to as the “Enforcement Rules”). 

(2) The KRX-TR shall examine the application pursuant to paragraph (1), decide whether to 
approve the application and notify the applicant of the result within 5 business days counted 
from the application date: Provided, That, the examination period may be extended for up to 
30 days if deemed necessary by the KRX-TR.     

(3) In the case of finding a deficiency in the submitted application, the KRX-TR may ask 
the applicant to supplement the application and may undertake an onsite investigation such 

4 

 
 
 
 
 
 
   
   
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
as a meeting with executive officers and/or employees of the applicant if deemed necessary 
to verify the contents of the said application.   

(4)  In  cases  where  the  KRX-TR  approves  the  application  for  registration  pursuant  to 
paragraph (2), the KRX-TR shall announce such approval on the TR system or the internet 
homepage.   

(5) Matters necessary for a user registration application and approval other than those in the 
paragraphs from (1) through (4) are prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

§6. Issuance of User ID 

(1) In cases where the KRX-TR approves an application for registration in accordance with 
[§5(2)], the KRX-TR issues and informs a user ID to the applicant.   

(2) In cases where the KRX-TR issues a user ID in paragraph (1), the KRX-TR issues one 
user ID for one LEI of an applicant: Provided, That, the KRX-TR may issue two or more 
IDs for one LEI if prescribed by the Enforcement Rules.   

Section 2. Termination of User Registration, etc. 

§7. Termination   

(1) A user shall request a termination of user registration from the KRX-TR in accordance 
with  the  form  prescribed  by  the  Enforcement  Rules  in  cases  where  the  user  intends  to 
voluntarily terminate the user registration. 

(2) In cases where a request for termination of a user registration pursuant to paragraph (1) 
has  been  submitted,  the  KRX-TR  checks  whether  the  reporting  obligations  of  the  user 
ceased  to  exist.  If  user  obligations  including  payment  of  user  fees  pursuant  to  [§51]  are 
fulfilled, the KRX-TR decides on the termination date and notify the user thereof.   

(3) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), the KRX-TR may terminate user registration without the 
user’s consent in any of the following cases:   

1.  Where the user was approved for the user registration by fraud or illegal means; 

2.  Where  the  user  who  is  also  a  reporting  entity  falls  under  all  of  the  following 
subparagraphs:   

(a) Where reporting obligations of a reporting entity have ceased due to revocation 
of its authorization for its financial investment business or discontinuance or transfer 
of its business or etc.; and 

5 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(b) Where there is no remaining balance of a transaction concluded by its name.   

3. Where the user has not paid the user fees that shall be paid to the KRX-TR for equal 
to or more than 3 months; or 

4. Other cases where the KRX-TR deems it necessary to terminate user registration for 
maintenance or management of trade repository services.   

(4)  Where  the  KRX-TR  terminates  user  registration  pursuant  to  paragraph  (2)  or  (3),  it 
notifies such termination to the relevant user and announce it on the TR system or internet 
homepage. 

§8. Succession of User Status   

(1) In cases where a user terminates registration because of a merger or a business transfer, 
the surviving or newly-established corporation resulted from such merger or the corporation 
taking  over  the  business  may  succeed  the  user  status  by  receiving  an  approval  from  the 
KRX-TR. 

(2) In cases where a corporation intending to succeed the user status pursuant to paragraph 
(1) is not a registered user, the KRX-TR issues and notifies a new user ID to the corporation 
in accordance with [§6(1)]. 

(3) Matters necessary for the succession of the user status other than paragraph (1) and (2) 
are prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

Section 3. User Obligations 

§9. Compliance with Laws and Regulations   

A user shall comply with the related statutes and this Regulation as well as the disciplinary 
actions and other measures taken pursuant thereto. 

§10. Non-Disclosure Obligations 

A user and its officers and employees shall keep trade information that it obtained through 
the  TR  system  confidential  and  shall  not  let  others,  excluding  the  other  party  to  the 
reportable transaction and its reporting agency, use such trade information. 

6 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
§11. Due Diligence and Principle of Good Faith 

In carrying out trade reporting tasks through the KRX-TR, a user shall observe due care and 
diligence and shall not engage in any actions that violate the principle of good faith. 

§12. Notification to the KRX-TR   

In the event falling under any of the following cases, a user shall, without delay, notify the 
KRX-TR  of  the  concerned  facts  or  decisions  (referring  to  the  resolutions  made  at  the 
general meeting of shareholders or the meeting of the board of directors, or the decision of 
the chief executive officer and etc. of the user): 

1. Where the cause of dissolution has occurred;   

2.  Where  dispositions  such  as  revocation  of  authorization  or  business  suspension  of 
financial investment business have been imposed; 

3. Where the business of headquarters or branch offices have been suspended, resumed, 
or discontinued;   

4. Where there has been a merger, discontinuance of business, or transfer or acquisition 
of the whole or a main part of business;   

5. Where the information a user had provided at the time of user registration such as its 
company name changed; or 

6. Other cases where an incident that has a significant impact on the fulfillment of trade 
reporting tasks has occurred. 

§13. Providing Additional Information to the KRX-TR 

(1) The KRX-TR  may  request  a user to  submit a standard  agreement on OTC derivatives 
such  as  the  ISDA  Master  Agreement,  a  trade  confirmation,  and  other  supplementary 
documents  in  relation  to  a  reportable  transaction  in  cases  where  it  deems  necessary  for 
confirming  accuracy  of  trade  information  and  resolving  conflicts  related  to  trade 
information.   

(2)  In  the  case  of  receiving  a  request  for  additional  information  in  accordance  with 
paragraph (1), the user shall respond to such request without delay

CHAPTER III. REPORTING TRADE INFORMATION   

Section 1. Reportable Transactions   

§14. Reportable Transactions     

(1)  Reportable  transactions  of  a  financial  investment  business  entity  are  OTC  derivatives 
transactions that are concluded in the financial investment business entity’s own name (In 
the  case  of  a  collective  investment  business  entity,  reportable  transactions  include  OTC 
derivatives transactions  concluded in  the name of a  collective investment scheme that  the 
collective investment business entity manages). 

(2) Reportable transactions of the CCP are each of the following transactions: 

1.  An  OTC  derivatives  transaction  pertaining  to  which  a  member  of  the  CCP 
(hereinafter referred to as “clearing member” in this Article) has requested that the said 
CCP shall assume obligations (excluding a transaction pertaining to which registration 
of  assumption  of  obligations  was  rejected  since  the  transaction  had  failed  to  satisfy 
requirements set forth by the CCP.); 

2. A transaction between the CCP and a clearing member that is established as the CCP 
assumes obligations of the transaction prescribed in subparagraph 1; and 

3. In the case where a clearing member is not a party to the transaction in subparagraph 
1, a transaction construed to be established between the clearing member and a party to 
the said transaction as soon as the transaction in subparagraph 2 is concluded.   

(3)  Matters  necessary  for  reportable  transactions  other  than  paragraph  (1)  and  (2)  are 
prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

§15.  Exemption  of  Reporting  Obligations  regarding  the  Transactions  within  a  Same 
Corporation   

Notwithstanding  [§14(1)],  a  financial  investment  business  entity  needs  not  report  trade 
information  of  a  transaction  concluded  within  a  same  corporation:  Provided,  That,  a 
transaction  falling  under  any  of  the  following  subparagraphs  is  not  considered  to  be 
concluded within a same corporation:   

1.  A  transaction  between  a  Korean  branch  of  a  foreign  financial  investment  business 
entity  and  its  headquarters  or  between  the  Korean  branch  thereof  and  any  other 
branches outside of Korea; and 

2. A Financial Investment Business Entity’s transaction in any of the followings:                                                                                     

8 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(a) A transaction between a proprietary property  and a trust  property, a collective 
investment property, or a discretionary investment property (hereinafter referred to 
as “trust property, etc.”); or 

  (b) A transaction between trust properties, etc. 

Section 2 Data Elements, etc.   

§16. Information Types   

(1)  Trade  information  of  the  KRX-TR  that  reporting  entities  are  obliged  to  report  is 
classified  in  accordance  with  the  information  types  below  (hereinafter  referred  to  as 
“information types”): 

1. Transaction information: Basic information required for recording, and maintaining 
reportable  transactions,  identification  information  on  parties  to  a  transaction  and 
information on the detailed contract terms and conditions, and etc.     

2.  Valuation  information:  Information  pertaining  to  the  present  values  of  reportable 
transactions. 

3.  Collateral  information:  Information  related  to  collateral  that  is  given  or  received 
between parties to a reportable transaction. 

4.  Other  information:  Other  information  the  KRX-TR  deems  necessary  pursuant  to  a 
request by the financial authorities or for the creation of statistics. 

(2)  Matters  related  to  specific  data  elements,  their  formats,  and  requirements  per  each 
information type are prescribed by the Enforcement Rules.   

§17. Obligation to Use an LEI   

When a reporting entity reports identification information of a party to a transaction, it shall 
use an LEI pursuant to [§2(1)2] as identification information for the said  party; Provided, 
That, the reporting entity may use an identifier prescribed by the Enforcement Rules other 
than an LEI  in the case where it reports identification information of the other party who 
is not a financial investment business entity nor a collective investment scheme 

§18. Obligation to Use a UTI   

(1) In the event when a reporting entity reports its trade information, it shall include a UTI 
pursuant to [$2(1)5] for each reportable transaction.   

9 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(2) Necessary matters concerning the format of a UTI and decision on UTI generating entity 
are prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

§19. Obligation to Use a UPI   

In the event when a reporting entity intends to initially report trade information of a newly 
concluded transaction, it shall include a UPI pursuant to [§2(1)4] for each of the reportable 
transactions. 

Section 3. Starting the Trade Reporting Tasks 

§20. Deadline for Starting the Trade Reporting Tasks   

(1)  A  person  whose  reporting  obligations  are  incurred  pursuant  to  [§5-50-3(1)]  of  the 
Regulations  on  Financial  Investment  Business  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “Reporting 
Obligation-Incurred  Entity”  or  ROI  Entity)  shall  apply  for  user  registration  in  accordance 
with [§5(1)] within 15 days counted from the date when the said reporting obligations arise 
for the first time (hereinafter referred to as “initial reporting obligation-incurring date”). 

(2) An ROI Entity shall start its trade reporting tasks within 60 days counted from the initial 
reporting obligation-incurring date. 

(3)  An  ROI  Entity  shall  report  all  trade  information  on  reportable  transactions  that  are 
concluded  on  or  maintained  as  of  the  initial  reporting  obligation-incurring  date  and  those 
that  are  concluded  after  the  said  initial  reporting  obligation-incurring  date,  within  the 
deadline for starting a trade reporting task in paragraph (2).   

§21. Notifying Incurrence of Reporting Obligations   

(1) In cases where the KRX-TR, based on the collected trade information, confirms the fact 
that the party to the reportable transaction falls under an ROI Entity, the KRX-TR notifies 
such fact to the said party. 

(2) In cases where a person that received the notification in paragraph (1) did not apply for 
user registration to the KRX-TR until the deadline determined in accordance with [§20(1)], 
the KRX-TR notifies the FSS of such fact and notify the said party of such non-registration 
and the fact that the non-registration notification was given to the FSS. 

(3) Matters necessary for the notification of incurrence of reporting obligations other than 
paragraph (1) and (2) are prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

10 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Section 4. Reporting of Trade Information by Reporting Entities 

§22. Method of Reporting Trade Information   

(1) A reporting entity shall report its trade information to the KRX-TR by sending it to the 
TR  system  in  accordance  with  the  reporting  form  prescribed  by  the  KRX-TR  per  each 
information type.   

(2) The KRX-TR shall notify users of reporting forms for each information type prescribed 
in paragraph (1) and other materials related to trade repository services on the TR system, 
the internet homepage, or etc. 

(3) Other necessary matters concerning trade information submission method, etc. pursuant 
to paragraph (1) are prescribed by the Enforcement Rules.   

§23. Consent related to Use and Provision of Trade Information   

A  reporting  entity  shall  obtain  the  consent  related  to  use  and  provision  of  financial 
transaction information from the other party to the transaction (including the beneficiaries 
of  the  reportable  transaction)  in  advance,  in  accordance  with  the  form  prescribed  by  the 
Enforcement Rules before reporting pursuant to [§22(1)]. 

§24. Reporting Deadline   

(1) In cases where a reporting entity reports trade information for the first time after a new 
transaction  is  concluded,  the  said  reporting  entity  shall  report  such  information  by  the 
deadline prescribed in each of the following subparagraphs: 

1.  Financial  Investment  Business  Entity:  By  24:00  of  the  first  business  day  after  the 
trade date. Provided, That, the reporting deadline for the transactions that are concluded 
at or after 18:00, however, is 24:00 of the second business day after the trade date; and 

2. CCP: By 24:00 of the date when the assumption of obligations is registered.     

(2) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), in cases where the trade information validation process, 
etc.  was  suspended  temporarily  due  to  a  system  failure  and  etc.  pursuant  to  [§26(1)],  the 
KRX-TR  may  extend  the  reporting  deadline  in  paragraph  (1)  in  consideration  of  the 
duration of such a system failure, the time needed for system recovery, and etc. 

§25. Time Window for Sending and Viewing Trade Information   

11 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
The  time  window  where  a  reporting  entity  or  a  reporting  agency  may  electronically  send 
trade information to the TR system or view trade information kept therein is from 8:00 to 
24:00 of a business day.   

§26. Measures against System Failure, etc.   

(1) The KRX-TR  may temporarily suspend validation, recording and quality management 
of trade information in [§32] through [§34] (hereinafter referred to as “validation, etc.”) in 
cases falling under any of the following subparagraphs:   

1.  Where  it  is  deemed  difficult  to  carry  out  the  trade  repository  services  in  a  normal 
manner due to a failure of the TR system or any other IT system that is recognized to 
be necessary for performing such trade repository services; or 

2. Other cases where the KRX-TR recognizes as necessary for the system management.   

(2) In cases where, after the KRX-TR temporarily suspended the validation, etc. pursuant to 
paragraph  (1),  the  cause  for  such  suspension  is  resolved,  the  KRX-TR  resumes  such 
validation, etc. without delay.   

(3)  In the case of temporarily suspending the validation,  etc. pursuant  to  paragraph (1) or 
resuming  the  validation,  etc.  pursuant  to  paragraph  (2),  the  KRX-TR  notifies  such 
suspension or resumption via the internet homepage or etc. 

§27. Modification Reporting for Transaction Information   

In  cases  where  reasons  falling  under  any  of  the  following  subparagraphs  occur,  the 
reporting  entity  shall  report  such  modified  content  in  relation  to  the  previously-reported 
transaction information prescribed in [§16(1)1] and in such cases, reporting shall be carried 
out by the next business day of the date when the modification to the contract was agreed: 

1. Where terms and conditions of a transaction are modified in accordance with mutual 
agreement between the parties to the transaction;   

2. Where a transaction is terminated either fully or partially before its maturity; or 

3. Where portfolio compression is carried out, offsetting transaction positions pursuant 
to mutual agreement by the parties to the transaction. 

§28. Update Reporting for Valuation and Collateral Information   

(1) A reporting entity shall update valuation information in the [§16(1)2] and/or collateral 
information in subparagraph (3) of the same paragraph related to a reportable transaction on 

12 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
every business day from the next business day of the date when the reporting entity reports 
such information for the first time until the reportable transaction is terminated. 

(2) When carrying out  update reporting pursuant  to  paragraph  (1), a reporting entity shall 
reflect the latest information it can obtain as of the corresponding reporting date. 

(3)  Notwithstanding  the  preceding  provisions  of  this  Article,  in  cases  where  a  reporting 
entity carries out update reporting in accordance with paragraph (1) on the termination date 
of the reportable transaction but is not able to obtain such information calculated on the said 
termination date, it shall update the valuation information and/or the collateral information 
within  the  period  prescribed  by  the  Enforcement  Rules  and  counted  from  the  termination 
date of a transaction.   

§29. Liability for the Accuracy of Trade Information 

A  reporting  entity  shall  confirm  whether  the  trade  information  it  reports  pursuant  to 
[§22(1)]  is  consistent  with  the  contents  of  the  actual  transaction  and  is  liable  for  the 
accuracy of the reported trade information.

[§22(1)]  and  modification  reporting  pursuant  to  [§27]  and  update  reporting  pursuant  to 
[§28]).

CHAPTER IV. VALIDATION, QUALITY MANAGEMENT AND 
RECORDKEEPING, ETC. OF TRADE INFORMATION 

§32. Validation of Trade Information   

(1)  The  KRX-TR  validates  the  formats  of  files  and  input  values,  a  logical  consistency 
among input values, and etc. in relation to trade information submitted to the TR system in 
accordance with [§22(1)]. 

(2)  The  KRX-TR  notifies  a  relevant  reporting  entity  (including  a  reporting  agency  if  the 
reporting  entity  delegated  its  reporting  task  to  such  a  reporting  agency  pursuant  to  [§31]; 
hereinafter the same shall be applied in this Chapter) of the results such as whether the trade 
information  passed  the  validation  process  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1)  and  what  are  the 
erroneous data elements. 

(3) The KRX-TR may refuse to perform the validation process for trade information if it is 
deemed  necessary  for  the  stable  operation  of  the  TR  system  and  the  protection  of  trade 
information kept in the TR (e.g. in cases where there are concerns that the volume of trade 
information reported from a certain reporting entity may have a considerable impact on the 
TR system). 

(4)  In  cases  where  the  KRX-TR  refused  to  perform  the  validation  process  for  trade 
information  pursuant  to  paragraph  (3),  it  shall  notify  the  relevant  reporting  entity  of  such 
fact and the reason therefor without delay. 

(5) The KRX-TR shall set forth validation rules in paragraph (1) and announce it on the TR 
system, the internet homepage, or etc. 

(6) Matters necessary for trade information validation other than paragraph (1) through (5) 
are prescribed by the Enforcement Rules.   

§33. Recording Trade Information   

(1) The KRX-TR records trade information that passed the validation under [§32(1)] in the 
TR system and proceeds with the process pursuant to [§34].   

(2) In cases where trade information failed to pass the validation, the KRX-TR deems the 
reporting entity not to have reported such trade information. 

(3)  A  reporting  entity  shall  correct  trade  information  that  failed  to  pass  the  validation 
process and report trade information again without delay. 

(4)  Matters  necessary  for  recording  trade  information  and  etc.  other  than  paragraph  (1) 
through (3) are prescribed by the Enforcement Rules.

§34. Quality Management of Trade Information 

(1) The KRX-TR operates each of the following processes in order to enhance accuracy of 
trade information: 

1. Pairing process:   
A process that confirms whether the trade information reported by the parties to the same 
transaction  indicate  the  same  single  transaction  by  pairing  the  party’s  trade  information 
that  passed  the  validation  process  prescribed  under  [§32(1)]  with  the  other  party’s  trade 
information that contains the same UTI and the same party identification information; and   

2. Reconciliation process:   
A  process  that  determines  whether  key  data  elements  of  trade  information  paired  in 
accordance with the pairing process in subparagraph 1 are consistent from each other by 
comparing them. 

(2) The KRX-TR shall notify the result of the process prescribed under each subparagraph 
of paragraph (1) to the relevant reporting entity. 

(3)  The  KRX-TR  shall  set  forth  the  rules  necessary  for  operating  the  process  prescribed 
under  subparagraph  2  of  paragraph  (1)  and  announce  such  rules  on  the  TR  system,  the 
internet homepage or etc.   

(4) Matters necessary for quality management of trade information other than paragraph (1) 
through (3) are prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

§35. Deadline for Corrective Reporting 

A  reporting  entity  shall  check  the  contents  of  any  incorrect  trade  information  from  the 
unmatched  data  elements  resulting  from  the  reconciliation  process  in  [§34(1)2]  and  carry 
out corrective reporting for the said trade information within 5 business days counted from 
the date on which the reporting entity was notified of such reconciliation result. 

§36. Recordkeeping of Trade Information   

The KRX-TR keeps trade information reported by its users in a magnetic disk, a magnetic 
tape  and  other  auxiliary  memory  devices  for  10  years  from  the  date  when  relationship  of 
rights and duties in relation to the reportable transaction are terminated.

CHAPTER V. OBLIGATIONS OF THE KRX-TR   

§39. Prohibition of Discriminatory Handling 

The  KRX-TR  shall  not  discriminate  against  a  specified  user  without  rational  reasons  in 
performing  trade  repository  services  (including  the  auxiliary  services  under  [§52]; 
hereinafter the same shall apply in this Chapter). 

§40. Notifying Violation of Reporting Obligations, etc. 

(1) In cases where users or their executive officers and/or employees fall under any of the 
following cases, the KRX-TR shall notify such fact to the Governor of the FSS: 

1.  Where  a  reporting  entity  rejects,  disrupts,  or  avoids  the  KRX-TR’s  request  for 
provision of supplementary information pursuant to [§13]; 

2. Where the reporting pursuant to [§22] has not been carried out or has been performed 
by fraud;   

3.  Where  a  reporting  entity  fails  to  report  trade  information  by  the  reporting  deadline 
pursuant to [§24]; 

4.  Where  a  reporting  entity  fails  to  report  transaction  information  by  the  modification 
reporting deadline pursuant to [§27]; 

5. Where a reporting entity fails to update valuation and/or collateral information pursuant 
to [§28]; and 

6.  Where  a  reporting  entity  fails  to  meet  the  corrective  reporting  deadline  pursuant  to 
[§35].   

(2)  In  case  where  the  KRX-TR  gives  notification  in  accordance  with  paragraph  (1),  it 
notifies such fact and the contents of such notification to the user. 

§41. Obligations of Notification   

Matters  that  the  KRX-TR  intends  to  notify  to  users  in  relation  to  its  trade  repository 
services  and  other  necessary  matters  concerning  such  notification  are  prescribed  by  the 
Enforcement Rules. 

§42. Management of Operational Risk   

18 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
   
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
The  KRX-TR  shall  establish  a  system  that  can  recognize,  monitor  and  manage  any  cause 
that can give rise to an operational risk in relation to trade repository services. 

§43. Identifying, Assessing and Managing Conflicts of Interest   

(1) The KRX-TR shall consider possibility of conflicts of interest that can arise in each of 
the  following  relationships  when  its  executive  officers  and/or  employees  carry  out  trade 
repository services: 

1. Relationship between the KRX-TR and a user; 

2.  Relationship  between  the  KRX-TR  and  a  party  to  a  transaction  who  is  not  a  user 
(hereinafter referred to as “trade party” in this Article); and 

3. Relationship between the trade repository services and other services. 

(2) If the executive officers and/or employees of the KRX-TR deem that there is possibility 
of  conflicts  of  interest  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1),  they  shall  carry  out  their  work  after 
lowering such possibility to the minimum level by in-advance discussion with the head of 
the internal control department in accordance with [§24(1)] of Regulations on the Internal 
Control so that the interests of users, trade parties and the KRX-TR are not infringed. 

(3)  Notwithstanding  paragraph  (2),  if  it  is  deemed  by  the  KRX-TR’s  executive  officers 
and/or employees that minimizing the possibility of the conflicts of interest is difficult, such 
fact shall be notified to the relevant users and the said KRX-TR’s executive officers and/or 
employees shall not carry out the relevant work until the possibility of conflicts of interest 
is removed. 

(4)  In  cases  where  conflicts  of  interest  occur  while  carrying  out  tasks  or  in  cases  where 
there  is  such  a  possibility,  the  executive  officers  and/or  employees  of  the  KRX-TR  shall 
keep and maintain the record related to such a conflict in writing.   

(5)  The  KRX-TR  shall  monitor  the  conflicts  of  interest  management  system  regularly,  at 
least once a year, and report the result to the department of internal control. 

§44. Security of Trade Information   

The  KRX-TR  shall  establish  a  security  management  system  that  satisfies  each  of  the 
following  requirements  for  the  sake  of  managing  the  safety  of  trade  information  that  it 
collected from users: 

1.  To  install  and  operate  access  control  programs  such  as  a  firewall  or  encryption  to 
prevent any illegal access to trade information;

3. To monitor the use of and access to the TR system at all times. 

§45. Protection of Confidential Information, etc.   

(1) The KRX-TR and its executive officers and employees shall keep information, such as 
transaction  details  of  a  party  to  a  transaction  that  the  KRX-TR  or  its  officers  and/or

use such information for the purposes other than carrying out trade repository services nor 
leak such information: Provided, That, the same shall not apply to a case where the KRX-
TR  receives  a  written  consent  on  usage  and  provision  of  trade  information  from  the  all 
information subjects thereof including parties to the transaction.   

(2) The KRX-TR’s executive officers and employees who directly conduct trade repository

prescribed  in  the  Enforcement  Rules  with  the  purposes  to  keep  trade  details  and  other 
information confidential. 

§46. Access to Trade Information   

(1) The KRX-TR shall grant differentiated access to the TR system depending on different 
executives and employees of the KRX-TR carrying out their tasks, with the minimum scope 
necessary in performing such tasks.     

(2)  In  the  event  where  a  person  in  charge  of  trade  repository  services  changed  due  to 
transfer of personnel such as a change of position or resignation. The KRX-TR shall change 
or cancel the TR system access without delay.   

(3)  The  KRX-TR  shall  record  the  details  of  granting,  changing  or  cancelling  access 
pursuant to paragraph (1) and (2) and keep such records for at least 3 years.

accordance with [§323-3] of the Financial Investment Services and Capital Markets Act).

(hereinafter referred to as “emergency situation” in this Article) occurs. 

(2) The KRX-TR shall take measures pursuant to the business continuity plan in paragraph 
(1) in cases where an emergency situation actually occurs, and notify users and report to the 
FSC of the occurrence of emergency situation, the contents of ensuing measures, and etc.

(3)  The  KRX-TR  shall  keep  and  manage  any  record  related  to  the  occurrence  of  an 
emergency situation and measures and etc. in writing.

(1) The KRX-TR may establish and operate a TR Operation Committee to deliberate each

    1. Matters related to improvement of trade repository services;

§51. Payment of User Fees   

(1) Users (excluding those in subparagraph 1(b) of Article 4 and subparagraph 3(a) through 
(c) of that Article; hereinafter the same in this Article shall apply) shall pay user fees to the 
KRX-TR in relation to the services of reporting and using trade information. 

(2)  Payment  methods,  payment  rates  or  amounts  or  payment  deadlines  of  user  fee  in 
paragraph (1) are determined by the resolution of the Board of the KRX-TR. 

(3) In cases where a user fails to pay the user fees until the deadline pursuant to paragraph 
(2), the interest rate prescribed by the Enforcement Rules will be multiplied to the amount 
of  outstanding  user  fees  and  the  resulting  amount  will  be  imposed  as  damages  for  delay 
from the next day of the deadline (including non-business days).   

(4) In cases where the KRX-TR determines or changes user fees in paragraph (1), damages 
for delay in  paragraph  (3), and/or methods of imposing the said  user fees or damages for 
delay,  the  KRX-TR  shall  announce  the  contents  of  such  decisions  or  changes  on  the  TR 
system, internet homepage, or etc. Unless otherwise stated, the year, month and day used in 
this Regulation shall be based on the Korean Standard Time.

§53. Limitation of Liability of the KRX-TR   

(1) The KRX-TR is not liable for deciding accuracy of the information submitted by a user 
(e.g. determining whether such information is consistent with the fact).   

(2)  The  KRX-TR  is  not  liable  for  integrity  or  accuracy  of  any  trade  information  that  it 
collected from a user and provided pursuant to [§37] or disclosed publicly pursuant to [§38]. 
The  KRX-TR  does  not  have  any  obligation  of  or  responsibility  for  carrying  out  the 
transaction included in such trade information.   

(3) The KRX-TR is not responsible for any direct or indirect loss or damage that is incurred 
in relation to user’s use of and access to the TR system without a cause attributable to the 
KRX-TR. 

§54. Standard Time   

Unless otherwise stated in this Regulation, the date and time applied herein shall be based 
on the Korean Standard Time. 

§55. Governing Laws and Jurisdiction. 

(1) The laws governing the application and interpretation of this Regulation are the laws of 
the Republic of Korea.   

(2)  The  competent  court  for  the  dispute  and  lawsuit  related  to  the  application  and 
interpretation  of  this  Regulation  and  trade  information  and  other  matters  provided  in  this 
Regulation is the Busan District Court.
