Regulations on Operating the New-Type Securities Market 

Chapter  I.  General Provisions 

§1. Purpose 

The purpose of this Regulation is to stipulate matters necessary for members, listing review, 
management  of  listed  securities,  reporting  and  disclosure  of  new-type  securities  listed 
corporations,  and  trading  of  listed  securities  in  the  new-type  securities  market,  which  is 
established  and  operated  by  the  Korea  Exchange  after  receiving  the  designation  as  an 
innovative financial service pursuant to the Special Act on Support for Financial Innovation. 

§2. Opening of Market 

(1) The Korea Exchange (hereinafter referred to as the “Exchange”) shall establish the market 
to  trade  non-monetary  trust  beneficiary  certificates  and  investment  contract  securities 
(hereinafter  referred  to  as  the  “new-type  securities  market”)  within  the  KOSPI  Market 
established pursuant to Article 386 of the Financial Investment Services and Capital Markets 
Act (hereinafter referred to as the “Capital Markets Act”). 

(2) The new-type securities market shall be classified as follows: 

    1. Non-monetary trust beneficiary certificates market; and 

    2. Investment contract securities market. 

(3)  The  Exchange  shall  operate  the  new-type  securities  market  for  the  period  (hereinafter 
referred to as the “designation period”) during which the market is designated as an innovative 
financial service pursuant to the Special Act on Support for Financial Innovation. 

(4)  The  Exchange  may  terminate  the  operation  of  the  new-type  securities  market  upon 
expiration of the designation period. In this case, the Exchange shall provide prior notice of the 
termination of market operations and take necessary measures, such as delisting, etc. 

§3. Definitions   

(1) The terms used in this Regulation shall be defined as follows: 

1.  “Non-monetary  trust  beneficiary  certificates"  refers  to  beneficiary  certificates  that 
indicate the beneficial interest of a trust other than a monetary trust or investment trust; 

2.  “New-type  securities"  refers  to  non-monetary  trust  beneficiary  certificates  and 

investment contract securities; 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
3.  “New-type  securities  listed  corporation"  refers  to  a  company  that  lists  new-type 

securities on the new-type securities market; and 

4.  “Small investor" refers to a person who owns less than 1/100 of the total number of 

issued securities of the relevant issue. 

(2) The terms, other than those specified in paragraph (1), used in this Regulation shall be in   
accordance with the relevant laws and regulations, the “KOSPI Market Listing Regulations” 
(hereinafter referred to as the “Listing Regulations”), the “KOSPI Market Business Regulations” 
(hereinafter referred to as the “Business Regulations”), and the “Membership Regulations.” 

§4. Accounting Standards and Financial Statement Application Standards 

(1) The audit report applied to this Regulation shall refer to the audit report [including the audit 
report on the consolidated financial statement, in the case of a company(hereinafter referred to 
as  “the  company  subject  to  the  preparation  of  consolidated  financial  statement”)  owning  a 
consolidated subsidiary stated in Article 3(1) of the Enforcement Decree of Act on External 
Audit  of  Corporations;  the  same  hereinafter]  prepared  after  the  auditor,  stated  in  Article  2, 
Subparagraph 7(a) of the Act on External Audit of Corporations (hereinafter referred to as “the 
auditor”), conducted an audit of the financial statement (referring to the financial statement 
stated in Article 2, Subparagraph 2 of the Act on External Audit of Corporations; the same 
hereinafter) prepared in accordance with the accounting standards specified in Article 5(1) of 
the  Act  on  External  Audit  of  Corporations.  In  such  cases,  where  the  concerned  auditor 
including the accountant in charge has interests in the new-type securities listed corporation 
through the ownership of stocks, equity-related bonds, or the acquisition of stock options of 
such corporation, etc., the audit report prepared by the auditor concerned shall be excluded.   

(2)  Unless  otherwise  specified  in  this  Regulation,  the  financial  details  applicable  to  this 
Regulation  shall  be  based  on  the  financial  statements  in  which  the  auditor’s  opinion  is 
“unqualified”  or  “qualified” due  to  uncertainty  over the  company’s  ability  to  continue  as  a 
going concern. Provided that, in cases where the auditor’s opinion is qualified due to a violation 
of domestic accounting standards, the adjusted financial details that reflect such violation shall 
be applied. 

Chapter  II.  Listing  of  New-Type  Securities 

§5. General Operation of Listing of Securities 

(1)  With  respect  to  listing  by  application,  prohibition  of  partial  listings,  delisting  without 
application for delisting, trading before delisting, documents for listing, effective date of listing 
agreement, obligation of prior consultation, etc. of new-type securities, [§4 (excluding the latter 
part of paragraph (3)], [§6], [§8] through [§10], [§16(2)] and [§20] of the Listing Regulations 
shall apply mutatis mutandis. In this case, “seven (7) days” in [§9(2)] of the Listing Regulations 
shall be deemed as “three (3) days”. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(2)  The  Exchange  may  establish  the  Enforcement  Rules  regarding  the  procedures  and   
methods, application of review requirements, etc. for conducting preliminary listing review or 
listing review based on an application by a listing applicant. 

(3) A listing applicant (excluding applicants who already listed the same securities on the new-
type  securities  market)  who  intends  to  list  new-type  securities  shall  enter  into  a  listing 
agreement  with  the  Exchange.  In  such  cases,  the  listing  applicant  shall  submit  the  listing 
agreement in the format prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

(4) A new-type securities listed corporation may apply for delisting from the Exchange if early 
liquidation (referring to the repayment or redemption of the underlying assets or rights through 
sale before maturity) is confirmed prior to maturity for each type of listed issues. In such cases, 
the  new-type  securities-listed  corporation  shall  submit  a  delisting  application  form  and  the 
accompanying  documents  as  prescribed  by  the  Enforcement  Rules  to  the  Exchange.  The 
Exchange  shall  determine  whether  to  delist  based  on  the  content  and  compliance  with 
procedures contained in the securities registration statement. 

(5) An applicant for the initial listing of new-type securities may appoint a listing sponsor for 
the  purpose  of  applying  for  such  listing.  In  this  case,  [§12(2)  through  (4)]  of  the  Listing 
Regulations shall apply mutatis mutandis to the duties and qualifications of the listing sponsor. 

(6) With respect to the disposal of securities under mandatory holdings, etc. and the 
notification of irregular securities certificates, [§159] and [§161] of the Listing Regulations 
and the Enforcement Decree of the same Regulations shall apply mutatis mutandis. 

§6. Appointment of Designated Advisor 

(1) An applicant for initial listing of investment contract securities (including listed companies; 
the same hereinafter from [§6] through [§8]) shall appoint a person (hereinafter referred to as 
the “designated advisor”) who performs the following tasks: 

1. Submission of a comprehensive opinion on the listing eligibility of investment contract 

securities; 

2. Recommendation and advice on initial listing procedures, etc. for initial listing applicants 
of investment contract securities, and handling of affairs related to the initial listing; 

3. Regular and continuous advice for investment contract securities listed corporations on 

compliance with financial laws; 

4. Disclosure and reporting on behalf of investment contract securities-listed corporations; 

5. Recording and retention of task-related contents as a designated advisor; and 

6. Other duties that the Exchange deems necessary in performing the tasks of a designated 

advisor. 

(2) Notwithstanding paragraph 1, in the case where an applicant for initial listing of investment 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
contract securities is recognized by the Exchange as a financial investment business operator 
licensed for investment trading or collective investment business under the Capital Markets 
Act, a designated advisor may not be appointed. 

(3)  When  an  applicant  for  initial  listing  of  investment  contract  securities  and  a  designated 
advisor enter into a contract for the appointment of a designated advisor pursuant to paragraph 
(1) (hereinafter referred to as the "designated advisor appointment agreement" in this Article), 
the necessary rights and obligations between the company and the designated advisor shall be 
established to ensure compliance with these Regulations. In this case, the designated advisor 
appointment agreement shall include the matters stipulated in the Enforcement Rules. 

(4) The contract period for the appointment of a designated advisor shall be at least one (1) 
year  from  the  listing  date  of  each  issue  (if  the  designated  advisor  is  replaced  due  to  early 
termination of contract, the contract period is added together), and if the designated advisor 
changes or the contents of the appointment contract change, it shall be reported in writing to 
the Exchange without delay. 

(5) An applicant for initial listing of investment contract securities shall make the notification, 
submission of documents, reporting and disclosure, etc. to the Exchange in accordance with 
these Regulations through a designated advisor, and the notification by the Exchange to the 
applicant  for  initial  listing  of  investment  contract  securities  may  also  be  replaced  with  a 
notification to the designated advisor. Provided that, if a designated advisor is not appointed 
pursuant to paragraph (2) or the appointment agreement for a designated advisor is terminated, 
the  investment  contract  securities  listed  corporation  shall  directly  make  the  reports  and 
disclosures. 

(6) Other necessary matters concerning the appointment, etc. of the designated advisor may be 
prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

§7. Requirements for Designated Advisors 

Matters concerning the qualifications, duties, and reporting obligations of designated advisors 
shall be governed by applying [§6], [§8] and [§9] of the KONEX Market Listing Regulations 
mutatis  mutandis.  In  this  case,  "[§3]  of  the  Membership  Regulations  of  the  Exchange"  in 
[§6(1)2] of the same Regulations shall be deemed as “[§3(1)1] of the Membership Regulations 
of the Exchange" and the "responsible company" in [§8(2)] of the same Regulations shall be 
deemed as "the company that has entered into the designated advisory agreement." 

§8. Measures Against Designated Advisors 

(1) In the case where a designated advisor falls under any of the following, the Exchange may 
suspend the advisor's qualifications for a certain period of time (referring to an action restricting 
the  advisor  from  entering  into  a  new  designated  advisor  appointment  agreement  with  an 
applicant  for  initial  listing  of  investment  contract  securities).  In  such  cases,  the  suspension 
period and other necessary matters shall be prescribed by the Enforcement Rules: 

1. If a security listed on this market after entering into a contract with a designated advisor   

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
is delisted pursuant to [§15] for reasons arising within one (1) year of the listing date; or 

2. If the Exchange recognizes that the replacement of a designated advisor is necessary for 
investor protection and the public interest, such as due to significant neglect of duties by 
the designated advisor. 

(2) If a reason for action under paragraph (1) arises, the Exchange shall notify the designated 
advisor. 

(3) A designated advisor may submit an opinion to the Exchange within seven (7) days from 
the date of notification pursuant to paragraph (2), and the Exchange shall decide whether to 
take  action  against  the  designated  advisor  within  one  (1)  month  from  the  deadline  for 
submission of opinions. 

§9. Preliminary Listing Review, etc. 

(1)  An  applicant  for  initial  listing  of  new-type  securities  shall  submit  an  application  for 
preliminary listing review and accompanying documents to the Exchange as prescribed by the 
Enforcement  Rules  and  undergo  a  preliminary  listing  review  before  applying  for  an  initial 
listing in accordance with [§11]. 

(2)  The  Exchange  shall  examine  the  application  for  preliminary  listing  review  and 
accompanying documents pursuant to paragraphs 2 and 3 of [§11] that an applicant for initial 
listing of new-type securities submits in accordance with paragraph (1) for preliminary listing 
review. 

(3) The Exchange shall notify the initial listing applicant and relevant authorities of the results 
of the preliminary listing review in writing within thirty (30) days (based on business days) 
from the date of receipt of the application for preliminary listing review. 

(4)  Notwithstanding  paragraph  (3),  where  it  is  necessary  to  correct  or  supplement  the 
application  for  preliminary  listing  review  and  accompanying  documents,  or  where  other 
unavoidable  reasons  arise,  the  Exchange  may  postpone  the  preliminary  listing  review  and 
notification of results. In such cases, the Exchange shall notify the relevant applicant for initial 
listing of the reasons and estimated processing time in writing. 

(5) In the case where, after having notified the result of the preliminary listing review pursuant 
to paragraph (3), any of the following incidents occurs for an initial listing applicant, and it is 
recognized that the incident has a significant impact on the result of the  preliminary listing 
review,  the  Exchange  may  refuse  to  recognize  the  validity  of  the  relevant  results  of  the 
preliminary listing review: 

1. Where a material fact to business management has arisen; 

2. Where important matters on investor protection being falsely stated in or omitted from the 

application for preliminary listing review have been identified; or 

3.  Where  an  application  for  initial  listing  of  new-type  securities  has  not  been  submitted 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
within six (6) months from the date of having been notified of the result of the preliminary 
listing review. Provided that if the applicant for initial listing requests an extension of the 
submission  deadline  due  to  unavoidable  reasons,  such  as  a  sudden  change  in  market 
conditions,  and  the  Exchange  approves  the  request,  the  submission  deadline  may  be 
extended once for up to six (6) months. 

(6)  Where  the  Exchange  decides  to  refuse  to  recognize  the  validity  of  the  results  of  the 
preliminary listing review pursuant to paragraph (5), it shall announce such fact and notify the 
initial listing applicant and relevant authorities. 

(7) An applicant for initial listing of new-type securities shall submit the documents prescribed 
by  Enforcement  Rules  to  the  Exchange  by  the  day  before  the  listing  date  of  the  new-type 
securities after applying for preliminary listing review. 

(8) Where an applicant for initial listing submits a written statement to the Exchange stating 
the reasons for withdrawal of the application for preliminary listing review, the Exchange shall 
terminate the preliminary listing review. 

§10. Mandatory Holdings 

(1) Where an applicant for initial listing of new-type securities and registered executives of the 
relevant company submit an application for initial listing under [§11(1)], they shall hold the 
new-type securities they own for at least three (3) months from the listing date. 

(2) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), an applicant for initial listing of new-type securities shall 
hold the quantity prescribed by the Enforcement Rules for each issue of new-type securities 
from the date of initial listing until maturity or delisting of the relevant securities. 

(3) Documents to be submitted and the submission period in relation to the mandatory holdings 
pursuant to paragraphs (1) and (2) shall be prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

§11. Initial Listing 

(1) Where an applicant for initial listing of new-type securities intends to initially list new-type 
securities after passing the preliminary listing review conducted by the Exchange, the applicant 
shall submit to the Exchange the application and accompanying documents prescribed by the 
Enforcement Rules within six (6) months (if the submission deadline is extended due to the 
proviso to [§9(5)], the deadline shall be the date of extension) from the date of having received 
the results of the preliminary listing review. 

(2) To initially list new-type securities, all of the following requirements shall be met. Provided 
that the requirements of subparagraphs 1(c) and 1(d) may be determined based on the recently 
submitted semi-annual financial statements: 

    1. Requirements for initial listing applicants: All of the following requirements shall be met: 
        (a) The applicant shall be a domestic corporation that is a joint stock company under the 

Commercial Act; 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
        (b) The applicant shall be an issuer of new-type securities. Provided that in the case of 
non-monetary trust beneficiary certificates, it may be a corporation that has entered 
into a listing agreement with the Exchange as a party that has entrusted assets to the 
issuer; 

        (c)  Equity  capital  shall  be  at  least  KRW  2  billion.  In  this  case,  the  equity  capital  of  a 
corporation  subject  to  the  preparation  of  consolidated  financial  statements  (for 
companies other than holding companies, only the fiscal year which is applicable to 
Korean International Financial Reporting Standards is applied) shall be based on the 
total  equity  in  the  consolidated  financial  statements,  excluding  non-controlling 
interests; 

        (d) The applicant shall not be in a state of capital impairment. In this case, the corporation 
subject to the preparation of consolidated financial statements shall be based on the 
capital  and  total  equity  (excluding  non-controlling  interests)  in  the  consolidated 
financial statements; 

        (e)  The  auditor's  audit  opinion  on  the  financial  statements  for  the  latest  fiscal  year 
(including the audit opinion on the consolidated financial statements for corporations 
subject to the preparation of consolidated financial statements) shall be “unqualified”. 
In  this  case,  corporations  subject  to  the  preparation  of  consolidated  financial 
statements  (excluding  holding  companies)  shall  apply  consolidated  financial 
statements  only  for  fiscal  years  in  which  Korean  International  Financial  Reporting 
Standards were applied. 

        (f) The applicant shall meet the mandatory holding requirements under [§10]; and 

        (g) In addition, the eligibility of the applicant shall be recognized in relation to performing 

the tasks of issuance, listing, and reporting of new-type securities. 

    2. Requirements for the issue to be listed: All of the following requirements shall be met. 
Provided that, if the issuing price for items (g) or (h) is not available, it may be substituted 
with an appraised value separately approved by the Exchange: 

        (a) A product disclosure statement (a document explaining matters separately requested 
by  the  Exchange,  such  as  the  structure  of  the  securities,  profit  distribution,  early 
termination, maturity, etc.) shall be submitted; 

        (b) In the case of investment contract securities, a designated advisor shall be appointed 

in accordance with [§6]; 

        (c)  In  the  case  of  investment  contract  securities,  the  requirements  for  bankruptcy 
remoteness (referring to matters related to investor protection devices described in the 
securities registration statement and investment contracts, etc.) shall be met; 

        (d) Electronic registration (excluding electronic registration using a distributed ledger; the 

same hereinafter) shall have been completed; 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
        (e) The securities shall have been issued through a public offering or secondary offering; 

        (f) The total number of listed securities must be 100,000 shares or 100,000 units or more; 

        (g) The base market capitalization (referring to the amount calculated by multiplying the 
number  of  shares  to  be  listed  by  the  issuing  price  at  the  time  of  the  latest  public 
offering  and/or  secondary  offering;  the  same  hereinafter)  must  be  at  least  KRW  3 
billion; 

        (h) The issuing price per share or unit must be KRW 5,000 or more; 

        (i) As of the date of application for initial listing, small investors must own at least 25% 

of the total number of listed securities; and 

        (j) There must not be restrictions on the transfer of securities. 

(3) The Exchange shall comprehensively consider the following matters to determine whether 
it is appropriate to list new-type securities that meet the requirements of paragraph (2): 

        (a) Matters related to the preparation of a list of beneficiaries and investors, maturity, early 

termination, etc., shall be deemed appropriate for the product; 

(b) The transparency and fairness of the product shall be recognized in light of the product 

disclosure statement; 

      (c) The securities listed by an initial listing applicant within the past two (2) years shall 

not have been designated as an issue subject to delisting pursuant to [§15(1)1]; and 

      (d) In addition, the listing of new-type securities shall be deemed not detrimental to the 

public interest and investor protection. 

(4) The Exchange shall, without delay, notify the applicant for initial listing of whether or not 
the initial listing has been approved from the date of application for initial listing pursuant to 
paragraph (1). 

(5) Where, as a result of the review pursuant to paragraphs (2) and (3), it is determined that the 
listing of the relevant new-type securities is not appropriate for the public interest and investor 
protection, the Exchange may refuse to list them. 

§12. Supplementary Listing   

(1) Where a new-type securities listed corporation intends to issue and list new securities of an 
issue with the same rights as an already listed new-type securities, it shall submit an application 
for  supplementary  listing  and  the  accompanying  documents  prescribed  by  the  Enforcement 
Rules to the Exchange. 

(2) Securities to be listed additionally pursuant to paragraph (1) shall satisfy the requirements 
of [§11(2)2(d) and (j)] as of the date of application for supplementary listing. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(3)  The  Exchange  may  reject  a  supplementary  listing  if  it  determines  that  the  issuance  of 
securities  to  be  listed  additionally  is  not  in  accordance  with  the  methods  or  procedures 
described in the securities registration statement or product disclosure statement. 

(4) Provisions of [§9 (excluding paragraph (3))], [§10(2)] and [§11(1), (4) and (5)] shall apply 
to preliminary review, mandatory holding, and listing application for supplementary listing. 

§13. Listing Change 

(1) Where a new-type securities listed corporation intends to apply for listing change due to a 
change in the name of the relevant securities, spin-off, merger, or business transfer with another 
corporation,  etc.,  it  shall  submit  to  the  Exchange  an  application  for  listing  change  and 
accompanying documents prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

(2) The application for listing change under paragraph (1) shall be made without delay after 
the reason for the relevant change takes effect. 

§14. Designation of Administrative Issues 

(1) In any of the following cases, the Exchange shall designate the relevant new-type securities 
(referring to all securities listed by the relevant listed corporation, in cases where subparagraphs 
1, 3 or 4 apply) as an administrative issue: 

1. If a new-type securities listed corporation falls under any of the followings: 

    (a) No submission of financial statements, etc.: Where the financial statements, etc. noted 

in [§21] are not submitted by the deadline specified in the same Article. 

    (b) Inadequate audit opinion: Where the auditor's audit opinion or review opinion on the 
financial statements falls under any of the following. In this case, if the corporation is 
subject to the preparation of consolidated financial statements, the audit  opinion or 
review opinion on the consolidated financial statements is included: 

        (i) The audit opinion on the latest fiscal year's financial statements is "qualified"; or 

        (ii) The review opinion on the latest semi-annual financial statements is "adverse" or a 

disclaimer of opinion. 

    (c)  Capital  Impairment:  Where  50%  or  more  of  the  corporation's  capital  has  been 
impaired as of the end of the latest fiscal year. In this case, for a corporation subject 
to the preparation of consolidated financial statements, the calculation shall be based 
on the capital and total equity (excluding non-controlling interests) in the consolidated 
financial statements. 

    (d) Merger, etc.: Where a merger, spin-off, merger after spin-off, or business transfer is 

being decided on. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
2. Where an issue of listed securities falls under any of the following. Provided that items 

(a) and (b) shall apply only to investment contract securities: 

    (a)  Failure  to  appoint  a  designated  advisor:  Where  a  reason  for  termination  under  the 
designated  advisor  appointment  agreement  arises  within  one  (1)  year  of  listing. 
Provided that this does not apply in cases where ten (10) months or more have passed 
since  listing,  and  the  Exchange  recognizes  that  the  reason  for  termination  is 
unavoidable; 

    (b) Failure to meet the requirements for bankruptcy remoteness: Where a reason arises 
that makes it impossible to fulfill the requirements for bankruptcy remoteness stated 
in the securities registration statement, etc.; 

    (c) Inadequate number of securities: Where the total number of listed securities is less 

than 100,000 shares or 100,000 units; 

    (d) Early termination, etc.: Where early termination, etc. is confirmed pursuant to [§5(4)] 

and delisting is requested. 

3. Where the matters requested by the Exchange pursuant to [§24(1)] are not fulfilled within 

the deadline; or 

4.  Other  cases  where  the  Exchange  deems  that  designation  as  an  administrative  issue  is 

necessary for the sake of public interest and investor protection. 

(2) If the Exchange confirms that the reasons for the designation of administrative issue under 
each subparagraph of paragraph (1) have been resolved, it shall remove the designation of the 
administrative issue. 

(3)  The  Exchange  shall  stipulate  the  application  method  of  the  reasons  for  designating  an 
administrative  issue,  the  timing  of  designation  and  removal  of  an  administrative  issue,  and 
other necessary matters in the Enforcement Rules. 

§15. Delisting 

(1) Where new-type securities fall under any of the following subparagraphs, the Exchange 
shall delist the relevant new-type securities (Where item 1 or 4 applies, it shall refer to all issues 
listed by the relevant listed corporation). In such case, the Exchange may grant the new-type 
securities  listed  corporation  a  grace  period  of  up  to  one  (1)  year  to  resolve  the  reasons  for 
delisting (excluding the reasons for delisting under item 3) or to apply for delisting upon request: 

1. Where a new-type securities listed corporation falls under any of the following: 

        (a) No submission of financial statements, etc.: In any of the following cases with regard 

to financial statements; 

            (i)  A  new-type  securities  listed  corporation  is  designated  as  an  administrative  issue 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
pursuant  to [§14(1)1] due to non-submission of financial statements,  etc.  for the 
fiscal year and fails to submit the relevant financial statements, etc. within ten (10) 
days from the day following the submission deadline stipulated in [§21(2)]; or 

            (ii)  A  new-type  securities  listed  corporation  is  designated  as  an  administrative issue 
pursuant  to [§14(1)1(a)]  due to non-submission of semi-annual reports, etc., and 
fails to submit the semi-annual report, etc. for the next fiscal year by the submission 
deadline stipulated in [§21(2)]. 

(b) Inadequate audit opinion: Where the auditor's audit opinion on the financial statements 
falls  under  any  of  the  following.  In  this  case,  if  the  corporation  is  subject  to  the 
preparation of consolidated financial statements, the audit opinion on the consolidated 
financial statements shall be included: 

        (i) The audit opinion on the latest fiscal year's financial statements is "adverse" or a 

disclaimer of opinion; or 

        (ii) A new-type securities listed corporation is designated as an administrative issue 
pursuant  to [§14(1)1(b)1] with an audit opinion  of “qualified”, and the auditor’s 
audit opinion on the financial statements is “qualified” even in the latest fiscal year. 

(c) Capital Impairment: Where the capital is fully impaired as of the end of the latest fiscal 
year. In this case, the corporation subject to the preparation of consolidated financial 
statements  applies  the  relevant  requirements  based  on  the  capital  and  total  capital 
(excluding non-controlling interests) in the consolidated financial statements. 

(d) Dissolution: Where a reason for dissolution according to the law (excluding a reason 

for dissolution due to a merger, spin-off, or merger after spin-off) occurs; 

(e) Final dishonor or suspension of bank transactions: Where a bill or check issued by a 
new-type securities listed corporation is finally dishonored or its banking transactions 
are suspended; 

(f)  Commencement  of  rehabilitation  procedures  or  bankruptcy:  Where  a  new-type 
securities  listed  corporation  falls  under  any  of  the  following  cases  in  relation  to  an 
application for initiation of rehabilitation proceedings or an application for bankruptcy: 

          (i)  Where  a  new-type  securities  listed  corporation  files  an  application  for  the 
commencement of rehabilitation procedures or bankruptcy in accordance with the 
Debtor Rehabilitation and Bankruptcy Act; or   

          (ii) Where, after a person other than a new-type securities listed corporation files an 
application  for  the  commencement  of  rehabilitation  procedures  or  bankruptcy 
against the relevant listed corporation in accordance with the Debtor Rehabilitation 
and Bankruptcy Act, the reason for the application is not resolved within one (1) 
month  or  a  court  decides  to  commence  rehabilitation  procedures  or  declares 
bankruptcy. 

(g)  Suspension  of  business,  etc.:  Where  the  business  of  a  new-type  securities  listed 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
corporation  is  suspended  or  it  is  impossible  to  perform  new-type  securities-related 
tasks due to administrative dispositions, etc.   

(h) Ineligibility of applicant for listing change: Where, when a new-type securities listed 
corporation decides to  conduct a merger after spin-off with another  company or to 
transfer related business and applies for listing change, the applicant for listing change 
falls under any of the items from (a) to (g). 

2. Where an issue of listed securities falls under any of the following: 

        (a) Failure to resolve the reason for designation as an administrative issue: Where, after 
an issue has been designated as an administrative issue for any of the reasons listed in 
[§14(1)2], the reason has not been resolved within the period specified below: 

            (i)  Failure  to  appoint  a  designated  advisor:  Where  a  new  designated  advisor  is  not 
appointed within three (3) months from the date of designation as an administrative 
issue.  Even  if  a  new  advisor  is  appointed,  if  the  designated  advisor  appointment 
period  stipulated  in  Article  6,  Paragraph  4  is  not  met,  it  shall  be deemed  that  the 
reasons for delisting have not been resolved; 

            (ii)  Failure  to  meet  the  requirements  for  bankruptcy  remoteness:  Where  the 
requirements for bankruptcy remoteness are not met within three (3) months from 
the date of designation as an administrative issue; or 

            (iii)  Inadequate  number  of  listed  securities:  Where  the  reason  for  designation  as  an 
administrative  issue  is  not  resolved  within  six  (6)  months  from  the  date  of 
designation as an administrative issue. 

        (b) Restrictions on transfer of securities: Where there is a decision restricting the transfer 

of securities; or 

        (c) Termination of trust, etc.: Where, for non-monetary trust beneficiary certificates, the 
trust is terminated or the trust company becomes insolvent or bankrupt.         

3. Where a request for delisting is made to the Exchange as the final trading day noted in 

[§16] arrives or early termination, etc. is confirmed pursuant to [§5(3)]; or 

4.  Other  cases  where  the  Exchange  recognizes  that  delisting  is  necessary  for  the  public 

interest and investor protection. 

(2) In the case of delisting pursuant to paragraph (1) (excluding the case of paragraph (1)3), 
the Exchange may permit trading before delisting pursuant to [§9] of the Listing Regulations. 

§16. Last Trading Day 

(1) The last trading day for new-type securities with a maturity date shall be four trading days 
prior to the maturity date. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(2) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), the Exchange may, if necessary for market management, 
set a different last trading day. 

§17. Suspension of Trading   

(1)  In  cases  where  a  listed  corporation  or  a  listed  security  falls  under  any  of  the  following 
subparagraphs, the Exchange may suspend the trading of the relevant security: 

1. Where a grace period is in effect pursuant to the latter part of the main sentence of [§15(1)]; 

2. Where a cause for delisting under [§15(1)1 and 2] occurs; 

3. Where the Exchange requests an explanation from a listed corporation pursuant to [§23]; 

4. Where electronic registration is required for the initial registration, change, or cancellation 

of the listed securities; 

5. Where a listed company applies for delisting due to early termination, etc.; or 

    6.  Other  cases  where  the  Exchange  deems  it  necessary  for  the  public  interest,  investor 

protection, and market management. 

(2) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), in cases where the Exchange recognizes that the relevant 
listed securities are already under trading suspension or that trading suspension is not necessary 
for market management purposes, it may not take action of the suspension of trading of the 
relevant securities. 

(3) Matters necessary for the suspension and resumption of trading pursuant to paragraph (1) 
shall be prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

§18. Public Announcement on Administrative Issues and Delisting Issues 

(1) In the event of designation as an administrative issue or the removal of such designation, 
the Exchange shall announce such fact through electronic delivery media. 

(2) Where a new-type securities listed corporation falls under the causes for delisting noted in 
[§15], the Exchange shall notify the relevant corporation in writing of such fact, the method 
and procedure for delisting, and other necessary matters without delay. 

(3) Where the Exchange delists new-type securities, the Exchange may announce the fact that 

the relevant listed securities have been delisted. 

(4) Where a new-type securities listed corporation has not fulfilled its obligations under   
these Regulations or has not submitted the relevant documents, the Exchange may announce 
such fact. 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
     
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Chapter III. Reporting and Disclosure of New Types Securities Listed Corporations 

§19. General Operation of Reporting and Disclosure 

(1)  With  regard  to  the  faithful  performance  and  responsibility  of  reporting  and  disclosure 
obligations  of  new-type  securities  listed  corporations  and  matters  to  be  reported  to  the 
Exchange, [§4], [§6(1)] and Part 3 of the “KOSPI Market Disclosure Regulations” (hereinafter 
referred to as the “Disclosure Regulations”) shall apply mutandis mutatis. 

(2) Where a new-type securities listed corporation makes a report pursuant to [§6(1)] of the 
Disclosure Regulations, the Exchange shall, without delay, disclose the main contents of the 
report  through  disclosure  media  pursuant  to  [§4]  the  Enforcement  Rules  of  the  Disclosure 
Regulations (hereinafter referred to as “disclosure media”) after going through a verification 
process  to  determine  whether  the  report  may  cause  confusion  in  investment  decisions  and 
whether supporting documents are attached. 

§20. Appointment of Disclosure Officer, etc. 

(1) A new-type securities listed corporation shall designate one (1) disclosure officer (a full-
time employee of the relevant listed corporation who participates in the decision-making of the 
board of directors or a person equivalent to such officer) and one (1) disclosure employee to 
perform the reporting and disclosure duties prescribed in these Regulations. Provided that, if 
an  investment  contract  securities  listed  corporation  within  one  year  of  its  listing  date  has 
appointed  a  designated  advisor,  the  designated  advisor  shall  act  on  behalf  of  the  relevant 
investment contract securities listed corporation in handling reporting and disclosure duties. 

(2) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), where a new-type securities listed corporation falls under 
any  of  the  following  subparagraphs,  it  may  designate  a  person  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  a 
“disclosure  agent”),  who  is  recognized  as  having  specialized  knowledge  of  disclosure  and 
meets the qualifications specified in the Enforcement Rules and who can represent the relevant 
listed corporation in all reporting and disclosure matters, and report and disclose in accordance 
with these regulations. 

    (a) Where less than three years have passed after the date of initial listing of the new-type 

securities; or 

    (b) Where a new-type securities listed corporation is a small and medium-sized enterprise 
(SME) as defined in Article 2 of the Framework Act on Small and Medium-sized 
Enterprises (SMEs). 

(3) When a new-type securities listed corporation designates or changes a disclosure officer, 
disclosure manager (in cases where a designated advisor handles reporting and disclosure work 
on behalf of the corporation, this refers to the disclosure manager of the designated advisor; 
the same hereinafter), and disclosure agent (hereinafter referred to as “disclosure officer, etc.”), 
it shall notify the Exchange of such fact in writing without delay. In the case of a disclosure 
agent, it shall notify the Exchange of such fact by attaching a document proving that it has been 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
granted authority to represent the relevant listed corporation. 

(4) Disclosure officers, etc. shall complete training on reporting and disclosure work separately 
conducted  by  the  Exchange  within  three  (3)  months  of  the  date  of  designation  or  change 
notification pursuant to paragraph (3). Provided that this does not apply where one (1) year has 
not elapsed since the completion of the relevant training. 

(5)  Where  the  Exchange  confirms  that  a  disclosure  agent  does  not  meet  the  qualifications 
stipulated  in  the  Enforcement  Rules  or  that  disclosure  officer,  etc.  has  not  completed  the 
training within the deadline in violation of [§4], it may request the relevant listed corporation 
to replace the relevant disclosure officer, etc. 

(6) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), where a corporation that has applied for the commencement 
of  rehabilitation  procedures  (including  simplified  rehabilitation  procedures;  the  same 
hereinafter)  pursuant  to  the  Debtor  Rehabilitation  and  Bankruptcy  Act  or  for  which  the 
commencement of rehabilitation proceedings has been decided, the administrator pursuant to 
Article 74 of the same Act or the preservation administrator pursuant to Article 85 of the same 
Act shall be deemed as the disclosure officer. 

§21. Submission of Financial Statements, etc. 

(1) Listed corporations shall submit to the Exchange the business report, etc. for the fiscal year 
as  stipulated  in  the  Enforcement  Rules  and  a  semi-annual  report,  etc.  as  stipulated  in  the 
Enforcement Rules. 

(2)  Listed  corporations  shall  submit  the  business  report,  etc.  for  the  fiscal  year  pursuant  to 
paragraph (1) to the Exchange within ninety (90) days after the end of each fiscal year, and the 
semi-annual reports, etc. shall be submitted within forty five (45) days after the end of six (6) 
months from the start of each fiscal year. 

§22. Reporting of Material Matters 

Where any of the following reasons arise, a new-type securities listed corporation shall report 
the details to the Exchange by the deadline specified in the relevant subparagraphs: 

1.  Arrival of maturity of listed issue: Up to one (1) month prior to the maturity date of the 

new-type securities; 

2.  Occurrence of early termination of listed issue: On the day of occurrence; 

3.  Decision to voluntarily delist the relevant securities, such as confirmation of early 

termination: On the day of the decision; 

4. Profit distribution of an issue: On the day of the decision. Provided that, where a large-
scale  profit  distribution  amount  exceeds  the  price  limit  per  security  and  requires  the 
adjustment of base price, the reporting deadline shall be 10 trading days prior to the date 
specified in [§105] of the Business Regulations; 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
    5. Occurrence of a cause that has a material impact on securities, such as the acquisition, 
disposal, loss or damage of assets subject to rights, or the execution, change or termination 
of a business contract: without delay; or 

    6. Other cases that, as a case that the Exchange deems it necessary for listing management, 

are stipulated in the Enforcement Rules: without delay. 

§23. Request for Explanation for Rumors or News, etc. 

(1) The Exchange may request an explanation from a new-type securities listed corporation to 
verify the facts of rumors or news regarding matters that fall under or are equivalent to reasons 
for  delisting  under  [§15]  and  the  relevant  listed  corporation  shall  comply  with  the  request. 
Provided that, if the rumor or other information has already been reported or disclosed within 
one (1) month, the Exchange may not request an explanation. 

(2) The Exchange shall request explanations from the CEO, disclosure officer, etc. of new-type 
securities listed corporations by phone, facsimile, or electronic document, and announce such 
fact of request for explanation and its contents through disclosure media. In such cases, if an 
explanation is requested by phone, the Exchange shall promptly submit the request in writing 
by facsimile or electronic document. 

(3) If a new-type securities listed corporation reports and discloses (hereinafter referred to as 
the "interim disclosure") that it is in the decision-making process for the explanatory disclosure 
under paragraph (1), it shall make a follow-up report and disclosure regarding the final details 
or progress on the matter within one (1) month from the date of the interim disclosure. Provided 
that, if it is deemed practically difficult to make a follow-up report and disclosure regarding 
the final details or progress within one (1) month from the date of the interim disclosure, the 
Exchange may require the relevant listed corporation to do so within the deadline specified in 
the  interim  disclosure  (referring  to  a  deadline,  within  six  (6)  months,  agreed  upon  with  the 
Exchange). 

(4) When making a report and disclosure of the interim disclosure, it shall include the detailed 
description of the relevant decision-making process. 

§24. Measures Against Corporations Violating Reporting Obligations 

(1) Where a new-type securities listed corporation intentionally or through gross negligence 
repeatedly  violates  the  reporting  obligations  under  [§21]  and  [§22]  or  fails  to  respond  to 
requests  for  explanation  under  [§23],  the  Exchange  may  require  each  of  the  following  to 
prevent recurrence: 

    1. Submission of an improvement plan;   

2. Replacement of the disclosure officer, etc.; or 

     
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
    3. Appointment of a disclosure agent (for a period of not more than one (1) year). 

(2) A new-type securities listed corporation shall submit an improvement plan to the Exchange 
within ten (10) trading days after receiving the request under paragraph (1). In this case, the 
Exchange may require the submission of a performance report within a deadline determined 
by the Exchange (it shall be a period of not more than six (6) months). 

(3) If the Exchange deems it necessary, it may request the relevant listed corporation to correct 
or supplement  the contents of the improvement  plan and implementation report  and submit 
them. 

(4)  The  Exchange  may  announce  the  fact  of  the  request  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1)  and  the 
improvement  plan  and  implementation  report  submitted  pursuant  to  paragraph  (2)  through 
disclosure media. 

Chapter  IV.  Market  Operation,  Trading,  Clearing  and  Settlement,  etc. 

§25. Application of Regulation 

(1)  Except  as  otherwise  provided  in  these  Regulation  and  Enforcement  Rules,  regulations 
applicable to the stock market and stocks, among the Business Regulations and the Securities 
Market Clearing and Settlement Business Regulations (hereinafter referred to as the “Clearing 
and Settlement Business Regulation”) (including the Enforcement Rules and related guidelines 
for each Regulations), shall apply to the new-type securities market and new-type securities. 
Provided that [§20-9] through [§20-11], [§28-2], [§30-2] through [§36], [§38-2] through [§41], 
[§79],  [§80],  [§106-2]  and  [§112]  of  the  Business  Regulation  shall  not  apply  to  new-type 
securities.   

(2) The new-type securities shall not fall under the category of substitute securities under [§88] 
of the Business Regulation. 

§26. Exclusion of Off-hours Market 

For new-type securities, there shall be no off-hours market other than the regular market. 

§27. Type of Quotations and Quotation Receiving Hours 

(1) A member shall submit only limit quotations for new-type securities.   

(2) Orders shall be submitted from thirty (30) minutes prior to the start of trading hours in the 
regular market to the time of market closing (hereinafter referred to as “order receiving hours” 
and includes changed order receiving hours pursuant to the Business Regulations). 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
§28. Limits on Quotation Prices 

(1) The quotation prices of new-type securities shall neither be higher than the price obtained 
by adding the price change limit (hereinafter referred to as “upper limit price”) to the base price 
(hereinafter referred to as the “base price”) determined by the Enforcement Rules nor lower 
than the price obtained by subtracting the price change limit from the base price (hereinafter 
referred to as “lower limit price). In this case, if the Exchange recognizes that the upper or 
lower limit price is not appropriate, the upper or lower limit price may be determined differently 
by the Enforcement Rules. 

(2) The price change limits noted in paragraph (1) shall be equal to the amount calculated by 
multiplying the base price by 0.3 (the amount less than the quotation price unit of the base price 
shall be discarded; the same hereinafter in this Article). Provided that it shall be the lowest 
price among the quotation price units when the relevant obtained amount is less than the lowest 
value of the quotation price unit. 

(3) Notwithstanding paragraphs (1) and (2), in the case of issues to be delisted, there shall be 
no price restrictions, and in cases stipulated in the Enforcement Rules, the price change limit 
may be set differently.   

§29. Quotation Quantity Units and Trading Quantity Units 

The quotation quantity units and the trading quantity units in the new-type securities market 
shall be specified by the Enforcement Rules.   

§30. Special Cases of Trade Execution of the New-Type Securities Market 

(1) In addition to the price determination under [§23(1)1 through 5] of the Business Regulations, 
the price determination under [§17(3)] and paragraph (4) of this Article shall also be conducted 
by a call auction. 

(2)  The  range  of  quotations  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “quotations  participating  in  a  call 
auction”)  to  be  included  in  determining  the  prices  under  [§17(3)]  and  paragraph  (4) of  this 
Article shall be prescribed in the Enforcement Rules.   

(3) The price determined by a call auction shall be the matched price (hereinafter referred to as 
the "matched price") pursuant to [§23(4)] of the Business Regulations. In this case, if there are 
two  (2)  or  more  matched  prices,  [§23(5)]  of  the  Business  Regulations  shall  apply,  but  the 
special quotation price shall not apply. If the Exchange recognizes that application of [§23(5)] 
of the Business Regulations is inappropriate, the price determined by the Enforcement Rules 
shall apply. 

(4) Where the provisional execution price determined by the quotation of new-type securities 
falls under any of the following subparagraphs, the Exchange may change the trade execution 
method of the relevant issue to the call auction under paragraph (1) or expand the quotation 
range  participating  in  call  auctions  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “change  of  trade  execution 
method, etc.”). 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
    1. Case of rising or falling by the rate specified in the Enforcement Rules or higher or lower 
based  on  the  latest  price  as  of  the  time  of  receipt  of  the  relevant  quotation  during  the 
trading hours of the regular session (excluding the time of deciding the opening price); or     

    2. Case of rising or falling by the rate specified in the Enforcement Rules or higher or lower 
based on the latest price as of the time of receipt of the relevant quotation (limited to the 
price determined by the method of a call auction, and referring to the base price of the day 
when determining the opening price) during the trading hours of the regular session. 

(5) Matters necessary for the change of trade execution method, etc. under paragraph (4) shall 

be prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

§31. Method of Determining Initial Trading Price of Initially Listed Issues, etc. 

(1) The initial price on the trading commencement date for issues specified in the Enforcement 
Rules including newly listed issues shall be determined by a call auction. 

(2) The range of quotations participating in the determination of price under paragraph (1) shall 
be determined by the Enforcement Rules. 

§32. Good Faith Deposit 

(1) When a member receives an order to buy or sell new-type securities from a customer, the 
member shall collect the total purchase amount (limited to cash) as a good faith deposit in the 
case of a purchase, and the entire quantity of the securities to be sold as a good faith deposit in 
the case of a sale. 

(2) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), a member may not collect the good faith deposit in any of 
the following cases:   

    1. Where, after a selling order of a customer that resolves the outstanding balance is executed, 

a purchase order within the amount of the relevant selling amount is placed;   

    2. Where, for securities of which a purchase order is executed or those that can be confirmed 
by a member to be electronically registered in the customer account book under Article 
22, Paragraph 2 of the Electronic Securities Act by the settlement date, a selling order 
within the number of the relevant securities is placed; or 

    3. Where an order to buy or sell is placed from a customer who intends to make settlement 

by receiving a margin loan or borrowing securities. 

§33. Investor Notice 

When a member first receives an order to buy or sell from a customer (including the customer 
who placed the order to an investment brokerage company, if the member receives the order 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
from such investment brokerage company), the member shall notify the customer of the market 
characteristics  and  investment  risks  using  one  (1)  of  the  methods  in  each  subparagraph  of 
[§82(1)] of the Business Regulations and obtain the customer's confirmation of the contents of 
the notification. Provided that this does not apply if the customer is a professional investor as 
defined  in  Article  9,  Paragraph  5  of  the  Capital  Markets  Act  or  a  person  specified  in  the 
Enforcement Rules. 

Chapter  V.  Supplementary  Provisions 

§34. Listing Fees and Annual Dues 

(1)  An  applicant  for  listing  of  new-type  securities  shall  pay  a  listing  fee,  and  a  new-type 
securities  listed  corporation  shall  pay  annual  dues  as  long  as  the  securities  remain  listed. 
Provided that the payment of the listing fee or annual dues may be exempted for the cases 
deemed necessary by the Exchange. 

(2) With respect to the listing fees and annual dues noted in paragraph (1), the rate of listing 
fee,  the  methods  for  calculation  and  payment,  the  scope  of  exemption  and  other  necessary 
matters shall be prescribed by the Enforcement Rules. 

§35. Enforcement Rules 

Matters necessary for implementing these Regulations shall be prescribed by the Enforcement 
Rules.
