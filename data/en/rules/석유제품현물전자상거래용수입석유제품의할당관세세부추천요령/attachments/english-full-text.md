§1. Purpose 

The purpose of this Guideline is to stipulate the detailed matters related to making the 

recommendation  on  quota  tariff  applied  to  petroleum  products  imported  for  the 

purpose  of  distributing  through  the  spot  e-trading  launched  by  the  Korea  Exchange 

(hereinafter referred to as the “Exchange”) pursuant to [§71] of the Customs Act and 

the  guideline  for  recommending  quota  tariff  application  on  the  goods  under 

jurisdiction  of  the  Ministry  of  Knowledge  Economy  (Ministry  of  Knowledge 

Economy’s announcement No. 2012-556).        (Amended on January 10, 2013) 

§2. Product Eligible for Recommendation and Quantity Limit 

(1)    The products eligible for recommendation shall be the petroleum products to be 

distributed  through  the  spot  e-trading  (regular  grade  gasoline  and  diesel  fuel  for 

automobiles), which are reported for import during the period from January 1, 2013 to 

June  30,  2013,  and  the  quantity  limit  shall  be  700,000  barrels  for  regular  grade 

gasoline and 3,000,000 barrels for diesel.      (Amended on January 10, 2013)   

(2)    Notwithstanding  Para.(1),  if  the  Minister  of  Knowledge  Economy  deems  it 

necessary, the Chairman of Exchange may recommend the quantity that is below the 

limit specified in Para.(1) and in case of monthly recommended quantity, it shall not 

exceed 116,000 barrels for regular grade gasoline and 500,000 barrels for diesel fuel 

(excluding  the  quantity  carried  over  from  the  previous  month).        (Amended  on 

January 10, 2013) 

(3)    The  quantity  (referred  to  as  the  “quantity  supplied  to  economy  petro  station” 

hereinafter)  that  the  person  wishing  to  obtain  the  endorsement  for  quota  tariff 

application  (referred  to  as  the  “applicant”  hereinafter)  has  supplied  to  the  economy 

petro station through e-trading shall be recommended in its entire quantity (excluding 

the quantity already recommended).    However, in case where the concerned quantity 

supplied to the economy petro station exceeds the recommendation limit pursuant to 

Para.(2),  it  shall  be  recommended  in  proportion  to  the  quantity  that  each  applicant 

supplied to the economy petro station.      [January 10, 2013]   

1 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(4)      In  case  where  total  quantity  applied,  other  than  the  quantity  supplied  to 

economy  petro  station  already  recommended  pursuant  to  Par.(3),  exceeds  the 

recommendation limit pursuant to Paras.(2) & (3), the recommendation shall be made 

in  proportion  to  the  quantity  applied  (excluding  the  quantity  supplied  to  economy 

petro station) by each applicant.        [January 10, 2013] 

§3. Person Eligible for Recommendation     

The person eligible for recommendation shall be the participants in spot e-trading who 

intend  to  import  the  petroleum  products  to  distribute  through  spot  e-trading.   

However, in case where the Minister of Knowledge Economy deems it necessary for 

the  maintenance  of  order  in  domestic  market  as  well  as  the  supply  and  demand,  the 
Chairman of Exchange  may make the recommendation only for the persons meeting 

specific  qualifications  by  taking  into  consideration  the  previous  supply  objectives, 

import records and facility size.        (Amended on January 10, 2013) 

§4. Request for Recommendation 

(1)    The applicant shall prepare the documents noted in each of the following items 

and submit them to the Chairman of Exchange:                  (Amended on January 10, 

2013)                   

1.    One  (1)  copy  of  request  for  recommendation  on  quota  tariff  application 

(Form 1 of Annex); 

2.    One (1) copy of bill of lading;   

3.    One (1) copy of import agent contract or supply contract (limited to the case 

where an import agent contract or supply contract has been entered); 

4.    One (1) copy of import declaration certificate;   

5.    One (1) copy of import schedule and plan for distribution through e-trading 
distribution (Form 8 of Annex); 

2 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
6.    In addition, the document that the Chairman of Exchange deems necessary.   

(2)    The quantity unit in the request for recommendation on quota tariff application 

noted in Para.(1)1 shall be “barrel” or “metric ton” while adding the quantity in “liter” 

(158.984 liter per 1 barrel) in parenthesis.   

§5. Sequence of Recommendation     

When  recommending  the  quota  tariff  application,  the  Chairman  of  Exchange  shall 

make the recommendation of the quantity for quota tariff application according to the 

order that the request for recommendation on quota tariff application pursuant to [§4] 
(limited  to  the  valid  application  that  was  submitted  with  all  required  documents)  is 

received.    However,  if  the  Minister  of  Knowledge  Economy  deems  it  necessary  for 

the maintenance of distribution order in the domestic petroleum market, including the 

purpose of ensuring the demand and supply and promotion of e-trading, the Chairman 

of  Exchange  may  restrict  the  recommendation  quantity  within  the  limit  set  by  the 

Minister  of  Knowledge  Economy  per  applicant  who  made  request  during  a  fixed 

period while taking into account such factors as the import records and if the applicant 

is  also  engaged in  the  export business  by manufacturing the same product  in  Korea.       

(Amended on January 10, 2013) 

§6. Recommendation Method 

The  quota  tariff  shall  be  recommended  per  applicant  by  allocating  the  requested 

quantity per petroleum product of concerned applicant using the quantity of gasoline 

and diesel supply in e-trading pursuant to [§7] as the limit.         

§7. Calculation Method of Petroleum Quantity Eligible for Allocation 

(1)    The quantity of petroleum eligible for recommendation shall be the quantity per 
petroleum product distributed through e-trading (excluding the quantity for which the 
custom  duty  was  exempted  according  to  the  free  trade  agreement)  while  using  the 

3 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
quantity  per  petroleum  product  that  was  imported  and  went  through  the  custom 

clearance  by  the  day  before  the  request  day  for  quota  tariff  recommendation  after 

January 1, 2013 as the upper limit.          (Amended on January 10, 2013) 

(2)    In calculating the quantity of petroleum eligible for quota tariff recommendation 

noted  in  Para.(1),  the  quantity  already  recommended    according  to  [§5]  shall  be 

excluded  to  prevent  the  duplicated  recommendation.      However,  in  case  where  the 

request for recommendation was returned pursuant to [§12], this provision shall not be 

applied to the quantity specified therein.      (Amended on January 10, 2013) 

(3)    The  quantity  per  petroleum  product  distributed  through  e-trading  pursuant  to 

Para.(1) shall be based on the quantity for which the delivery of petroleum product has 

been completed as it was traded by the trading day immediately before the request day 
for recommendation after January 1, 2013.        (Amended on January 10,2013) 

§8.  Submission  of  Materials  for  Calculation  of  Quantity  of  Petroleum  Product 

Eligible for Quota Tariff Recommendation 

For the comparison and verification of quantity recommended for quota tariff by the 

Chairman of Exchange, the applicant shall officially submit the evidential documents 
such as the acceptance of import declaration to the Chairman of Exchange by the 20th 
day of the month following the day submitting the request for recommendation.         

§9. Processing Deadline and Issuance of Recommendation 

(1)      The  Chairman  of  Exchange  shall  issue  the  recommendation  on  quota  tariff 

application (hereinafter referred to  as the “recommendation”) to  the applicant  within 

the business day following the receipt day of valid request for recommendation noted 

in  [§4(1)].    However,  this  provision  shall  not  be  applied  to  the  case  where  the 

recommendation  was  made  pursuant 

to  [§2(2) 

through  (4),  §3p  and  §5p].       

(Amended on January 10, 2013)

(2)      The  recommendation  pursuant  to  Para.(1)  may  be  issued  as  an  electronic 

document (referring to the electronic document specified in [§2(1)] of the Framework 

Act on Electronic Commence) (Form 3 of Annex).     

§10. Validity Period of Recommendation, etc. 

(1)    The validity period of recommendation shall be twenty (20) days from the issue 

date.    However,  in  case  where  the  last  day  of  validity  period  is  a  public  holiday,  it 

shall deem that the validity of recommendation expires on the first business day after 

the concerned holiday.     

(2)    When  wishing  to  extend  the  validity  period  of  recommendation,  the  concerned 
applicant  shall  submit  to  the  Chairman  of  Exchange  the  request  for  extension  of 

validity  of  recommendation  by  attaching  the  recommendation  thereto  before  the 

expiry of recommendation validity.     

(3)      If it deems that there is a reason for the request noted in Para.(2), the Chairman 

of Exchange shall re-issue the recommendation after extending the validity period of 

original recommendation. 

(4)      The  expiry  date  of  validity  of  recommendation  (including  the  expiry  date  of 

extended  validity  pursuant  to  Para.(3))  shall  not  be  later  than  the  last  day  of  quota 

tariff application of product eligible for the recommendation.       

§11. Splitting up of Recommendation 

(1)    In  case  where  the  applicant  who  has  received  the  quota  tariff  recommendation 

asks for the split of quantity stated in the concerned recommendation, the Chairman of 

Exchange may issue a split-up recommendation.         

(2)    The applicant who wishes to obtain the split-up recommendation shall submit to 

the Chairman of Exchange the request for split-up of recommendation by attaching the 
recommendation to be split thereto.   

5 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(3)    If it deems that there is a reason for requesting the split-up noted in Para.(2), the 

Chairman  of  Exchange  shall  re-recommend  by  re-issuing  the  recommendation  that 

was attached to the request for split-up.       

§12. Return of Recommendation 

In  case  where  the  applicant  who  received  the  quota  tariff  recommendation  comes 

under  the  case  noted  in  any  of  the  following  items,  the  concerned  applicant  shall 

return the recommendation to the Chairman of Exchange without delay:     

1.    Case of giving up the import of total quantity recommended or the remaining 

quantity  (the  quantity  after  deducting  the  quantity  passed  through  the  custom 
clearance from the total recommended quantity) according to the recommendation;         

2.    Case where the validity of recommendation has expired; and   

3.    In addition, case where it is not possible to import the recommended quantity.             

§13. Speedy Custom Clearance of Recommended Product 

The  applicant  who  received  the  quota  tariff  recommendation  shall  make an  effort  to 

distribute the recommended product as soon as possible by speeding up the import of 

recommended product and passing the custom clearance within the scheduled import 

declaration date and expected shipping out date from the bonded zone.     

§14. Reporting 

For  each  quarter,  the  Chairman  of  Exchange  shall  submit  to  the  Minister  of 

Knowledge  Economy  the  report  on  the  record  of  recommendation  on  quota  tariff 

application  (Form  7  of  Annex),  where  the  quantities  recommended  and  quantities 

cleared through the custom in the concerned quarter are comprehensively analyzed, by 
the 10th day after the end of each quarter.     

6 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
§15. Verification about Recommendee 

In  case  where  there  is  need  to  verify  the  details  of  business  of  recommendee,  the 

Chairman of Exchange may request the recommendee to submit the relevant materials 

or  inspect  the  books  and  documents  or  other  objects  by  visiting  the  offices  and 

business locations and other necessary location of recommendee.         

ADDENDUM 
(July 23, 2012) 

§1. Effective Date 

This  Guideline  shall  be  put  into  effect  on  the  date  that  the  Minister  of  Knowledge 

Economy approves.       

§2.    Exception in Application of Other Regulations 

In  case  where  the  contents  of  this  Guideline  differ  from  the  guideline  on 

recommendation of quota tariff for goods subject to quota tariff under the jurisdiction 

of the Ministry of Knowledge Economy (Announcement No. 2012-335), the guideline 

of Ministry of Knowledge Economy shall take precedence over this Guideline.   

§3.    Interim Measure 

In  case  where  the  Chairman  of  Exchange  has  recommended  the  quota  tariff 

application from July 1, 2012 to the effective date of this Guideline, it shall deem that 

the concerned quota tariff application was recommended according to this Guideline.
