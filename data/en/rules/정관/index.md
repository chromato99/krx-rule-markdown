CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS 

§1. Name 

The  name  of  this  Corporation  shall  be  the  Korea  Exchange,  Inc.  (hereinafter  referred  to  as 
"the Exchange") and the Exchange’s acronym shall be the KRX. (Amended on January 14, 
2009) 

§2. Purpose 

The  Exchange  shall  endeavor  to  operate  and  manage  each  of  the  following  business: 
(Amended on January 14, 2009, April 15, 2009; March 29, 2012; October 2, 2013; March 5, 
2014; July 16, 2014; April 14, 2016; May 2, 2019) 

1. Business related to  the establishment and operation of the KOSPI Market, KOSDAQ 
Market,  KONEX  Market  and  Derivatives  Market  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the 
Markets”); 

2. Business related to trading of securities and exchange-traded derivatives products; 

3. Business  related to  the clearing and settlement  subsequent  to  the trading  of securities 
and derivative products listed on the KRX Market; 

3-2. Business  related to  the verification, clearing and settlement of derivatives  traded in 
OTC markets (hereinafter referred to as “the OTC derivatives”); 

3-3. Business related to collecting, keeping and managing of the transaction information 
of the OTC derivatives; 

4. Business related to the listing of securities; 

5.  Business  related  to  the  determination  of  transaction  types  and  product  groups  of 
exchange-traded derivative products; 

6. Business related to the reporting and disclosure by listed corporations; 

7. Business  related  to  market  surveillance,  investigation  into  abnormal  trading  and 
supervision of members; 

8. Businesses related to the one-sided auction of securities; 

9. Business  related  to  the  mediation  of  disputes  arising  from  the  trading  in  the  KOSPI 
Market,  KOSDAQ  Market,  KONEX  Market  and  Derivatives  Market  (only  in  the  case 
where there is a request from the concerned parties);

in the Markets;

connection with the operation of the Markets;

14. Business related to the entering into cooperation, linkage and alliance with the foreign

15.

§3. Location of Main Office, etc.

(2)  When  it  is  deemed  necessary,  the  Exchange  may,  upon  the  resolution  of  the  Board  of

CHAPTER II. STOCKS 

§6. Total Number of Stocks to be Issued 

Total number of stocks to be issued by the Exchange shall be 160 million shares. (Amended 
on April 10, 2007) 

§7. Price per Share 

The price of the stocks issued by the Exchange shall be KRW 5,000 per share. 

3 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
§8. Total Number of Stocks to be Issued at the time of Inauguration 

Total number of the stocks to be issued at the time of inauguration of the Exchange shall be 
20 million common registered stocks. 

§9. Classes of Stocks, etc. 

(1) The stocks to be issued by the Exchange shall be the registered common stocks and the 
registered different classes of stocks. (Amended on April 10, 2007; May 2, 2019) 

(2)  The  different  classes  of  stocks  issued  by  the  Exchange  shall  be  the  preferred  stocks 
concerning profit dividends (hereinafter referred to as “preferred stocks”). [May 2, 2019] 

(3)  The  stock  certificates  to  be  issued  by  the  Exchange  shall  be  in  total  of  eight  (8) 
denominations of 1-share, 5-share, 10-share, 50-share, 100-share, 500-share, 1,000-share and 
10,000-share. 

§9-2. Number and Details of Preferred Stocks 

(1) The preferred stocks to  be issued by the Exchange shall be the nonvoting share and the 
number of preferred stocks to be issued shall be 40,000,000. 

(2) The Board shall determine the preferred dividend rate, which is higher than an annual rate 
of 10/100 on the basis of the face value, at the time of issuing the preferred stocks. 

(3) In cases where the dividend rate of common stock exceeds the dividend rate of preferred 
stock,  as  to  the  excess  amount,  the  same  rate  shall  be  added  in  the  dividend  rate  of  the 
preferred stock as in the case of common stock before paying dividends. 

(4) In cases where the required dividend has not been paid to preferred stocks for a business 

  year, the payment of accumulated unpaid dividend shall take the precedence at the dividend 
payout time in the following business year. 

(5) In cases where there has been a decision not to pay the required dividend for the preferred 
stock,  it  shall  be  deemed  that  the  preferred  stocks  have  the  voting  right  from  the  date  of 
general shareholders’ meeting subsequent to the general shareholders’ meeting that voted for 
that  decision  to  the  closing  time  of  the  general  shareholders’  meeting  that  approves  the 
decision to make a preferential dividend payment. 

(6) In cases where the Exchange makes a capital increase with and without consideration or 
pays stock dividends, it shall, in principle, issue common stocks for the common stocks and 
preferred  stocks  for  the  prepared  stocks  with  the  same  condition  at  the  respective 
shareholding rate. However, when it is deemed necessary, the Exchange may issue only one 
class of stock, and in this case, all shareholders have the right to the allotment or dividend for

§9-3. Subscription Rights 

(1)  When  new  stocks  are  issued,  the  shareholders  shall  have  the  right  to  allotment  of  new 
stocks in proportion to the number of stocks owned. 

(2) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), in any of the following cases, new stocks may be allotted 
to non-shareholders according to the decision of the Board: (Amended on January 14, 2009; 
April 15, 2009; February 21, 2018; May 2, 2019) 

1. In the case of making public offering pursuant to [§119] of the Act or having made the 
underwriter to underwrite for the public offering; 

2. In the case of issuing new stocks as a capital increase by way of public offering upon 
the decision of the Board pursuant to [§165-6] of the Act; 

3. In the case of preferentially allotting new stocks to the members of the employee stock 
ownership association pursuant to [§165-7] of the Act; 

4. In the case of issuing new stocks subsequent to the exercise of stock option pursuant to 
[§542-3] of the Commercial Act; 

5. In the case of issuing new stocks subsequent to the exercise of employee stock options 
pursuant to [§39] of the Framework Act on Labor Welfare; 

6.  In  the  case  of  issuing  new  stocks  subsequent  to  the  issuance  of  depository  receipts 
(DRs) pursuant to [§165-16] of the Act; 

7. In the case of issuing new stocks to the foreign exchange concerned for the purpose of 

cooperation and alliance with a foreign exchange; and 

8. In the case of issuing new stocks to the domestic or foreign financial institutions for the 
purpose of raising the capital needed urgently. 

(3) In cases where the subscription rights is surrendered or denied or the fractional shares are 
resulted  in  the  course  of  allotting  new  stocks,  such  cases  shall  be  handled  according  to  the 
decisions of the Board. 

[April 10, 2007]

(1)  Pursuant  to  [§165-6]  of  the  Act,  within  the  quantity  that  is  less  than  20/100  of  total

§9-5. Stock Options 

(1)  The  Exchange  may  grant  the  stock  options  pursuant  to  Article  542-3(3)  of  the 
Commercial Act, within the quantity that is less than 15/100 of total number of stocks issued, 
to  its  officers  and  employees  by  way  of  the  special  resolution  of  general  shareholders’ 
meeting.  However,  the  stock  option,  which  is  within  the  number  of  stocks  prescribed  in 
Article 542-3(3) of the Commercial Act and Article 30(4) of the Enforcement Decree of the 
same Act, may be granted by way of the Board’s resolution. (Amended on January 14, 2009; 
May 2, 2019) 

(2) The stock options shall be granted to the officers and employees who make or can make 
contributions  to  the  establishment,  management  or  technology  innovation  of  the  Exchange, 
but shall exclude the persons falling under any of the following subparagraphs: (Amended on 
January 14, 2009; February 21, 2018) 

1. The largest shareholder (referring to the largest shareholder prescribed in  item (a) under 
subparagraph 6 of [§2] of the Act on Corporate Governance of Financial Companies. In such cases, 
the “financial company” shall be construed as the “Exchange”) and the related persons thereof 
(referring to the related persons prescribed in item (a) under subparagraph 6 of [§2] of the Act on 
Corporate Governance of Financial Companies;  the  same  hereinafter).  However, any  person 
who  falls under  the  related persons as a result of becoming an officer of the Exchange shall be 
excused; 

2.  A  major  shareholder  (referring  to  the  major  shareholder  prescribed  in  item  (b)  under 
subparagraph 6 of [§2] of the Act on Corporate Governance of Financial Companies. In 
such  cases,  the  “financial  company”  shall  be  construed  as  the  “Exchange”;  the  same 
hereinafter in this Article) and the related persons thereof. However, any person who falls 
under  the  related  persons  as  a  result  of  becoming  an  officer  of  the  Exchange  shall  be 
excused; and 

3. The person who becomes a major shareholder by exercising the stock options. 

(3) The stocks to be delivered as a result of exercise of stock options (in case of delivering the 
difference between the exercise price of stock options and the market price in cash or treasury 
stocks, it refers to the stock that is used as the base of calculating the difference) shall be the 
common stocks or preferred stocks. 

6 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(4) The stock options granted to  any one (1) officer  or employee shall not  exceed 1/100  of 
total number of stocks issued. 

(5) The stock options shall be exercised within five (5) years from the date that has elapsed 
two (2) years since the decision date noted in paragraph (1). 

(6) Any person granted a stock option shall hold the office or work for at least two (2) years 
from  the  date  of  resolution  noted  in  paragraph  (1)  before  exercising  the  stock  option. 
However, in cases where a person granted a stock option dies within two (2) years from the 
resolution  date  mentioned  in  paragraph  (1),  or  retires  or  resigns  from  the  office  due  to  an 
event not attributable to the person (excluding retirement or resignation due to old age), such 
person may  exercise his/her stock option during  the relevant  exercise period.  (Amended on 
February 21, 2018) 

(7) The provisions of [§9-7] shall apply mutatis mutandis to the distribution of dividends to 
the new stocks issued as a result of exercise of stock options. 

(8)  The  Exchange  may  revoke  the  grant  of  stock  options  upon  resolution  of  the  board  of 
directors in any of the following cases: (Amended on May 2, 2019) 

1. Case where the officer or employee who was granted a stock option retires or resigns 
on his/her own decision; 

2.  Case  where  the  officer  or  employee  who  was  granted  a  stock  option  has  caused  a 
serious damage to the Exchange on intention or by mistake; 

3.  Case  where  the  Exchange  is  bankrupt  or  dissolved,  thus  unable  to  comply  with  the 
exercise of stock options; or 

4.  Additionally,  in  cases  where  the  reasons  for  retraction,  which  are  specified  in  the 
agreement for granting the stock options, have occurred.

§9-6. Employee Stock Options 

(1) The Exchange may grant the employee stock options pursuant to [§39] of the Framework 
Act  on  Labor  Welfare,  within  the  quantity  that  does  not  exceed  20/100  of  total  number  of 
stocks  issued,  to  the  members  of  the  employee  stock  ownership  association  in  accordance 
with the decision of the general shareholders’ meeting. However, the employee stock options 
of  which  the  quantity  is  less  than  10/100  of  total  number  of  stocks  issued  may  be  granted 
through the Board’s decision. (Amended on February 21, 2018) 

(2) The stocks to be delivered as a result of exercise of employee stock options shall be either 
the common stocks or the preferred stocks. 

(3) The employees shall not transfer the employee stock options received to another person, 
and shall retain the membership of the employee stock ownership association until the time of 
exercising the concerned employee stock options. However, in case where an employee who 
received the employee stock options has died, it shall be deemed that the heir of the deceased 
has inherited them. 

(4) The provisions of [§9-7] shall be applied mutatis mutandis to the distribution of profits to 
the new stocks issued as a result of exercise of employee stock options. 

(5) In the case of falling under any of the following subparagraphs, the Exchange may retract 
the employee stock options granted in accordance with the decisions of the Board: (Amended 
on May 2, 2019) 

1.  The  member  of  the    employee  stock  ownership  association,  who  has  received  the 
employee stock options, has caused a serious damage to the Exchange on intention or by 
mistake; 

2. In cases where the Exchange is bankrupt or dissolved, thus unable to comply with the 
exercise of stock options; or 

3.  Additionally,  in  cases  where  the  reasons  for  retraction,  which  are  specified  in  the 
agreement for granting the employee stock options, have occurred. 

(6)  The  Exchange  may  not  grant  the  employee  stock  options  to  the  member  of  employee 
stock ownership association who have less than one (1) year of continuous service. 

[April 10, 2007]

§9-7. Record Date for Dividend Payment of New Stocks 

When  the  Exchange  issues  the  new  stocks  as  a  result  of  capital  increase  with/without 
consideration  or  stock  dividend,  with  regard  to  the  dividends  allotment,  it  shall  be  deemed 
that the new stocks have been issued at the end of the business  year preceding the business 
year which includes the issuing date of new stocks belongs. 

[April 10, 2007] 

§9-8. Transfer Agent 

(1) The Exchange may retain a transfer agent for its stocks. (Amended on February 21, 2018) 

(2) The transfer agent,  its  location and the range of  services provided by the agent  shall be 
determined  by  the  resolution  of  the  board  of  directors  and  the  Exchange  shall  make  public 
such decisions. 

(3) When  it  retains  a  transfer  agent  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1),  the  Exchange  shall  keep  the 
shareholder register or a copy of shareholder register in the office of the transfer agent, and 
shall let the transfer agent to handle the administrative matters about the stocks, such as the 
stock  transfer,  registration  or  cancellation  of  right  of  pledge,  indication  or  cancellation  of 
trust assets, receipt of report, etc. (Amended on February 21, 2018) 

(4) The matters noted in paragraph (3) shall be handled according to the procedures stipulated 
in the regulations that the transfer agent has established for the transfer of shareholder’s name, 
etc. (Amended on January 14, 2009) 

[April 10, 2007] 

§9-9. Reporting of Addresses, Names, Seals or Signatures of Shareholders, etc. 

(1)  The  shareholders  and  registered  pledgees  shall  report  their  names,  addresses  and 
seals/signatures to  the Exchange (the transfer agent  in  the case of retaining a transfer agent 
pursuant to [§9-8(1)]). (Amended on February 21, 2018) 

(2)  The  shareholders  and  registered  pledgees  who  reside  abroad  shall  establish  the  mailing 
address in Korea and appoint an agent based in Korea, and report them to the transfer agent. 

(3) In cases where there is a change in the matters to be reported under paragraphs (1) and (2), 
the shareholders shall do the same. (Amended on May 2, 2019) 

[April 10, 2007]

§10. Closing of Register of Shareholders and the Record Date thereof 

(1)  The  Exchange  shall  close  down  the  register  of  shareholders,  suspending  the  change  of 
records relating to shareholder’s rights, from January 1st to January 31st each year.

CHAPTER III. GENERAL SHAREHOLDERS’ MEETING 

§11. Convening of Meeting 

(1) There shall be the general shareholders’ meeting and extraordinary shareholders’ meeting, 
and the general shareholders’ meeting shall be convened within three (3) months after the end 
of each business  year and the extraordinary shareholders’ meeting shall be convened as the 
needs arise. 

(2)  Except  for  the  cases  where  there  are  other  regulations  pursuant  to  the  laws,  the 
shareholders’  meeting  shall  be  convened  by  the  Chairman  of  the  Exchange  (hereinafter 
referred to as “the Chairman”) in accordance with the resolutions of the Board. 

(3) When convening a shareholders’ meeting, the Exchange shall notify the date, place and 
purposes of the meeting to all shareholders no later than two (2) weeks before the date of the 
meeting by sending a written notice via postal service or electronic messaging system. 

(4)  Notwithstanding paragraph (3), the meeting notification to the shareholders who hold less 
than 1/100 of total number of shares issued may be substituted by making an announcement 
of  intention  of  convening  the  general  shareholders’  meeting  and  the  detailed  objectives 
thereof  at  least  two  (2)  times  in  each  of  the  Pusan  Ilbo  published  in  Busan  and  the  Seoul 
Shinmun published in Seoul by two (2) weeks before the meeting date.  [April 10, 2007] 

§12. Location of Meeting 

The shareholders’ meeting shall be held at the place where the main office of the Exchange is 
located or the place determined by the Board. 

§13. Chairperson of Shareholders’ Meeting 

(1) The chairperson of the shareholders’ meeting shall be the Chairman.

(1) The chairperson of the shareholders’ meeting may prohibit a person, who clearly disturbs

The shareholders shall have one (1) voting right per share.

members  present  at  the  meeting,  which  must  represents  at  least  ¼   of  the  total  number  of 
stocks issued.

CHAPTER VII. ACCOUNTING 

§53. Business Year 

The business year of the Exchange shall begin on 1st  of January and end on 31st  of December 
each year. 

§54. Appropriation of Retained Earnings 

(1)  The Exchange shall appropriate the inappropriate retained earnings of each business year 
as noted in the followings: (Amended on April 10, 2007) 

1. Earned surplus reserve; 
2. Various legal reserves; 
3. Dividends; 
4. Settlement reserve; 
5. Reserve for business expansion; 
6. Dividend equalization reserve; 
7. Other voluntary reserves; and 
8. Other appropriations of retained earnings. 

(2) The settlement reserve shall be used only for the purpose other than the cases where the 
Exchange  compensates  for  the  losses  caused  members’  failure  to  fulfill  their  settlement 
obligations  for  the  securities  trades  or  derivatives  trades.  (Amended  on  January  14,  2009; 
October 2, 2013) 

40 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(3) The settlement reserve noted in paragraph  (2) shall be accumulated separately according 
to each of the following subparagraphs: [October 2, 2013] 

1. Settlement of securities trade or exchange-traded derivatives; and 
2. Settlement of OTC derivatives. 

(4)  The  reserve  for  business  expansion  shall  be  used  for  the  acquisition  of  real  estates, 
development  of  computer  systems  and  others  that  are  necessary  for  the  accomplishment  of 
the business of the Exchange. 

(5)  The  dividend  equalization  reserve  shall  not  be  used  for  the  purpose  other  than  the 
capitalization  for  the  cash  dividends  or  stock  dividends  paid  to  the  shareholders.  [April  10, 
2007] 

§55. Dividends 

(1) Dividends may be paid in cash or stocks. 

(2) The Exchange may pay an interim cash dividend once a business year, using June 30 as 
the record date, upon the decision of Board. 

(3)  The  dividends  specified  in  paragraphs  (1)  and  (2)  shall  be  paid  to  the  shareholders  or 
pledges listed on the register of shareholders as of the record date for interim dividend or the 
end of each accounting period.
