CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS 

§1.  Purpose 

The  purpose  of  this  Regulation  is  to  stipulate  the  matters  necessary  for  the  trading  of 
securities  in  the  KOSPI  Market  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the  Market”)  that  the  Korea 
Exchange (hereinafter referred to as “the Exchange”) has established under Article 393 of the 
Financial Investment Services and Capital Markets Act (hereinafter referred to as “the Act”). 
(Amended on January 28, 2009)   

§2.  Definitions 

(1) The term “member” in this Regulation means a person who can participate in all or part of 
securities  trading  in  the  market  among  members  classified  under  each  subparagraph  of 
[§3(1)] of the Membership Regulation. (Amended on July 19, 2023) 

(2) The term “clearing member” in this Regulation means, from among the clearing members 
under  [§3(2)1]  of  the  Membership  Regulation,  any  member  who  is  authorized  to  clear  the 
securities trades  carried out in its name or the securities trades, the settlement of which has 
been entrusted to the member by non-clearing members. (Amended on January 28, 2009) 

(3) The term “non-clearing member” in this Regulation means, from among the non-clearing 
members  under  [§3(2)2]  of  the  Membership  Regulation,  any  member  who  entrusts  the 
settlement of the securities trades carried out in its name to clearing members. (Amended on 
January 28, 2009) 

(4)  The  term  "quotation"  in  this  Regulation  refers  to  an  act  of  a  member  that  expresses  its 
intention to buy or sell for trading securities in the Market, and shall be classified into each of 
the following types: (Amended on January 28, 2009; July 21, 2010; December 1, 2010; July 6, 
2011; February 5, 2025) 

1. Limit quotation: A quotation specifying the issue of a listed security (hereinafter referred 
to as "issue"), the quantity to be traded and the price at which the trade be executed (in the 
case of foreign currency denominated debt securities, the points shall be deemed the prices, 
and  in  the  case  of  the  repurchase  agreements  mentioned  in  Section  6  of  Chapter  IV,  the 
repurchase rates shall be deemed the prices; the same hereinafter); 

2.  Market  quotation:  A  quotation  specifying  the  issue  and  quantity,  but  not  the  price, 
intending to trade at a price that is deemed in [§23(3)] and [§24(2)], respectively; 

3.  Limit-to-market-on-close  quotation:  A  limit  quotation  with  a  condition  that  it  will  be 
converted into a market quotation at the time when the price at the time of market closing 
is determined by the call auction pursuant to the provisions of [§23(1)4];   

4. Immediately executable limit quotation: A quotation specifying the issue and quantity, 
but  intending  to  trade  by  deeming  that  the  price  is  specified  at  a  price  set  by  the 
Enforcement Rules of this Regulation (hereinafter referred to as the “Enforcement Rules”), 
1 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
on the basis of bid prices in the case of an offer quotation and on the basis of offer prices in 
the case of a bid quotation;   

5.  Best  limit  quotation:  A  quotation  specifying  the  issue  and  quantity,  but  intending  to 
trade by deeming that the price is specified at a price set by the Enforcement Rules, on the 
basis of other bid prices in the case of a bid quotation and on the basis of other offer prices 
in the case of an offer quotation;   

6. Auction-based block trade quotation: A quotation specifying the issue and quantity, but 
intending to trade at a price under [§30-2(1)] or [§34-3(1)]. 

7.  Mid-price  quotation:  A  quotation  specifying  the  issue  and  quantity,  but  intending  to 
trade by deeming that the price is specified at a price set by the Enforcement Rules, on the 
basis of prices of the bid and offer quotations; and [February 5, 2025] 

8. Stop-limit quotation: A quotation specifying the issue, quantity and price, but intending 
to  trade  when  the  stop  price  (referring  to  the  conditional  price  for  making  the  limit 
quotation effective) satisfies the conditions stipulated in the Enforcement Rules. [February 
5, 2025] 

(5) The term "order" means an act of a customer that expresses the intention to buy or sell for 
the  trading  of  securities,  and  shall  be  classified  into  each  of  the  followings:  (Amended  on 
May 13, 2005;  January  28, 2009;  July 21,  2010; December 1, 2010; July 6, 2011;  July  10, 
2019; February 5, 2025) 

1. Limit order: An order specifying the issue, quantity, and the price;   

2. Market order: An order specifying the issue and quantity, not the price, thus intending 
to trade at a price deemed in [§23(3)] and [§24(2)], respectively;     

3. Limit-to-market-on-close order: A limit order with a condition that it will be converted 
into a market order at the time when the price at the time of market closing is determined 
by the call auction pursuant to the provisions of [§23(1)4];   

4.  Immediately  executable  limit  order:  An  order  specifying  the  issue  and  quantity,  but 
intending to trade at a price specified in paragraph (4)4; 

5. Best limit order: An order specifying the issue and quantity, but intending to trade at a 
price specified in paragraph (4)5; 

6.  Target  price  order:  An  order  specifying  the  issue  and  quantity,  but  specifying  the 
condition  to  execute  the  trade  at  a  specific  price  level  such  as  the  trading  volume 
weighted average price under [§34-3(1)1];   

7.  Auction-based  block  trade  order:  An  order  specifying  the  issue  and  quantity,  but 
intending to trade at a price under [§30-2(1)] or [§34-3(1)]. 

8. Mid-price order: An order specifying the issue and quantity, but intending to trade by 
deeming that the price is specified at a price set in paragraph (4)7; and [February 5, 2025] 
2 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
9.  Stop-limit  order:  An  order  specifying  the  issue,  quantity  and  price,  but  intending  to 
trade when the stop price satisfies the conditions. [February 5, 2025] 

(6)  The  term  "simultaneous  quotation"  in  this  Regulation  means  the  quotations  that  are 
treated identically, irrespective of the sequence of the time they were received.   

(7)  The  term  "opening  price"  in  this  Regulation  means  the  first  price  established  after  the 
opening time of the regular session (hereinafter referred to as the “market opening").   

(8)  The  term  "closing  price"  in  this  Regulation  means  the  last  price  (including  special 
quotations) establish before the time of closing of the regular session (hereinafter referred to 
the “market closing”).   

(9) The term "special quotations" in this Regulation means, in the case of issues that are not 
executed  until  the  time  of  market  closing,  if  there  are  offer  quotations  (excluding  an  offer 
quotation submitted as a mid-price quotation; the same hereinafter in this paragraph) with the 
prices that are lower than the base price stipulated in [§20(1)], the lowest price of such offer 
quotation or if there are offer quotations (excluding a bid quotation submitted as a mid-price 
quotation and all bid quotations submitted to  different  classes of  stocks for which the price 
difference  from  the  price  of  a  common  stock  falls  under  the  criteria  specified  in  the 
Enforcement  Rules;  the  same  hereinafter  in  this  paragraph)  with  the  prices  that  are  higher 
than  the  base  price,  the  highest  price  of  such  bid  quotation.  In  such  cases,  the  market 
quotation  shall  use  the  prices  determined  in  accordance  with  [§23(3)]  and  [§24(2)]. 
(Amended on December 29, 2009; April 18, 2012; February 5, 2025)       

(10) The term “customer account transaction” in this Regulation refers to buying or selling of 
securities that the member conducts after receiving a trade entrustment from a person other 
than the member concerned. (Amended on January 28, 2009; March 8, 2021) 

(11)  The  term  “self-account  transaction”  refers  to  buying  or  selling  of  securities  that  the 
member conducts in its name using its own account. (Amended on January 28, 2009; March 
8,2021)     

(12)  The  term  "depositary  receipts  (DRs)  of  foreign  stocks"  in  this  Regulation  means  the 
depositary  receipts  mentioned  in  [§2(1)4]  of  the  KOSPI  Market  Listing  Regulation 
(hereinafter referred to as “the Listing Regulation”). (Amended on January 28, 2009; July 6, 
2011; February 22, 2013) 

(13) The term “collective investment securities of Exchange-Traded Funds (ETFs) (including 
the collective investment securities of foreign ETFs: the same hereinafter)” in this Regulation 
means the stocks of ETF investment company or beneficiary certificates of ETF investment 
trust under Article 234(1) of the Act. (Amended on July 16, 2009) 

(14)  The  term  “equity-linked  warrants  (ELWs)”  in  this  Regulation  means  the  derivatives 
linked securities under Article 125(2)6 of the Enforcement Decree of the Act. (Amended on

§60.  Definitions 

The  terms  used  in  this  Section  shall  have  the  meaning  given  to  them  in  each  of  the 
followings:   

1. The term “trading of bonds repurchase agreement” (hereinafter in this Section referred 
to as “trading of Repo”) means the trading of bonds under the condition that they will be 
repurchased or resold at an agreed upon price on the repurchase date; 

2. The term “repurchase date” means the date specified in the sales agreement, on which 
the seller of Repo (referred to as “the seller” hereinafter in this Section) makes the payment 
of repurchase price and  the buyer of Repo (hereinafter in  this Section referred to  as  “the 
buyer”) return the purchased bonds, thus terminating the sales agreement; 

3. The term  “repurchase  rate” means the annual  interest  rate that, in  accordance with  the 
sales agreement, the seller agrees to pay to the buyer on the repurchase date;

5.  The  term  “repurchase  price”  means  the  amount  that,  in  accordance  with  the  sales 
agreement, the seller pays to the buyer on the repurchase date; and   

6. The term “bid for general collateral Repo” means the quotation placed for the purpose of 
purchasing bonds under the condition of repurchase, without specifying the bond issue.

Repurchase  price  =  Purchase  price  ×  (1  +  Repurchase  rate  ×  Number  of  days  in  Repo

§104-3.  Registration  of  Algorithmic  Traders,  etc. 

(1)  Before  receiving  orders  for  high-speed  algorithmic  trading  from  a  customer,  a  member 
shall  register  the  relevant  customer  as  a  high-speed  algorithmic  trader  in  the  Exchange  as

Rules as deemed necessary by the Exchange to manage high-speed algorithmic trading. 
(2)  A  member  who  intends  to  engage  in  high-speed  algorithmic  trading  shall  report  to  the

(3) In the case of opening, changing, or closing of a customer’s or a member’s own account

(5) In the case where a  member’s customer falls under any of following subparagraphs, the 
member  shall  take  measures  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  the  “high-speed  algorithmic  trading

intentionally omitted important matters when registering through the member: or 

3.  Other  cases  where  the  Enforcement  Rules  stipulates  that  it  is  necessary  to  suspend 

receiving orders for high-speed algorithmic trading. 

(6) In the case where the Exchange deems inappropriate for a member’s customer who is a

§112.  Auction  of  Securities 

(1) Any person  who wishes  to  entrust  auctioning of  securities (referring  to the cases where 
the Exchange receives an entrustment from the person who wishes to sell the securities, and 
sell them to the person who wishes to buy: the same hereinafter) shall file a request with the 
Exchange. (Amended on January 28, 2009) 

(2)  An  auction  shall  be  accomplished  by  inviting  bids.  Provided  that,  when  the  Exchange 
deems  it  necessary,  it  may  be  carried  out  by  way  of  holding  a  floor  auction  session. 
(Amended on August 28, 2019) 

(3) Bidding shall be deemed valid even if there is only one bidder,   

(4) In the case of bidding, the successful bidders, among the bidders whose bidding price is 
higher than the expected price, shall be all bidders with the bidding prices at which the entire 
quantity of entrusted securities is sold by successively lowering bidding price, starting with 
the highest bidding price. In the case of floor auction session, the successful bidder shall be 
the auction subscriber with the highest bidding price, among the bidders whose bidding price 
78

(5) In addition to paragraphs (1) through (4), the matters relating to the request for auction, 
auction  subscription,  receiving  auction  entrustment,  method  of  determining  auctioneer, 
auction  agreement  and  auction  procedures  and  any  other  matters  necessary  for  auctioning 
shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.
