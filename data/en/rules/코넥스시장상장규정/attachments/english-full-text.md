CHAPTER I. GENERAL PROVISIONS   

§1. Purpose   

The  purpose  of  this  Regulation  is  to,  in  accordance  with  Article  390  of  the  Financial 
Investment  Services and Capital  Market  Act  (hereinafter referred to  as  "the Act"), stipulate 
the matters necessary for the Korea Exchange (hereinafter referred to as "the Exchange") to 
review the securities to be listed and administer the securities listed on the KONEX Market.   

§2. Definitions   

(1)  The  term  "KONEX-listed  corporation"  in  this  Regulation  shall  mean  a  corporation  that 
has issued the securities listed on the KONEX Market. 

(2) The term “listed issues” in this Regulation shall mean stocks, etc. issued by the KONEX-
listed companies, which have been listed in accordance with this Regulation. 

(3) The term “initial listing” in this Regulation shall mean the listing on the KONEX Market 
of the stocks which have not been listed on the KONEX Market. 

(4)  The  term  “KONEX-listing  applicant”  in  this  Regulation  shall  mean  a  corporation 
preparing for initial listing in the KONEX Market. 

(5) The term  “venture capital” in  this Regulation  shall mean  a person coming under any of 
Article 2-2(1)2a of the Special Act on the Promotion of Venture Businesses. 

(6)  The  term  “the  largest  shareholder,  etc.”  in  this  Regulation  shall  mean  the  largest 
shareholder  pursuant  to  Article  2(6)a  of  the  Act  on  Corporate  Governance  of  Financial 
Companies.  In  this  case,  “financial  company”  shall  be  deemed  “corporation”  and  “shares 
issued  (including  capital  contribution;  the  same  hereinafter)”  shall  be  deemed  “”shares 
issued.”  Provided  that,  in  cases  where  a  venture  capital  is  the  largest  shareholder,  a  person 
who  becomes  the  largest  shareholder  after  excluding  the  shares  owned  by  the  concerned 
venture capital shall also be deemed the largest shareholder if the person  participates in the 
management; and the stock owned for the investment purpose by those professional investors 
specified in the Enforcement Rules, among professional investor prescribed in Article 9(5) of 
the  Act  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the  professional  investor”),  shall  be  excluded  from  the 
number  of  shares  held  at  the  time  of  determining  the  largest  shareholder.  (Amended  on 
December 14, 2016) 

(7) The term “affiliated persons” in this Regulation shall mean persons falling under any of 
subparagraphs  under  Article  3(1)  of  the  Enforcement  Decree  of  the  Act  on  Corporate 
Governance  of  Financial  Companies.  In  this  case,  officers  shall  include  executive  officers

§3. Application Standards   

(1) The audit report applicable to this Regulation shall refer to the audit report [including the 
audit report on the consolidated financial statement, in the case of a corporation (hereinafter 
referred to as “the corporation subject to the preparation of consolidated financial statement”) 
owning  a  subsidiary  stated  in  Article  3(1)  of  the  Enforcement  Decree  of  Act  on  External 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Audit of Corporations] which the auditor, stated in Article 2(7) of the Act on External Audit 
of Corporations (limited to  accounting firms  designated pursuant  to  Article  11 of the Same 
Act  or  appointed  pursuant  to  Article  12  of  the  same  Act;  hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the 
auditor”), prepared subsequent to auditing of the financial statement (referring to the financial 
statement  stated  in  Article  2/2  of  the  same  Act;  the  same  hereinafter)  or  the  consolidated 
financial statement (referring to the consolidated financial statement  stated in Article 2/3 of 
the  same  Act:  the  same  hereinafter)  prepared  in  accordance  with  the  accounting  standards 
stated in  Article 5(1) of  the Act on External  Audit of Corporations.  In such cases,  in cases 
where  the  concerned  auditor  including  the  accountant  in  charge  has  interests  in  the 
corporation-to-be-listed  by  holding  the  stocks  or  equity-related  bonds,  or  acquiring  stock 
options of the corporation concerned, etc., the audit report prepared by the auditor concerned 
shall be excluded. (Amended on April 17, 2019)   

(2)  Unless  otherwise  specified  in  this  Regulation,  the  financial  details  applicable  to  this 
Regulation  shall  be  based  on  the  financial  statements  in  which  the  auditor’s  opinion  is 
unqualified  or  qualified  due  to  an  uncertainty  over  the  company’s  ability  to  continue  as  a 
going  concern.  Provided  that,  in  cases  where  the  auditor’s  opinion  is  qualified  due  to  a 
violation  of  accounting  standards,  the  adjusted  financial  details  that  reflect  the  concerned 
violation shall be applicable. (Amended on April 27, 2022)

§4. Appointment of Designated Advisor, etc.   

(1)  A  KONEX-listing  applicant  and  a  KONEX-listed  corporation  shall  appoint  a  person 
(hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the  designated  advisor”)  who  performs  the  following  tasks: 
(Amended on June 14, 2017; April 27, 2022) 

1. Due diligence and listing eligibility review of a corporation to be listed in the KONEX 
Market;     

2.  Recommendation  and  advice  on  initial  listing  procedures  for  a  KONEX-listing 
applicant, and handling of affairs related to initial listing; 

3.  Regular  and  continuous  advice  for  a  KONEX  listed  corporation  on  compliance  with 
financial laws; 

4. Disclosure and reporting on behalf of a KONEX-listed corporation; 

5. Fulfillment of obligation to submit liquidity provision quotations pursuant to [§18] of 
the KONEX Market Business Regulation for the stocks of a KONEX-listed corporation; 

6.  Preparation  and  posting  of  reports  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the  corporate  status 
report”) analyzing the current status of business and financial situation and their forecasts 
of a KONEX-listed corporation; 

7. Recommendation and support for holding IR events for KONEX-listed corporations; 

8. Record and storage of task-related contents as a designated advisor; and 

9. Other duties that the Exchange deems necessary in performing the tasks of a designated 
advisor. 

(2) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), in case of falling under any of following subparagraphs, 
the  designated  advisor  may  not  conduct  the  task  stated  in  the  subparagraph  concerned. 
Provided that this shall not apply to cases that are specified in the Enforcement Rules. [June 
14, 2017] (Amended on April 27, 2022) 

1.  Cases  that  do  not  fall  under  the  proviso  to  the  part  other  than  each  subparagraph  of 
[§23(1)] of the KONEX Market Disclosure Regulation and where the disclosure officer of 
the  KONEX-listed  corporation  discloses  and  reports:  The  task  stated  in  paragraph  (1)4; 
and 

2. Where a designated advisor does not submit liquidity provision quotations pursuant to 
the  proviso  to  [§17(1)]  of  the  KONEX  Market  Business  Regulation:  The  task  stated  in 
paragraph (1)5. 

(3) Where a KONEX-listing applicant and a KONEX-listed corporation enter an appointment 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
contract for the designated advisor with a designated advisor pursuant to paragraph (1), they 
shall determine the rights and obligations necessary between the relevant corporation and the 
designated  advisor  in  order  to  comply  with  this  Regulation.  In  such  cases,  the  matters 
prescribed by the Enforcement Rules shall be included.   

(4)  The validity of the appointment contract  for designated advisors  stated in paragraph (3) 
shall  continue  as  long  as  the  listing  of  the  corporation  concerned  continues,  and  if  the 
designated  advisor  changes  or  the  contents  of  the  appointment  contract  change,  it  shall  be 
reported in writing to the Exchange without delay. (Amended on June 14, 2017) 

(5)  A  KONEX-listing  applicant  and  a  KONEX-listed  corporation  shall  conduct  the

KONEX-listed corporation may also be replaced by notification to the designated advisor in

be prescribed by the Enforcement Rules.

§8. Duties of Designated Advisor   

(1) Designated advisors shall fulfill their duty of care as a good manager in  conducting the 
tasks of designated advisors. 

(2)  Designated  advisors  shall  maintain  independence  from  stakeholders  including  the 
corporation in charge. (Amended on June 10, 2015) 

(3)  Designated  advisors  shall  have  no  conflicts  of  interest  from  stakeholders  including  the 
corporation in charge and establish a system to prevent conflicts of interest. 

(4)  Designated  advisors  must  be  fair  and  appropriate  in  contracting  with  the  corporation  in 
charge of and performing their duties as designated advisors 

(5)  Designated  advisors  shall  maintain  the  confidentiality  of  information  acquired  while 
conducting the tasks of designated advisors. 

(6) Designated advisors shall report to the Exchange without delay if the resolution, decision, 
or  occurrence  of  a  matter  that  has  a  significant  impact  on  the  investment  judgment  of 
investors  or  the  maintenance  of  market  soundness  is  predicted  or  recognized  from  the 
corporation in charge. 

(7) The Exchange may determine necessary matters in relation to the performance of tasks of 
designated advisors under paragraphs (1) through (6) in the Enforcement Rules.

following  subparagraphs,  the  designated  advisor  shall  report  the  details  of  the  resolution, 
decision, or fact to the Exchange without delay: (Amended on June 10, 2015) 

      1. When a note or check issued by the corporation is defaulted or transactions with a bank 

are suspended or prohibited; 

      2. When an  application for commencement  of rehabilitation procedures  is  filed, when a 
decision is made to  commence rehabilitation procedures, or when a decision is made to 
abolish the commencement of rehabilitation procedures; 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
      3. When an application for bankruptcy is filed 

      4. When a cause for dissolution occurs 

      5. When an action such as revocation of authorization for investment dealing business or 

suspension of business has been received; 

      6. When a lawsuit that will significantly affect the performance of business is filed; 

      7.  When  there  is  a  change  in  the  working  unit  or  employee  in  charge  of  tasks  of 

designated advisory; 

      8. When a designated advisor or the person in charge of tasks of the designated advisor 

has been taken an action in violation of financial laws and regulations; or 

      9.  When  there  is  other  resolution,  decision  or  occurrence  of  a  matter  prescribed  in  the 
Enforcement Rules, which affects the performance of tasks of a designated advisor.

CHAPTER III. LISTING 

§12. Application for Initial Listing   

(1) The corporation intending to initially list its stocks on the KONEX Market shall undergo 
the  examination  by  the  Exchange  by  submitting  to  the  Exchange  the  application  for  initial 
listing  fifteen  (15)  days  before  the  desired  date  of  listing.  In  this  case,  if  it  is  deemed 
necessary  for  the  investor  protection  and  reliability  of  KONEX  Market,  the  Exchange  may 
discuss the listing procedures, etc. with the KONEX-listing applicant before the application 
for initial listing. 

(2) When a corporation intending to apply for initial listing of stocks pursuant to  paragraph 
(1) submits the application for initial listing, it shall file with the Exchange two (2) respective 
copies  of  application  for  initial  listing  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the  initial  listing 
application”) stipulated in the Enforcement Rules and report for listing eligibility (hereinafter 
referred  to  as  “the  listing  eligibility  report”)  prepared  by  the  designated  advisor  in  charge 
together with documents noted in each of the following subparagraphs. Provided that, if there 
is a correction or change in the initial listing application and listing eligibility report  due to 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
initial public offering, secondary offering or private placement, etc., the details of the initial 
listing application and listing eligibility report shall be corrected or changed and submitted to 
the  Exchange  by  the  date  of  completion  of  initial  public  offering,  secondary  offering  or 
private  placement  (referring  to  the  payment  date  for  stocks):  (Amended  on  June  18,  2014; 
October 1, 2014; June 10, 2015; April 17, 2019) 

1.  Two  (2)  respective  copies  of  the  financial  statements  (referring  to  the  statement  of 
financial  position,  income  statement  or  comprehensive  income  statement,  cash  flow 
statement, statement of capital change and supplementary schedules thereof, and including

(1) In case where there has been a change in the class, par-value stock into no-par value stock

(2)  The  provisions  of  [§12(3)]  shall  be  applied  mutatis  mutandis  to  the  case  of  applying

(3)  In  case  where  the  stocks  for  which  the  application  for  listing  change  (supplementary

postponed until the concerned cause are resolved: 

1.  In  case  where  a  lawsuit  concerning  such  matters  as  the  validity  of  issuance  of  new

including  the  case  where  there  is  insignificant  risk  of  undermining  the  safety  of 
transactions, shall be excluded;

class of stocks; and

concerned stocks for investor protection and reliability of the KONEX Market

(3)1,  the  applicant  for  listing  change  or  supplementary  listing  may  file  an  appeal  with  the

paragraph (4) shall be stipulated in the Enforcement Rules.

At  the  time  of  enforcement  of  this  Regulation,  the  previous  act,  which  is  related  to  a

§4. Special Cases Concerning Corporations Not Subject to External Audit 

When a corporation that does not meet the criteria of Article 2 of the External Audit Act in 
FY 2011 applies for initial listing on the KONEX Market, the audit report of the accounting 
firm that has audited only the financial statements of the corporation concerned for FY2012 
shall be replaced as the auditor's audit report.
