CHAPTER 1. GENERAL PROVISIONS 

§1. Purpose   

The purpose of this Guidelines is to stipulate the matters necessary for the management and 
operation of the joint compensation funds (hereinafter referred to as the “JCF”) pursuant to the 
Articles  323-14  and  394  of  the  Financial  Investment  Services  and  Capital  Markets  Act. 
(Amended on March 28, 2014; February 15, 2019) 

§2. Definition 

(1) The term ‘members’ in this Guidelines refers to the clearing members under [§3(2)1] of the 
Membership Regulation and the clearing member under the [§2(1)3] of the OTC Derivatives 
Clearing Business Regulation (hereinafter referred to as the “Clearing Business Regulation”). 
(Amended on March 28, 2014; February 15, 2019) 

(2) The term ‘Exchange market JCF’ in this Guidelines means the JCF of the securities market 
and  derivatives  market,  and  the  term  ‘OTC  derivatives  JCF’  means  the  JCF  for  the  OTC 
derivatives. [March 28, 2014] (Amended on February 15, 2019) 

(3) The term ‘general operation’ in this Guidelines refers to operating the JCF accumulated in 
cash through financial instruments that allow the deposit thereto and withdraw therefrom at 
any time. [December 30, 2010] (Amended on February 15, 2019) 

(4) The term ‘special operation’ in this Guidelines refers to operating the JCF contributed in 
cash  through  the  methods  other  than  the  general  operation  as  specified  in  each  of  the 
subparagraphs of [§5(10)]. [December 30, 2010] (Amended on February 15, 2019) 

(5) The term ‘management department’ in this Guidelines means a department that is in charge 
of  clearing  and  settlement  services  of  the  Korea  Exchange  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  “the 
Exchange”) and responsible for the matters concerning the management and general operation 
of the JCF. (Amended on December 30, 2010; March 28, 2014; February 15, 2019) 

(6) The term ‘operation department’ in this Guidelines means a department that is in charge of 
the  financial  and  accounting  business  of  the  Exchange  and  responsible  for  the  matters 
concerning the special operation of the JCF. (Amended on December 30, 2010; February 15, 
2019) 

(7) The term “CCP risk management department” in this Guidelines means a department that 
is in charge of the business of CCP risk management and responsible for the matters concerning 
suspension of special operation, etc. [February 15, 2019]

CHAPTER 2. OPERATION OF JCF 

Section 1. General Principles 

§3. Management and Operation of the JCF 

(1) The head of the management department shall manage the JCF separately for each member 
who has made contributions to the JCF. 

(2) The head of the management department and the head of the operation department shall 
keep accounting for the JCF separately from other properties of the Exchange. (Amended on 
December 30, 2010) 

(3) The head of the management department and the head of the operation department shall 
operate the JCF by classifying it as follows: (Amended on December 30, 2010; May 28, 2014; 
January 22, 2016) 

1. Securities market JCF;   

2. Derivatives market JCF;   

3. OTC derivatives JCF; and 

4.  The  JCFs  under subparagraphs  1  through  3  shall  be  managed  being  divided  into  the 
amount for compensation of damages already incurred and the amount for compensation 
of damages incurring from future settlement failures. 

(4) The operation of JCF accumulated in cash shall be classified into the general operation and 
the special operation. [December 30, 2010] 

(5) [Deleted on May 13, 2016] 

§4. Principles of Operation 

(1) The JCF shall be operated considering the safety as the top priority so that the operational 
risk can be minimized, while taking into account the cashability and profitability, etc. 

(2) When the JCF is operated by being deposited with a bank or a securities finance company 
(hereinafter referred to as “deposit operation”), the amount deposited with a single settlement 
bank or securities finance company shall not exceed 50 percent of the total amount for deposit 
operation. In the case of the OTC derivatives JCF, however, this shall apply only when the 
amount of cash deposit is no less than KRW 50 billion for twenty (20) or more trading days in 
a row. [November 13, 2014] (Amended May 21, 2015)

Section 2. General Operation 

§4-2. Methods of General Operation 

(1) The head of management department shall conduct the general operation of the JCF by the 
method of depositing it in financial instruments allowing deposit and withdrawal at any time 
which are managed by a bank falling under any of the following subparagraphs or a securities 
finance company (hereinafter referred to as the “settlement bank, etc.”): (Amended on June 24, 
2013; March 28, 2014) 

1. A bank where a fund settlement account is open for delivering and receiving settlement 
payments in the securities markets or net cash settlement amounts in the derivatives market; 
or 

2. A bank where an account under the name of the Exchange is open pursuant to [§69] of 
the Clearing Business Regulation. 

(2) In cases where the liquidity of the amount of JCF for general operation in a settlement bank, 
etc.  is  insufficient  or  expected  to  be  insufficient,  the  general  operation  amount  in  other 
settlement banks, etc. may be transferred to the concerned settlement bank, etc. (Amended on 
June 24, 2013) 

[December 30, 2010] 

§4-3. Amount of General Operation 

The general operation shall be conducted for the amount other than the amount managed under 
the special operation. [December 30, 2010] 

Section 3. Special Operation 

§5. Methods of Special Operation 

(1)  Pursuant  to  Articles  318-8(4)  and  362(5)  of  the  Enforcement  Decree  of  the  Financial 
Investment  Services  and  Capital  Markets  Act,  the  head  of  the  operation  department  shall 
conduct the special operation by the methods noted in each of the following subparagraphs: 
(Amended on December 30, 2010; March 28, 2014; February 15, 2019) 

1. Purchasing government bonds, municipal bonds or monetary stabilization bonds of the 
Bank of Korea under [§69] of the Bank of Korea Act; 

2. Purchasing guaranteed corporate bonds; 

3. Lending to, or depositing in securities finance companies or purchasing corporate bonds 
issued by securities finance companies; and

4. Depositing in banks. 

(2) The head of the operation department shall maintain the amount operated by the methods 
under subparagraphs 1 and 2 of paragraph (1) below 50 percent of the special operation amount. 
(Amended on December 30, 2010) 

(3) The head of the operation department may deposit a certain amount in financial instruments 
that  allow  deposit  and  withdrawal  at  any  time,  for  the  receipt  and  payment  of  the  special 
operation amount, management of proceeds therefrom and the convenience of conversion into 
cash. In this case, the operation amount shall be determined separately by the head of operation 
department. (Amended on December 30; 2010; January 22, 2016) 

(4) The head of the management department may request the head of the operation department 
for designation or change of the operation method within the scope specified in paragraphs (1) 
through (3). 

§5-2. Amount of Special Operation 

(1) The amount to be operated by special operation shall be determined  by the head of the 
management  department  considering  the  amount  accumulated  in  cash,  management  of 
settlement risks and other circumstances. (Amended on March 28; 2014 and January 22, 2016)

operation by the last trading day (in the case of OTC derivatives JCF, it shall be the last business

(1)  In  cases  where  the  liquidity  of  the  general  operation  is  insufficient  or  expected  to  be 
insufficient, the head of CCP risk management department may suspend the whole or a part of 
the special operation, in consultation with the head of operation department and the head of 
management department. (Amended on February 15, 2019)

Section 4. Calculation, etc. of Proceeds from JCF 

    [December 30, 2010] 

§7. Calculation of Proceeds 

(1) The proceeds from the JCF operation shall be calculated based on the date on which the 
proceeds accrue. In this case, for the discount bonds, the interests of which are paid in advance, 
from among the instruments referred to in subparagraphs 1 through 3 of [§5(1)], the maturity 
date or the disposal date shall be deemed the accrual date of proceeds. (Amended on October 
23, 2009; December 30, 2010) 

(2) In cases where corporate income tax or local income tax was withheld when calculating the 
proceeds  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1),  the  withheld  tax  amount  shall  be  deducted,  and  the 
deducted corporate income tax or local income tax shall be added up to the member margin 
when the Exchange receives the tax deduction on the withheld corporate income tax or local 
income  tax  through  the  corporate  tax  report  or  the  local  income  tax  report.  (Amended  on 
October 23, 2009; December 30, 2010; March 30, 2016) 

(3)  When  calculating  the  proceeds  pursuant  to  paragraph  (1),  the  head  of  the  operation 
department shall notify the head of the management department of the information on accrual 
period, amount of proceeds and details of withholding tax thereof, etc., along with the status of 
operation, on a quarterly basis. (Amended on December 30, 2010) 

(4) The  head  of  the  operation  department  shall  deposit  the accrued  proceeds  in  the  general 
operation account under [§4-2(1)]. [December 30, 2010] 

§8. Pro Rata Allocation of Proceeds and Notification Thereof 

(1) The head of the management  department shall make pro rata allocation of the proceeds 
from the operation of the JCF according to the cash contribution ratio of each member during 
the accrual period of proceeds on the basis of every quarter-end, and add the proceeds to the 
principal amount within twenty (20) days from the first day of the next quarter. (Amended on 
October 23, 2009; December 30; 2010; March 28, 2014) 

5 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(2) The cash contribution ratio noted in paragraph (1) shall be calculated by dividing the total 
amount of daily cash contribution to the JCF of a member by the aggregate amount of daily 
total cash contribution to the JCF, and the figures after the 15th decimal place shall be discarded. 
(Amended on October 23, 2009; December 30, 2010; March 28, 2014) 

(3) [Deleted on October 23, 2009] 

(4) The pro rata allocation amount of each member shall be rounded off at the first decimal 
place,  and,  if,  as  a  result  of  the  round-off,  the  total  amount  of  proceeds  allocated  to  each 
member exceeds (or falls short of) the total amount of proceeds to be allocated, the difference 
shall be retained by deducting (or adding) KRW 1 from (or to) the members in turn one after 
another from the member with the largest amount of allocation. (Amended on March 28, 2014) 

§9. Receipt or Payment of Difference in JCF, etc. 

(1) The head of the management department shall receive or pay the clearing members the 
Exchange market JCF contribution amounts from or to the clearing members pursuant to [§23] 
of the Membership Regulation and [§9-3] of the Enforcement Rules of the same Regulation, 
and  receive  or  pay  the  OTC  derivatives  JCF  contribution  amounts  from  or  to  the  clearing 
members pursuant to [§26] of the OTC Derivatives Clearing Business Regulation and [§13] of 
the Enforcement Rules of the same Regulation. 

(2) Notwithstanding paragraph (1), if it deems necessary, the Exchange may change the period 
of receipt or payment of the difference of the JCF. 

(3) The head of the management department shall handle the deposit or withdrawal of the JCF 
in accordance with [§9-3(6)] of the Enforcement Rules of Membership Regulation and [§14(4)] 
of the Enforcement Rules of OTC Derivatives Clearing Business Regulation. 

(4) The matters related to the notification, receipt or payment, deposit, withdrawal, etc. of the 
contribution amount of the JCF and the matters related to the pro rata allocation of proceeds 
and the notification thereof, etc. shall be handled through the Exchange system and the OTC 
derivatives clearing system of the Exchange or other methods recognized by the Exchange.   

[Wholly amended on September 28, 2018] 

§9-2. Settlement for withdrawing/converting Members 

(1) When a clearing member withdraws its membership or converts into a non-clearing member, 
the head of the management department shall return or pay the principal and proceeds of the 
Exchange  market  JCF  to  the  relevant  member  as  stipulated  in  each  of  the  following 
subparagraphs: (Amended on December 30, 2010; March 28, 2014) 

1. For principal, within twenty (20) days from the date of withdrawal or conversion; and 

2. For proceeds, within twenty (20) days from the end of the quarter which includes the 
date when the Exchange received the proceeds. 

6 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(2)  When  a  clearing  member  withdraws  its  membership,  the  head  of  the  management 
department shall return or pay the principal and proceeds of the OTC derivatives JCF to the 
relevant member as stipulated in each of the following subparagraphs: (Amended on March 28, 
2014) 

1. For principal, within twenty (20) days from the date of withdrawal. However, for any 
member  who  is  deemed  a  clearing  member  pursuant  to  [§20]  of  the  Clearing  Business 
Regulation, within 20 days from the date when the loss caused by a settlement default of 
other clearing members was finalized and completely compensated; and 

2. For proceeds, within twenty (20) days from the end of the quarter which includes the date 
when the Exchange received the proceeds. 

(3) Notwithstanding paragraphs (1) and (2), in cases where it is impossible to make the payment 
of proceeds due to the extinction of corporate personality, etc., the proceeds shall belong to the 
members as of the accrual date of the proceeds. [March 28, 2014] 

[October 23, 2009] 

§10. Use of JCF 

CHAPTER 3. USE OF JCF 

(1)  In  the  event  that  a  settlement  default  of  a  member  has  occurred,  the  head  of  CCP  risk 
management department may use the JCF to compensate the losses incurred by the settlement 
default. (Amended on December 30, 2010; March 28, 2014; February 15, 2019) 

(2) In the case of paragraph (1), the priority of using the financial resources shall be determined 
considering the cashability among the JCFs that are operated by the operation methods under 
[§5]. (Amended on March 28, 2014) 

(3)  Notwithstanding  paragraphs  (1)  and  (2),  in  cases  where  a  member  fails  to  deposit  the 
settlement  amount  or  the  net  cash  settlement  amount  by  the  deadline  and  subsequently  the 
Exchange uses the JCF to supply liquidity pursuant to [§36] of the Securities Market Clearing 
and Settlement Regulations, [§105] of the Derivatives Market Business Regulation and [§73] 
of  the  OTC  Derivatives  Clearing  Business  Regulation,  such  liquidity  provision  shall  be 
conducted as separately set by the Exchange. (Amended on December 30, 2010; September 17, 
2013; March 28, 2014; February 27, 2025)

ADDENDA 

(February 27, 2009) 

§1. Effective Date 

This Guidelines shall become effective on March 2, 2009. 

§2. Repeal of Other Guidelines 

The  Management  Guidelines  of  the  Default  Loss  Joint  Compensation  Fund  and  the 
Management Guidelines of the Joint Compensation Fund shall be repealed at the same time 
when this Guidelines becomes effective. 

ADDENDA 
(October 23, 2009) 

§1. Effective Date 

This Guidelines shall become effective on November 1, 2009. 

§2. Cases of Application concerning Calculation and Payment of Proceeds 

The proceeds from financial instruments in operation as of the effective date of this Guidelines 
shall be paid pro rata in the month following the end of the quarter which includes the date 
when the relevant proceeds were received. In this case, the proceeds prepaid in accordance with 
the former Guidelines shall be deducted. 

ADDENDA 
(December 30, 2010) 

§1. Effective Date 

This Guidelines shall become effective on January 1, 2011. However, the amended provisions 
of [§5(2)] shall become effective on April 1, 2011. 

§2.Interim Measures for Special Operation Amount 

Notwithstanding the amended provisions of [§5-2(1)], the amount of special operation shall be 
maintained at least 80% of the total accumulation amount of the JCFs of the securities market 
and the derivatives market until March 31, 2011 and at least 65% until June 30, 2011.
