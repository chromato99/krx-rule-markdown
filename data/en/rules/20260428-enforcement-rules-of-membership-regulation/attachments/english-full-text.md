Enforcement Rules of Membership Regulation 

§1. Purpose 

The  purpose  of  this  Enforcement  Rules  is  to  stipulate  the  matters  necessary  for  the 
enforcement of the Membership Regulation. (Amended on May 6, 2020) 

§2. Definitions 

The terms used in this Enforcement Rules shall have the same meanings as those given 
to  them  in  the  Membership  Regulation  (hereinafter  referred  to  as  the  “Regulation”). 
(Amended on May 6, 2020) 

§3. Application for Membership 

(1)  The  applicant  who  seeks  the  approval  of  application  for  membership  pursuant  to 
[§5(1)] of the Regulation shall submit to the Exchange the application for membership 
in [Annex: Form 1] and documents attached thereto. (Amended on May 6, 2020) 

(2) [Deleted on May 6, 2020]   

(3)  Pursuant  to  [§5(7)]  of  the  Regulation,  in  the  case  where  a  person  who  wish  to 
become  a  collective  investment  securities  member  falls  under  any  of  the  following 
subparagraphs, the person shall be deemed not to correspond to those who are eligible 
to  apply  for  the  membership  in  accordance  with  [§5(2)]  of  the  Regulation.  [July  20, 
2023] 

1.  Where, in the process of reviewing the details of an application for the approval 
of  investment  dealing  business  or  investment  brokerage  business  in  accordance 
with  Article  13(2)  of  the  Financial  Investment  Services  and  Capital  Market  Act 
(hereinafter  referred  to  as  the  “Act”)  for  collective  investment  securities  noted  in 
Article 15(2)6 of the Enforcement Decree of the Financial Investment Services and 
Capital Market Act (hereinafter referred to as the “Enforcement Decree of the Act), 
an examination necessary to participate in trading in the stock market opened by the 
Exchange has not been conducted; or 

2.  Where, due to approval conditions, etc. under Article 13(4) of the Act, it is not 
possible to participate in trading of all or part of the collective investment securities 
of ETFs noted in Article 234 of the Act.     

§3-2. Physical Facility Requirement 

１ 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
Pursuant  to  [§6(3)]  of  the  Regulation,  an  entity  intending  to  become  a  member  shall 
meet each of the following items in connection with facilities such as computer facilities, 
etc.: 

3.  To  be  equipped  with  the  computer  facilities  and  telecommunication  facilities 
that  are  necessary  for  the  reliable  and  efficient  execution  of  transactions  in  the

§3-3. Human Resource Requirement 

Pursuant  to  [§6(3)]  of  the  Regulation,  an  entity  intending  to  become  a  member  shall 
meet  each  of  the  following  items  in  relation  to  the  expertise  and  fidelity  of  human 
resources necessary to perform tasks: 

1.  To  be  adequately  staffed  with  the  employees  corresponding  to  any  of  the 
following items in order to carry out tasks related to trading in the Exchange market 
and perform the pertinent functions as a member: 

a.  Person  who  has  specific  qualifications  related  to  the  tasks  concerned 
(including the tasks incidental thereto: the same hereinafter) or who has at least 
five (5) years of work experiences in performing the concerned tasks; 

b.  Person  who  has  considerable  professional  knowledge  and  competence 
subsequent to the completion of professional training program (graduate school 
etc.) or study of courses required to perform the tasks concerned, and who has at 
least two (2) years of work experiences in performing the task concerned; 

c.  Person  who  has  substantial  basis  to  be  considered  to  have  equivalent 
experience or capabilities to the persons noted in items 1a and 1b above. 

2.  To assign specialists appropriate for the accomplishment of such tasks as trade 
execution  and  settlement,  computer  system  management,  risk  management  and 
internal  control,  compliance  and  audit,  etc.  taking  into  account  the  contents, 
coverage and volume of the tasks. 

[May 6, 2020]

§3-4. Social Confidence Requirements 

(1) Pursuant to [§6(3)] of the Regulation, an entity intending to become a member shall 
meet requirements in each of the following subparagraphs in connection with the social 
confidence: (Amended on July 20, 2023) 

      1.  The  corporations,  etc.  [including  the  branches  or  business  offices  of  foreign 
financial  investment  companies  specified  in  Article  12(2)1  of  the  Act]  that  have 
submitted applications for membership and their directors, largest shareholders, etc. 
(referring to the largest shareholder specified in [§12(2)6] of the Act, and in the case 
of the branches and business offices of foreign investment companies, it refers to the 
foreign  financial  investment  company  specified  in  [§12(2)6]  of  the  Act:  the  same 
hereinafter) shall not correspond to the cases stated in any of the following items in 
relation  to  trading  of  securities  and  derivative  products  for  a  one  (1)  year  period 
prior to the application date for the membership. 

          a. Cases where a judgment has been finalized or is being executed, as a result of 
having being sentenced to a fine or a severer sentence pursuant to the Korean or 
foreign financial laws; or 

          b.  Cases  where  executive  officers,  largest  shareholders,  etc.  of  the  concerned 
corporations,  etc.  have  received  disciplinary  actions  such  as  suspension  from 
exercise  of  executive  authority  or  suspension  from  office  (in  case  of  the 
concerned  corporations,  etc.  or  a  corporation  that  is  the  largest  shareholder,  the 
suspension of business/operation) or severer actions by the supervisory authority 
pursuant to the Act (including the cases of being imposed of disciplinary actions 
equivalent thereto by the Exchange in accordance with the business regulations of 
the  Exchange),  or  are  under  such  disciplinary  period.  However,  exceptions  may 
be  made  in  case  a  period  longer  than  two  (2)  times  the  disciplinary  period  has 
elapsed since the date of receiving disciplinary action 

      2.  In  the  case  where  there  has  been  voluntary  withdrawal  from  any  of  types  of 
membership of the Exchange within three (3) years prior to the date of application 
for membership, it should be acknowledged that the reason for the withdrawal was 
not to evade the obligation of a member or undermined the sound market practice.   

      3.  In  the  case  where  there  has  been  an  expulsion,  suspension  of  all  or  part  of 
transactions, or other similar disciplinary measures in accordance with the business 
standards of multilateral trading companies within three (3) years prior to the date of 
application  for  membership,  it  shall  be  recognized  that  the  reason  for  such 
disciplinary measures does not harm sound market order. [February 27, 2025] 

(2)  In  the  case  stated  in  any  of  followings  or  equivalent  thereto,  the  Exchange  may 
postpone the review for membership until the time such case is validated:   

３

undertaken  by  such  agencies  as  the  Prosecutors’  Office,  Fair  Trade  Commission, 
Financial  Service  Commission,  Financial  Supervisory  Service  and  Exchange 
against  the  concerned  company,  etc.,  its  executive  officers  or  largest  shareholder,

2.  Cases  where  the  merger,  dissolution,  closedown  of  business,  or  transfer  of

§9-2. Cap Periods 

(1) The term “period specified in the Enforcement Rules” in [§22-2] of the Regulation 
shall be ten (10) trading days (hereinafter referred to as the “cap period”) from the day 
when  a  settlement  failure  by  the  defaulting  clearing  member  has  occurred  in  the 
securities markets, etc. (Amended on August 3, 2020; February 27, 2025) 

(2) In cases where another settlement failure occurs in the securities markets, etc. where 
a  settlement  failure  has  occurred  during  the  cap  period,  the  Exchange  may  extend  the 
cap period within ten (10) trading days. (February 27, 2025) 

(3) In cases where the cap period commences or is extended, the Exchange shall notify 
such fact to all clearing members.

§9-3. Management of Basic Joint Compensation Fund 

(1) The term “date specified in the Enforcement Rules” in [§23(1)1] of the Regulation 
means the day when the estimated total contribution amount of the joint compensation 
fund  that  is  calculated  on  a  daily  basis  to  check  the  appropriateness  of  the  total 
accumulation amounts of the joint compensation fund of the securities market  and the 
derivatives  market  [referring  to  the  amount  obtained  by  subtracting  the  amount  of

2019) 

(2) The term “period specified in the Enforcement Rules” in [§23(1)1] of the Regulation 
means the period from the date of  calculation  of the basic joint  compensation  fund to

on April 25, 2019)

such  cases,  the  results  of  the  stress  testing  shall  be  reported  to  the  Risk  Management

§10. Operation of Basic Joint Compensation Funds                                           
(Amended on December 30, 2015) 

(1) In the case of operating the basic joint compensation funds contributed in cash, the 
Exchange  shall  operate  the  basic  joint  compensation  fund  of  the  securities  markets 
separately from the basic joint compensation fund of the derivatives market. (Amended 
on February 2, 2009; December 27, 2010; December 30, 2015)   

(2)  In  cases  where  the  Exchange  operates  the  basic  joint  compensation  funds 
contributed in cash by depositing them in banks or securities finance company among 
the ways specified in each subparagraph of Article 362(5) of the Enforcement Decree of 
the Act, the Exchange shall avoid depositing the funds in a single bank or the securities 
finance company in accordance with the criteria that the Exchange separately stipulates. 
[November 14, 2014] (Amended on December 30, 2015; May 6, 2020; July 20, 2023) 

８ 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
(3)  In the case of operating  the basic joint  compensation  funds contributed  in  cash  by 
depositing them in banks pursuant to Article 362(5)4 of the Enforcement Decree of the 
Act, the funds shall be deposited in the bank whose capital ratio according to the Bank 
of  International  Settlements  is  8/100  or  higher.  (Amended  on  February  2,  2009; 
December 27, 2010; December 30, 2015; May 6, 2020) 

(4) When making proportional distribution of the proceeds accrued from the operation 
of basic joint compensation funds contributed in cash, the Exchange shall calculate the 
cash  contribution  ratios  pursuant  to  [§23(6)]  of  the  Regulation  while  taking  into 
consideration  the  amount  and  the  period  of  contribution  that  each  clearing  member 
made to the basic joint compensation funds in cash during the period when the proceeds 
accrued. (Amended on December 27, 2010; December 30, 2015; May 6, 2020)         

§11.  Contributions  to  Basic  Joint  Compensation  Funds  by  Debt  Securities  Members

amount equivalent to 25/100 of the basic contribution amount specified in [§24(1)1]

(2)  Notwithstanding  paragraph  (1),  paragraph  (1)  shall  not  apply  in  the  case  where  a

3, 2020; February 27, 2025) 

§11-2. Amount of Re-contributions to the Basic Joint Compensation Funds, Etc. 

(1) “By the date set in the Enforcement Rules” in [§24-2(2)] of the Regulation shall be 
by 4:00 pm on the 3rd trading day counting from the day when the Exchange’s request 
for  re-contribution  was  received.  Provided,  that,  where  the  board  of  directors  of  the 
Exchange deems it necessary, the Exchange may set such period differently. 

(2) The amount of re-contributions to the basic joint compensation funds that clearing 
members are obliged to  make pursuant  to  [§24-2(4)] shall be  classified  in  each of the 
following  subparagraphs,  according  to  whether  or  not  the  amount  of  losses  incurred 

９ 

 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
from  settlement  failures  was  confirmed  during  the  cap  period  at  the  time  when  the 
Exchange  requests  the  clearing  members  to  make  re-contributions  to  the  basic  joint 
compensation funds. (Amended on April 25, 2019) 

    1.  Where  the  losses  are  confirmed:  The  amount  is  calculated  by  subtracting  the 
balance  of  joint  compensation  fund  as  of  the  last  day  of  the  cap  period  from  the 
amount obtained by applying mutatis mutandis [§24(1) or (2)] of the Regulation. In 
such  cases,  the  “immediately  preceding  the  calculation  date  of  the  basic  joint 
compensation fund” shall be construed as “preceding the last day of cap period”. 

    2.  Where  the  losses  are  not  yet  confirmed:  The  amount  is  calculated  by  applying 
mutatis  mutandis  [§24(1) or (2)] of the Regulation.  In such cases, the “immediately 
preceding  the  calculation  date  of  the  basic  joint  compensation  fund”  shall  be 
construed as “preceding the last day of cap period”.

2. Postponement of Disciplinary Measures against Members 

(a)  The  Exchange  may  postpone  the  measures  to  be  taken  against  members  by 
comprehensively considering the concreteness, validity and feasibility of the plan for 
financial condition improvement of the member. 

(b)  In  the  case  of  postponing  the  disciplinary  measure,  the  Exchange  shall  grant  the 
grace  period  of  the  measure  against  the  member  within  the  limit  of  two  (2)  months 
(hereinafter referred to as “the grace period”), unless there is any particular reason. 

(c)  The  member  concerned  shall  submit  materials  verifying  that  the  causes  for 
unsatisfactory financial conditions were eliminated, by the end of the grace period. 

(d) When it is believed that it is clearly difficult for the member to recover from the 
unsatisfactory  financial  condition  during  the  grace  period,  the  Exchange  may 
terminate the grace period granted and take the disciplinary measure postponed.   

(e) In cases where the member fails to resolve the unsatisfactory financial conditions 
by  the  end  of  the  grace  period,  the  Exchange  shall  take  the  disciplinary  measure 
postponed. 

(f)  When postponing  the disciplinary measure against a  member, the Exchange shall 
notify the member of followings: 

      (i) The grace period; and 
      (ii) The matters mentioned in items d and e. 

3. Granting the Period for Improvement of Financial Conditions 

  (a)  In  the  case  of  taking  disciplinary  measures  of  notice  of  warning  or  attention,  the 
Exchange shall grant a grace period for improvement of financial condition within the 
limit of six (6) months unless there is any particular reason. 

  (b) The  member shall submit  to  the Exchange  materials  verifying  that  the causes for 
unsatisfactory  financial  conditions  were  eliminated,  by  the  end  of  the  period  for 
improvement of financial condition.
